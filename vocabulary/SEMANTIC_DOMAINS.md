# Phi Semantic Domains

This document describes the semantic domain tagging system used in Phi's vocabulary. Rather than forcing words into single categories, Phi uses a flexible tagging system that reflects how words naturally embody multiple aspects of meaning.

## Tag System Overview

Each content word in Phi can be tagged with one or more semantic domains. These tags help users find conceptually related words and understand the interconnected nature of Phi's vocabulary. Tags capture semantic relationships, not grammatical categories (which are already specified in the `pos` field).

## Complete List of Semantic Domain Tags

1. **nature** - natural world, environment, ecology
2. **community** - relationships, social bonds, collective
3. **wisdom** - understanding, awareness, knowledge
4. **creation** - making, building, artistic expression
5. **dialogue** - communication, expression, listening
6. **temporal** - time, change, transformation
7. **aesthetic** - beauty, harmony, sensory appreciation
8. **emotion** - feeling states, emotional experiences
9. **physical** - bodily experiences, tangible qualities
10. **ritual** - ceremony, tradition, sacred practices
11. **cognition** - mental processes, thinking, reasoning
12. **spatial** - location, movement, direction

## Detailed Domain Descriptions

### Nature
This domain encompasses words related to the natural world, including landscapes, weather, plants, animals, and natural phenomena.

**Philosophical Connections:**
- **Solarpunk Values**: Nature words in Phi embody the solarpunk vision of harmonious coexistence with the environment. These terms don't merely label natural objects but express relationships of stewardship, regeneration, and integration with natural systems.
- **Pre-Industrial Wisdom**: This domain preserves knowledge of natural cycles, seasonal patterns, and ecological relationships that industrial societies often overlook. Words here carry embedded understanding of nature's rhythms and our place within them.
- **Art Nouveau Aesthetics**: Natural forms inspire Phi's approach to these words, with sound symbolism often mimicking the organic curves and flowing patterns found in nature itself.

**Worldview**: In Phi, nature is not separate from human experience but interwoven with it. These words reflect a worldview where humans are participants in natural systems rather than external observers or controllers. The vocabulary emphasizes relationships, cycles, and interdependence rather than static categorization.

### Community
This domain encompasses words related to social bonds, kinship, collective activities, and the web of relationships that form human communities.

**Philosophical Connections:**
- **Ubuntu Philosophy**: Community words in Phi embody the Ubuntu principle of "I am because we are." These terms recognize that individual identity emerges through relationships and that personhood is inherently relational rather than individualistic.
- **Peace Linguistics Practices**: This domain supports non-violent communication by providing vocabulary that emphasizes connection, mutual support, and collaborative problem-solving rather than hierarchy or conflict.
- **Linguistic Minimalism**: Rather than elaborate kinship systems or social hierarchies, Phi's community vocabulary focuses on essential relationships and roles that foster genuine connection.

**Worldview**: In Phi, community is the foundation of human experience. These words reflect a worldview where relationships are primary and individual achievement is meaningful only within the context of collective wellbeing. The vocabulary emphasizes reciprocity, interdependence, and the joy found in shared experience.

### Wisdom
This domain encompasses words related to consciousness, understanding, learning, and the cultivation of wisdom through mindful awareness.

**Philosophical Connections:**
- **Secular Buddhist Philosophy**: Wisdom words in Phi reflect Buddhist concepts of awareness, impermanence, and the cultivation of understanding through direct experience. These terms support the practice of mindfulness and the recognition of interconnectedness.
- **Pre-Industrial Wisdom**: This domain preserves ways of knowing that value embodied experience, oral tradition, and wisdom gained through patient observation rather than abstract theorization.
- **Peace Linguistics Practices**: Mental state vocabulary supports emotional literacy and self-awareness, essential tools for peaceful conflict resolution and compassionate communication.

**Worldview**: In Phi, wisdom emerges through mindful engagement with experience rather than accumulation of facts. These words reflect a worldview where understanding is cultivated through presence, reflection, and openness to continuous learning. The vocabulary emphasizes process over product, journey over destination.

### Creation
This domain encompasses words related to making, building, artistic expression, and the transformation of materials into meaningful objects and experiences.

**Philosophical Connections:**
- **Art Nouveau Aesthetics**: Creation words in Phi embody the Art Nouveau principle of beauty in everyday objects and the integration of art with life. These terms celebrate craftsmanship, organic forms, and the unity of function and beauty.
- **Solarpunk Values**: This domain supports regenerative making—creation that enhances rather than depletes, repairs rather than discards, and considers the full lifecycle of what is made.
- **Pre-Industrial Wisdom**: Traditional crafts and making practices are honored here, preserving knowledge of working with natural materials and time-tested techniques that create lasting, repairable objects.

**Worldview**: In Phi, creation is an act of participation in the ongoing making of the world. These words reflect a worldview where making is mindful, beauty is functional, and human creativity works in harmony with natural patterns. The vocabulary emphasizes process, sustainability, and the joy of bringing something meaningful into being.

### Dialogue
This domain encompasses words related to speaking, listening, understanding, and all forms of human expression and dialogue.

**Philosophical Connections:**
- **Peace Linguistics Practices**: Dialogue words in Phi embody principles of non-violent communication, active listening, and expression that seeks understanding rather than dominance. These terms support genuine dialogue across differences.
- **Linguistic Minimalism**: This domain demonstrates that rich communication emerges not from elaborate vocabulary but from mindful use of essential expressions that honor both speaker and listener.
- **Ubuntu Philosophy**: Communication in Phi recognizes that meaning emerges between people, not within individuals. These words support the co-creation of understanding through respectful exchange.

**Worldview**: In Phi, communication is a collaborative art where meaning is woven between participants. These words reflect a worldview where listening is as valued as speaking, silence carries meaning, and true dialogue transforms all participants. The vocabulary emphasizes connection over correctness, understanding over agreement.

### Temporal
This domain encompasses words related to temporal concepts, transformation, cycles, and the dynamic nature of existence.

**Philosophical Connections:**
- **Secular Buddhist Philosophy**: Temporal words in Phi embody the Buddhist understanding of impermanence—that all things arise, change, and pass away. These terms help speakers engage skillfully with the transient nature of experience.
- **Pre-Industrial Wisdom**: This domain preserves cyclical time concepts based on natural rhythms rather than mechanical divisions. Words here reflect seasonal patterns, life stages, and the spiral nature of time in traditional worldviews.
- **Solarpunk Values**: Change vocabulary supports adaptive resilience and the understanding that transformation can be regenerative rather than destructive when approached mindfully.

**Worldview**: In Phi, time is experienced as flow rather than segmentation, and change is the constant that connects all experience. These words reflect a worldview where transformation is natural and necessary, where endings enable beginnings, and where the present moment contains both memory and possibility.

## Usage Guidelines

### Multiple Tags
Most words will have multiple tags reflecting their multifaceted nature:
- A word like "mentor" might be tagged: ["community", "wisdom"]
- "Exchange" could be: ["community", "dialogue", "creation"]
- "Harmony" might include: ["aesthetic", "nature", "community", "wisdom"]

### Tag Selection Principles
1. Choose tags that reflect genuine semantic connections, not forced associations
2. Include all relevant domains without over-tagging
3. Consider how speakers would naturally seek related concepts
4. Remember that tags complement, not replace, the detailed pillar connections in each word's JSON

### Relationship to Pillars
The semantic domain tags operate at a different level than the philosophical pillars:
- **Pillars** explain deep philosophical connections and rationales
- **Tags** provide practical semantic categorization for navigation and discovery

Both systems work together to create a rich, multidimensional understanding of each word's place in Phi's vocabulary.
