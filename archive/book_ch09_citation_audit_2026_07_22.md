## An audit finding corrected by verification

The audit pass reported the Galante et al. 2023 reference title as wrong, preferring the PubMed variant ("Individual participant data systematic review and meta-analysis of randomised controlled trials assessing adult mindfulness-based programmes for mental health promotion in non-clinical settings"). Independent verification against Crossref confirmed the chapter's existing entry character-exact as the published article's registered title ("Systematic review and individual participant data meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials assessing mindfulness-based programs for mental health promotion," Nature Mental Health 1, no. 7: 462-476); the PubMed variant belongs to the deposited manuscript. The entry stands unchanged. This is the third audit in the book's series whose own findings needed correction before application, after the chapter 1-4 audit's Whorf pointer and the chapter 3 audit's Ido percentage: the verify-the-audit-first rule remains in force even when the audit is commissioned rather than supplied.

## Method note

The pre-draft verification (all sources fetched, titles transcribed at entry-writing time, figures pinned with verbatim quotes) left this audit two real errors to find, both in the same place: a list composed for rhythm from the surrounding literature rather than from the cited author's own subjects. The pattern refines the series's running lesson: quotations and numbers survive pre-verification; lists and categories drift when they are assembled from field knowledge rather than from the source. The next chapter's method should treat any enumerated list attached to a citation as a quotation, transcribed from the source's own enumeration.
