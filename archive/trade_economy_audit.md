# The trade and gift economy audit (2026-07-06)

The survey behind the *economy is a gift* ruling in `/canon.md`: the last of the seven system sweeps. No new words were coined; the design was complete, and the gap was the third and final refusal in the measurement family, real everywhere and ruled nowhere.

## What the survey found

**The hands' verbs are complete and mutual by grammar.** `loa` (give), `howela` (receive), `phowe` (share), `pilu` (take), and `wisola` (exchange), whose own entry leans reciprocal: `lo mia wiso wisola.` (we exchange with each other). `loamira` (gift) and `piru` (trader, the goods-mover beside `toloi` the word-mover) stand ready; `seru` (commit) holds the promise as a binding moment. The work field runs `riola` (labor) with its cooperative kin `molawi`, `wenola`, `shorema`, and `muraki` (harvest).

**Worth is carved and honest.** `rolia` (worth) is inherent; `sone` (value) is a verb, so valuing is visibly something someone does to a thing rather than a property the thing has. `simoe` (rich) enriches soil, colors, and flavors; it does not rank persons. Scarcity needs no vocabulary of poverty: a harvest is `phina` (few), a bowl is `whemoa` (empty), and `henoi` (enough) with `lorua` (abundant, plenty to share) carry the sufficiency end.

**The market is a day, not a noun.** The market-day text and the primer's market chapter conduct an entire trade (bowls for eggs, counted exactly) without a market word, a price, or a payment: the scene is a village, the mechanism is `wisola`, and the counting is exact because, as the pamphlet teaches, exactness at the moment of exchange is owed to the other. If a market place ever needs naming, the nouns-describe rule composes it; nothing more is required.

**The refusal was real everywhere and ruled nowhere.** Money, coin, price, pay, buy, sell, debt, owe, wage, wealth, and poor have no words. The measurement essay's own opening examples are prices ("this costs $29.99, that costs $31.49") and follower counts: pricing is precisely the ranking-by-number the essay names as violence. The Prophet transmutation renders reward as `loamira` because "this economy has no wages," and the Tao notes hand a harmful thing back with kindness. The stance saturated the corpus; canon said nothing, and nothing prevented a future "price" from monetizing the language.

## The ruling and the teaching

*The economy is a gift* lives in `/canon.md`, settled 2026-07-06, completing the measurement family: the clock, the ruler, and now the price tag, all refused on the same grounds. The teaching lives in manual ch13 (the words of the exchange), the last panel of the words-of series.
