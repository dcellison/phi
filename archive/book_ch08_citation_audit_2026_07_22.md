# Book chapter 8 citation audit (2026-07-22)

This audit verifies every citation and load-bearing factual claim in `book/08_the_workshop.md` as merged in pull request #497. Two web verification passes re-fetched primary and near-primary sources: archive.org scans of Mackail, Sparling, Cockerell, and the July 1886 Century, the Stirling article's own PDF, museum collection records via the V&A, Met, National Trust, and Society of Antiquaries APIs and pages, the Hostetter PDF, and the live or Wayback-captured policy, court, and report documents behind every contemporary claim. Repository-internal claims were verified directly against canon, the development log, the peace-linguistics audit, the validator configuration, and the colophon. Verdicts below; every correction was applied to the chapter in the same pull request as this record.

## Method note

The two verification passes were instructed to hunt invented specificity, and the three findings of that kind (the Ritchie title, the kernel document title, the Fedora policy title) were all in reference entries whose factual claims were sound. The pattern matches the earlier audits in this series: quotations and numbers survive because they were verified before drafting; titles and provenance details drift when a reference entry is composed from research notes rather than re-fetched. The next chapter's method should re-fetch each reference's own title line at the moment the entry is written.
