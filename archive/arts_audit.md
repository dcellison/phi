# The music and arts audit (2026-07-06)

The survey behind the *a work is its making* ruling in `/canon.md`: the fifth of the seven system sweeps. No new words were coined; the field was designed in depth, carved where it needed carving, and asleep almost in its entirety.

## What the survey found

**Music is a complete little orchestra.** The mel- family runs `meliho` (sing), `melira` (song), `meliphe` (music); `sulai` (musician) plays with the ordinary verb `wile` (`sulai kuma wile.`, the musician plays the drum); the two coined instruments split the craft between them, `kuma` (drum) carrying rhythm and `phui` (flute) carrying melody, each named for its own voice; `lei` (harmonize, intransitive), `shemoli` (harmonize, transitive), and `koru` (harmonious) cover concord; `telui` (rhythm) and `sena` (pattern) carve temporal against spatial repetition; `haoni` (voice) anchors the registry's `haoni welisha` (timbre); `shonuwa` (sound) meets `maeli` (quiet), whose quality noun is silence.

**The making words are carved and the metaphors built in.** The synonym audit carved the maker cluster (`pilewa` transform, `kealo` create, `theko` skilled shaping, `lorima` assembly, `phoa` general doing); `kire` (shape) covers drawing, as the Little Prince transmutation practices; `selomi` (weave) extends to stories by its own entry (`mia nophi selomi.`, I weave a story). `shela` (art) is the category, `mioru` (beauty) what it expresses, `lunai` (vision) what guides it. The joy end is complete: `pharuki` (celebrate), `kela` (rejoice), `holia` (festival, joy against `thorea`'s ritual).

**Nearly everything sleeps.** Corpus counts at survey time: `meliphe` 0, `kuma` 0, `phui` 0, `shela` 0, `mioru` 0, `shonuwa` 0, `pharuki` 0, `sena` 0, `theko` 0, `melira` 1, `rotiku` 1, `lei` 1, `holia` 1. For a language with an Art Nouveau pillar, the arts were the emptiest room in the corpus.

## The gap

No genre vocabulary exists: no poem, no painting, no sculpture, no novel. The absence is right (each would name a container rather than an act), but it was unruled, and genre words are exactly what a helpful future coining would reach for. The same question hovered over further instruments.

## The ruling and the teaching

*A work is its making* lives in `/canon.md`, settled 2026-07-06: art-form and genre roots are refused; a work is named by the event noun of the verb that made it, or by the coined artifacts of voice (`melira`, `meliphe`, `nophi`); a poem is `melira`, because Phi's page is a score for the voice; instruments may still be coined by the full protocol when the household meets one, in the voice-named pattern `kuma` and `phui` set. The teaching lives in manual ch13 (the words of the arts).
