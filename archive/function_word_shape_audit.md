# The function-word shape audit (2026-07-06)

The audit behind the *function-word shape charter* ruling in `/canon.md` — the sequel to the particle commission (PRs #79–#81), extending shape discipline from the 35 particles to all eleven function classes. Every function word was tabulated by syllable shape (FV = fricative-digraph onset, structurally one onset). Result: **113/113 conform to a describable charter; no word moved.**

## Why the shapes carve this way

Three recognition tiers, matching how fast a listener must classify the word: **atoms** (CV monosyllables — particles and base numerals, parsed instantly, which is why digraph onsets are refused there); **relators and frames** (disyllables with a shape split: hiatus (C)V.V marks words that relate — prepositions over nouns, `lao pheo phoe shai` over clauses, pronouns which relate discourse to persons — while plain CV.CV marks words that frame — complementizers, frame conjunctions, the vocative, classifiers); and **content-shaped riders** (quantifiers, discourse markers, interrogatives), which occupy descriptor and adverbial positions where content words live, and so wear content shapes.

The frame-words-stay-two-syllable ruling (settled 2026-07-05, taught in PR #78) is the fixed point the charter is built around: frames never shrink to particles.

## Two observations recorded, not ruled

1. **Discourse markers are 6/7 fricative-initial** (phisu, sheno, shorela, shekoi, shelao, thelao — the breath before reframing); `kewai` (CONTR) is the exception. Ruling a breath-onset signature would mean recoining kewai; left as an observation per the no-statistics-into-rules principle. If kewai is ever recoined for other reasons, a fricative onset would complete the pattern.
2. **Three interrogatives share the relator shape** (kua where, sua who, wia which — pronoun-shaped, and they stand in argument positions), while four are plain disyllables (hina what, misa why, weno when, thela how). The split is suggestive but hina (what, also an argument word) breaks a clean functional carve; not ruled.

## Enforcement

The validator enforces the chartered shape per class on every entry; the protocol's Structural Hierarchy checklist now summarizes the charter and points to canon.
