# The body and health audit (2026-07-06)

The survey behind the *states are worn, sensations are felt* ruling in `/canon.md`: the second of the seven system sweeps, run in the pattern the time, color, and space audits set. No new words were coined; the one gap closed with a ruling over a pattern the corpus already used everywhere without ever being told to.

## What the survey found

**The body is complete.** Thirty body nouns exist, covering the head (`koma menoa mirae shonui nophae phulae lathoe kulai whila nolue`), the trunk and limbs (`pharoe mokura rokai talua pelio manuwe thumai tolae kunoa paloi`), the living interior (`korua lorika tholau hisae kumoe haoni`), the animal extensions (`wapile tulira philea`), and the whole (`weloni`). The registry's Body table already composes thumb, navel, fur, and tears. The apparent gaps dissolve through the standing rules: a breath is `whunei`'s event noun (ruled), a cut is `kati`'s, hunger and thirst are the quality nouns of `kumoli` and `kishu`.

**The health field is designed and carved.** `mokela` (sick), `towe` (well), `wolu` (healthy), `sunai` (health) carve one another as state, condition, and bearer; the medicine family runs `nepha` (medicine), `heloa` (remedy), `thelai` (balm), `wenu` (tonic), with `theala` (heal) and `talome` (restore) as its verbs and `theama`/`numelo` (care, nurture) beside them. `kipona` (pain) is a noun whose entry already carries the located pattern and the healer's question.

**Nearly all of it is corpus-dead.** Counts at survey time: `kumoli` 0, `kishu` 0, `wolu` 0, `sunai` 0, `nepha` 0, `weloni` 0, `koma` 0, `manuwe` 0, `paloi` 0, `mokela` 1, `kipona` 2. Only `towe` (32, through the Metta Sutta's `towe nai`) and `korua` (14, through its compounds) live. The same sleep as time, color, and space.

**One pattern was attested everywhere and ruled nowhere.** `phaelo` (feel) appears in 35 corpus files; ch25's journal practice teaches "`phaelo` with whatever is true: `shea`, `nuhe`, `siora`, `korua thero`" as daily practice. But no canon bullet and no grammar section ever stated the construction, its relation to adjective states with `nai`, or how a body part locates a feeling. The emotions sweep (queue C6) flagged the same gap from its side; it is ruled here because pain needs it, and the emotions sweep inherits it.

## The gap

How Phi says "I am hungry," "I feel pain," "my back hurts," and "where does it hurt" was answerable only by reading individual lexicon entries. The grammar-level fact (adjective states predicate with `nai`; noun sensations are objects of `phaelo`; `mua` plus a body part locates; the identity shortcut `mia kipona nai` is refused) was implicit in word classes and entry examples, one silent fork away from a corpus that says "I am pain."

## The ruling and the teaching

*States are worn, sensations are felt* lives in `/canon.md`, settled 2026-07-06. The teaching lives in manual ch13 (the words of the body), which wakes the body nouns, the caring questions the entries already carried, the healer's exchange, the medicine family, and the health restatement of the no-generic-bad refusal: a person is `mokela` or `towe`, never bad, and never their pain.
