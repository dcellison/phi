# The time system audit (2026-07-06)

The survey behind the four time rulings in `/canon.md` — Phi's handling of time taken as a whole for the first time: what the core already did well, what the lexicon held but never taught, and the four gaps that needed ruling. No new words were coined; every gap closed with a ruling over what already existed.

## The core is sound (untouched)

Tense and aspect needed nothing. Manual ch15 teaches `to`/`so` (past/future, unmarked present) and the five aspects `si ki te pa ro`, with the ten-combination table in §5. Canon already carries the load-bearing rulings: main-clause tense is obligatory, embedded clauses mark tense relative to the matrix event, `ki` completes at the tense's reference time, `si` and `ro` do not overlap, and each Slot 1 rank admits one particle per clause. This commission touches none of it.

## A vocabulary asleep in the lexicon

Thirty-one time words existed with near-zero corpus presence and no teaching in the manual, primer, or pamphlets:

- **The day-cycle**: `horathe` (dawn), `kelua` (morning), `thaeso` (midday), `lorui` (afternoon), `howai` (evening), `norawhi` (dusk), `shero` (night), `phoemu` (midnight) — a complete eight-point clock no learner had ever been shown.
- **The calendar**: `philo` (day), `phaeno` (week), `lunoa` (month), `meluna` (season), `torua` (year), `noruwa` (generation).
- **The cyclical words**: `nuri` (cycle), `telui` (rhythm), `kaero` (phase), `mosha` (period), `nuwoe` (stage).
- **Locating and spanning**: `luera` (past), `nomi` (present), `wireo` (future), `nosa` (now), `thimu` (time), `shemu` (moment), `tiroe` (instant), `thorui` (beginning), `kesho` (middle), `lumae` (end), `tholua` (ancient), `phaero` (transit).

Corpus presence before this commission: the honest journal's `sui philo` (evidentiality pamphlet), market day's `sui philo`, and the compound registry's yesterday/today/tomorrow rows. The prepositions `sui` (DUR) and `tei` (UNTIL) were live in the corpus; the day-cycle nouns were not.

## The four gaps

1. **No clock time.** Hour, minute, second, and clock have no words. `documents/psychological_violence_of_measurement.md` argues the refusal at length, but canon never ruled it — nothing prevented a future coining from quietly erasing the design.
2. **Age was unsayable.** No pattern existed for "I am N years old."
3. **Temporal distance was unruled.** "Two days ago" and "in three days" had no documented pattern; ch20 documents `pheo`/`phoe` as clause subordinators only — though `tei` already takes plain NPs (`tei kelua`) and the shape charter classes `pheo phoe` as relators, prepositions over clauses.
4. **Frequency compounds were item-by-item.** `sheloi shemu` (often), `theula thimu` (always), and `mawha thimu` (never) sat in the registry with no statement that the pattern is productive.

## The rulings

All four live in `/canon.md`, settled 2026-07-06: **the natural clock** (the eight day-cycle nouns are Phi's time-telling, located with `sui`/`tei`/`mua`; hour, minute, second, and clock-time refused by design), **age** (`mia [numeral] torua phelu` — predicative possession per the clause-initial-pronouns ruling), **temporal distance** (`pheo`/`phoe` stand over counted time noun phrases as well as clauses), and **frequency composes** (quantifier + `shemu`/`thimu` is productive and preferred to coining; `phina shemu` and `soli shemu` join the registry).
