# The synonym-carve audit (2026-07-06)

The final sweep of the consistency program: the semantic mirror of the minimal-pair opposition pass. Where that pass separated words too close in *form*, this one audited words too close in *meaning* — every near-synonym cluster in the content lexicon — asking whether each member's entry carves its own territory the way canon's `si`/`ro` ruling carved aspect. A speaker choosing between calm-words or path-words should find the answer in the entries.

## Method

Candidate clusters from three angles: glosses sharing a stem (joy/joyful), mutual citation in entry notes (the Related networks), and known semantic fields. 36 clusters (79 words) were read closely. A cluster passes when every member's entry states what distinguishes it from its neighbors.

## Findings

- **23 clusters were already fully carved** — some in phrasings a keyword scan undercounts ("whaline is the expression, woraka the feeling"; "attributed versus inherent"; "between 'sima' and 'suro' in thickness"; fog and mist distinguished by density and lightness). The mycelial design is working: most mutual citations are opposites and field-mates, not redundancies, and the carving habit is well established.
- **13 entries carried one-sided or missing carves, completed in this sweep**: loshi, siora, pilewa, kealo, phaelu (had no relations at all), theloa, molawi, ruela, menai, nelu, rohi, soliru, wiroa.
- **One stale POS reference**: loshi's note still called it the "adjective counterpart of 'siora' (joy)" — true until the qualities-do-not-act ruling made siora an adjective too (PR #85). Recarved rather than retired: `siora` names felt joy (the experiencer's own upwelling); `loshi` names the joy-giving character of things — days, festivals, news that shine. The closest call in the lexicon; if the pair ever feels thin in use, retirement of loshi is the fallback.
- **Zero retirements needed.** Every cluster resolved by carving.

## Enforcement

Not mechanically enforced: carving is prose judgment, and a keyword lint would misread the good phrasings above. The Word Creation Protocol already requires semantic-family placement for new coinings, and the citation-integrity check (PR #88) keeps the cross-references honest. This audit is the baseline; repeat it when the lexicon grows substantially.
