## Sound Families by Philosophical Pillar

### 1. Solarpunk Sounds (Bright, Open, Energetic)
**Core sounds**: `a`, `l`, `s`, `ph`
**Vowel pairs**: `au`, `ai`
- **a** - Openness, abundance, possibility
- **l** - Flow, connection, light
- **s** - Sun, sustaining energy
- **ph** - Transformation, future-thinking
- **Examples**: `phalau` (solar abundance), `saila` (sustainable aspiration), `phala` (transformative energy)

### 2. Buddhist/Mindfulness Sounds (Centered, Aware)
**Core sounds**: `n`, `m`, `o`, `th`
**Vowel pairs**: `oa`, `ou`
- **n** - Grounding, presence, now
- **m** - Mindfulness, inner quiet
- **o** - Wholeness, contemplation
- **th** - Thought, awareness, careful consideration
- **Examples**: `nouma` (deep meditation), `thoala` (wisdom-path), `noma` (mindful presence)

### 3. Art Nouveau Sounds (Flowing, Beautiful)

- **i** - Delicate beauty, refinement, precision
- **sh** - Gentle aesthetics, soft beauty
- **Examples**: `weira` (flowing grace), `shiuna` (organic unity), `shiro` (aesthetic form)

### 4. Peace Linguistics Sounds (Soft, Connecting)

### 5. Pre-industrial Wisdom Sounds (Earthy, Cyclical)
**Core sounds**: `k`, `u`, `t`
**Vowel pairs**: `ue`, `io`
- **k** - Stone, ancient knowledge, permanence

- **t** - Cycles, returning patterns, time
- **Examples**: `kuelo` (returning wisdom), `tukio` (earth-sky cycle), `kuta` (ancient foundation)

## Compositional Principles

### 1. Semantic Families Share Sound Patterns
Words within conceptual families maintain consistent initial sounds:

**Care/Nurture Family** (th- pattern):
- `thea` - care/nurture
- `theno` - gentle
- `theola` - wisdom (care-path)
- `thesoma` - healing (care-body)

**Sharing/Community Family** (sh- pattern):
- `sha` - shared/together
- `shele` - give/receive
- `shiro` - forest (shared growth)
- `shila` - togetherness

**Flow/Movement Family** (l- pattern):
- `lisa` - flow
- `lisu` - water (flowing substance)
- `liso` - river (flowing path)

### 2. Sound Combinations Reveal Relationships
- Flowing liquid + open vowel = connection concepts
- Nasal + closed vowel = grounding concepts
- Fricative + high vowel = abstract/spiritual concepts
- Vowel pair + resonant = transformative concepts

### 3. Morphological Transparency
Building blocks maintain their sound identity in compounds:
- If `no` = mind, it retains this in `nothela` (mind-path/reason)
- If `lo` = toward, it retains this in `lothea` (toward-care/love)
- If `weo` = we/community, it retains this in `weola` (community-being)

## Guidelines for New Word Creation

1. **Identify the concept's primary philosophical alignment** (which pillar?)
2. **Choose appropriate initial consonant(s)** from that pillar's sound family
3. **Select vowels** that match the concept's energetic quality
4. **Add vowel pairs** for dimensional meaning (expansion, convergence, etc.)
5. **Ensure morphological transparency** in compounds
6. **Test the word** in the semantic family to ensure coherent relationships
