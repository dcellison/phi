# The question system audit (2026-07-06)

The survey behind the *gap-word stands where the answer will* ruling in `/canon.md`: the seventh and final system sweep. One ruling, one gloss correction, no coins.

## What the survey found

**The grammar was taught twice and ruled nowhere.** Content questions place the interrogative exactly where the answer's phrase will stand, with no `wa`; the manual teaches it (ch12 §5, "the interrogative word sits exactly where its answer would sit") and the spoken-punctuation pamphlet drills it ("the gap-word sits where the answer will; no `wa`"), and the corpus practices it from the primer's `thia hina nila.` to the healer's `thia kua kipona phaelo.` and embedded `lo mia shia kua nai ma sano`. Canon carried only the `wela`/`welo` complementizer bullet. The load-bearing in-situ principle had the phaelo problem: attested everywhere, settled nowhere.

**The manual counted six askers; the lexicon holds seven.** ch12 §5's list (`sua hina weno kua misa thela`) omits `wia`, the quantity asker, which the ternary pamphlet teaches and the market text uses (`wia themo noru.`, how many bowls?). The words-of sections created new askable systems (color, age, time) with no home showing how to ask about them.

**`wia` wore the wrong gloss.** Its gloss read "which" while its description, notes, and every use define it as the quantity interrogative ("how many people?", "how much water?"). English "which" means selection, which in Phi is `hina` before a noun (what-kind); a learner following the gloss would misuse the word. Corrected in this sweep: gloss "how many", entry file renamed to match, the four documentation lines updated, and two entry citations plus one pre-existing mis-cased gloss line (`many friend` for MANY) caught by the validator's citation net during the change.

**The shape observation stays an observation.** The function-word shape audit noted that three askers wear the relator shape (`kua sua wia`) and four are plain disyllables, with `hina` breaking any clean functional carve. Nothing rides on it; it remains recorded, not ruled.

## The ruling and the teaching

*The gap-word stands where the answer will* lives in `/canon.md`, settled 2026-07-06: in-situ interrogatives, `wa` for yes/no only and never beside a gap-word, the seven askers named with `wia` as quantity, and selection covered by `hina` before a noun. The teaching updates ch12 §5: seven askers, and the questions the new systems invite, from `ha hina welisha nai.` (what color is this?) to `thia wia torua phelu.` (how old are you?).
