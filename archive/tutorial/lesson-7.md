## Lesson 7: The Power of Adjectives

In Phi, there is no separate category for adverbs (words like "quickly" or "softly"). Instead, the language uses adjectives in a flexible way to modify both nouns and verbs. The function of an adjective is determined entirely by its position in the sentence.

This is a core principle of Phi's elegance: one word class does the work of two.

### Modifying Nouns

As we've seen in previous lessons, an adjective comes **before the noun** it describes. This follows the universal head-final rule.

Let's learn a new adjective and a new noun:
*   `lopia`: beautiful (this is an alternative to `wela`, focusing more on aesthetic beauty)
*   `shelu`: a book / a text

To say "a beautiful book," the adjective comes first:

*   **Phi:** `lopia shelu`
*   **Gloss:** `beautiful book`
*   **Translation:** a beautiful book

Let's put this in a full sentence:

*   **Phi:** `mia lopia shelu shipe.`
*   **Gloss:** `1SG beautiful book see.`
*   **Translation:** I see a beautiful book.

### Modifying Verbs

This is where the flexibility comes in. To use an adjective as an adverb, you simply place it **before the verb** it describes.

Let's use our word `nosa` (mindful).

To say "The community mindfully creates," we place `nosa` right before the verb `kealo` (to create).

*   **Phi:** `sila nosa kealo.`
*   **Gloss:** `community mindful create.`
*   **Translation:** The community creates mindfully.

Here, `nosa` is not describing the community; it is describing *how* the community creates. Its position before the verb gives it an adverbial function.

### Two Functions, One Word

Let's look at one word in both positions to make the distinction crystal clear. We'll use the adjective `philo` (calm).

**Modifying a noun:**
*   `philo liso`: the calm water

**Modifying a verb:**
*   `mia philo nima`: I calmly sleep / I sleep calmly.

The same word performs both functions based on its placement. This is a key part of what makes Phi so consistent and elegant.

### Lesson 7 Summary

*   Phi uses a single class of "descriptor" words for both adjectives and adverbs.
*   To modify a **noun**, place the adjective **before the noun**.
*   To modify a **verb**, place the adjective **before the verb**.

You can now add a rich new layer of descriptive detail to every part of your sentences. In the next lesson, we will cover the final types of modifiers: possession and comparison.
