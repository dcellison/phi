# Tier 2 Essential Roots for Phi

## Overview
These essential roots fill the practical gaps between NSM primes and everyday communication needs. They are selected to be culturally universal, philosophically aligned with Phi's Five Pillars, and productive for compositional vocabulary building. Each category represents universal human experiences across all cultures.

## Natural Elements & Phenomena
Universal experiences of the physical world:
- **Water** - fundamental to life
- **Fire/Heat** - transformation, energy
- **Earth/Soil** - grounding, foundation
- **Air/Wind** - breath, movement
- **Sun** - giver of light/life
- **Moon** - cycles, rhythm
- **Star** - navigation, wonder
- **Rain** - nourishment, renewal
- **Stone** - permanence, building
- **Seed** - potential, beginning

## Life & Growth
Cross-cultural biological universals:
- **Root** - foundation, connection
- **Bloom/Flower** - flourishing, beauty
- **Fruit** - result, nourishment
- **Grow** - expansion, development
- **Flow** - movement, continuity
- **Rest** - restoration, pause
- **Wake** - awareness, beginning
- **Breathe** - life force, rhythm
- **Heart** (as rhythm/center, not organ)
- **Birth** - emergence, beginning

## Consciousness & Awareness
Supporting Buddhist/mindfulness concepts:
- **Mind** - awareness, thought-space
- **Spirit/Essence** - animating principle
- **Dream** - other consciousness
- **Peace** - harmony, stillness
- **Joy** - positive life energy
- **Compassion** - caring awareness
- **Wisdom** - deep knowing
- **Attention** - focused awareness
- **Balance** - equilibrium
- **Presence** - being here/now

## Relationships & Connection
Universal social concepts without hierarchy:
- **Parent** - one who nurtures
- **Child** - one who grows
- **Elder** - one with experience
- **Friend** - chosen connection
- **Community** - shared being
- **Give/Receive** - reciprocal flow
- **Share** - communal having
- **Care** - nurturing attention
- **Teach/Learn** - knowledge sharing
- **Gather** - coming together

## Action & Creation
Aligned with Solarpunk values:
- **Make/Create** - bringing into being
- **Build** - collaborative construction
- **Plant** - placing seeds
- **Harvest** - gathering results
- **Mend** - restore wholeness
- **Weave** - connecting threads
- **Sing** - joyful expression
- **Dance** - embodied rhythm
- **Story** - meaning-making
- **Journey** - purposeful movement

## Space & Place
Universal orientations:
- **Home** - belonging place
- **Path** - way of movement
- **Center** - gathering point
- **Edge** - transition space
- **Open** - unrestricted space
- **Shelter** - protective space
- **Sacred** - special/set-apart space
- **Wild** - untamed space
- **Garden** - cultivated space
- **Circle** - inclusive shape

## Time & Rhythm
Beyond linear time:
- **Cycle** - returning pattern
- **Season** - natural division
- **Dawn** - beginning light
- **Dusk** - ending light
- **Full** - completeness
- **Empty** - openness
- **Young** - early stage
- **Old** - late stage
- **Always** - continuous
- **Rhythm** - patterned time

## Quality & State
Value-neutral descriptors:
- **Warm/Cool** - temperature
- **Soft/Firm** - texture
- **Clear/Clouded** - transparency
- **Whole/Broken** - integrity
- **Ripe/Unripe** - readiness
- **Wild/Tame** - cultivation state
- **Awake/Asleep** - consciousness state
- **Strong/Gentle** - force quality
- **Fast/Slow** - movement quality
- **Full/Hungry** - satiation state

## Implementation Principles

1. **Cultural Universality**: Each root represents experiences common to all human cultures
2. **Philosophical Alignment**: Roots support rather than contradict Phi's Five Pillars
3. **Compositional Productivity**: Each root can combine with others to create meaningful compounds
4. **Semantic Transparency**: The meaning of compounds built from these roots should be intuitive
5. **Sound-Meaning Correspondence**: Each root needs careful phonological assignment based on its semantic qualities

## Notes on Excluded Concepts

Notably absent are:
- Technology-specific terms (these can be built compositionally or borrowed as needed)
- Culture-specific artifacts or practices
- Hierarchical social roles
- Violence-oriented concepts
- Possession-focused terms

These can be expressed compositionally when needed, but are not given root status.
