# Book chapter 12 citation audit (2026-07-22)

This audit verifies every citation and load-bearing claim in `book/12_a_language_for_the_commons.md` as merged in pull request #505. An independent verification pass read the finished chapter cold and checked its final wording, sentence by sentence, against the primary sources: the manifesto's live page and its October 2019 archive capture, Flynn's Hieroglyph essay, the 2008 coinage post, the 2014 Tumblr post, both Dear Alice uploads with their metadata, the Reactor announcement, the Hugo Awards record, the magazine and encyclopedia pages, the Wikisource text of Zamenhof's Cambridge speech and the Boulogne declaration, the Toki Pona pages, and Crossref for the three academic references. The date-stamped community counts were verified against the stored July 22 retrieval files, the practice the chapter 10 audit's method note called for. Repository-internal quotations and claims (the proposition 21 excerpt, the shelf's structural claims, the LICENSE exception, the registry row, the catalogue's first-text epithet) were verified byte-exact directly, and one claim was checked against git history. Every correction was applied to the chapter in the same pull request as this record.

## Method note

Two designed defenses each caught their intended failure class this round. The audit's field-by-field reference check caught a title back-formed from a URL slug, the exact invented-title drift the series' first lesson names, surviving here because the pre-draft agent verified the quote on the page but not the page's title line. And the repository-internal pass caught a false history claim ("has always opened") by checking git for any sentence that asserts how long something has been true, a check worth keeping: a warm image can be current fact and recent arrival at once. The chapter 10 lesson also paid its way: because the fetched sources stayed on disk, the date-stamped community counts were verified against the exact files behind the chapter's retrieval-date claims rather than against pages that had already moved on.
