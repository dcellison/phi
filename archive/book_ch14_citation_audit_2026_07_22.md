## Method note

The one substantive defect was a list conflation inside a correctly quoted source, the same failure class as chapter 9's rebuilt subject list: enumerations drift when composed from memory of a paper rather than transcribed from its own sentence, even when the surrounding quotes are character-exact. The pre-draft verification had delivered both of the paper's lists verbatim and the draft still blended them, which sharpens the lesson: when a chapter sentence attributes a list to a source, the list is a quotation whether or not it wears quotation marks, and it gets checked against the source's own enumeration at drafting time, not just at audit time. The fence itself held: every claim inside it stands in the sources' own words, and the chapter's refusal to borrow the findings survives contact with what the sources actually say.
