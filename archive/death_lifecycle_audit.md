# The death and life-cycle audit (2026-07-06)

The survey behind the *death is named plainly* and *rites are described, never prescribed* rulings in `/canon.md`: the third of the seven system sweeps. No new words were coined and no carve lines were needed; the vocabulary was complete and already carved, and the gaps were rulings and teaching alone.

## What the survey found

**The life arc is designed end to end.** The li- family carries living (`lima` alive, `liona` live, `lioru` life); the age arc is a built three-step (`limu` young, `seroli` mature, `serao` old), with `wheo` (elder) and `phewo` (sage) as its honored roles and `tholua` (ancient) for things; `thowia` (born) and `lumeo` (die) bound it, each licensing its event noun by rule. `lumeo`'s own entry already uses death as a subject: `lumeo wei theula lioru shua.` (death comes to all life).

**Grief is designed and carved.** `nuhe` (sadness), `nuhemi` (grieve, with the mourned one marked by `wei`: `mia wei ni melu nuhemi`), `holume` (loss, already paired with `phaelo` and with `phowe`: `lo mia holume phowe.`, we share the loss), `nuhewa` (weep), and the registry's `mirae phialu` (tears). The states-are-worn-sensations-are-felt ruling covers all of it without addition. `norea` (soul) and `phiora` (spirit) already carve each other: spirit animates, soul identifies.

**Nearly all of it is corpus-dead.** Counts at survey time: `lumeo` 1, `nuhemi` 2, `holume` 0, `norea` 1, `phiora` 0, `liona` 2, `lioru` 4. The deepest subject in the language, essentially unwritten.

**The apparent gaps dissolve by rule.** Death and birth are event nouns; the dead one is `rena to lumeo` (the one who died); memory is `halemu`'s event noun; grief the noun is `nuhe` or `nuhemi`'s event noun; a grave, a funeral, a ghost have no words, and the Prophet's transmutation notes already state the stance: Phi keeps rites unnamed so every speaker may bring their own. But that stance lived in a pamphlet's notes, not in canon, and nothing prevented a future "funeral" coining. Likewise nothing stated that `lumeo` would never grow a softened synonym.

## The gaps

1. **No euphemism policy.** Every natural language grows a fog bank around death (pass away, depart, be lost). Phi's honesty register implies the refusal; canon never said it.
2. **The rites stance unruled.** Generic `thorea` (ceremony) exists; specific rite vocabulary does not, deliberately, per a note in a text's margin rather than a ruling.
3. **Nothing taught.** No manual section on the life arc, on speaking of death, or on grief.

## The rulings and the teaching

*Death is named plainly* and *rites are described, never prescribed* live in `/canon.md`, settled 2026-07-06. The teaching lives in manual ch15 (the turning of a life), beside the words of time. The Heart Sutra transmutation (`pamphlets/heart_sutra.md`) accompanies the sweep as its living text: the emptiness teaching built from `whemoa`'s quality noun, `kire`'s event noun, the `mu` litany the Tao text licensed, and the same optative engine as the Metta Sutta.
