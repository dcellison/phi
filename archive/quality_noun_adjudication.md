# The adjective+noun adjudication (2026-07-06)

The word-by-word audit behind the *Qualities and their names* ruling in `/canon.md`. After the event-noun rule (verb→noun, one way) and the things-do-not-verb ruling closed the verb/noun axis, 24 content entries still listed adjective and noun together, each as a per-word fact with no covering rule. This pass pruned every one to a single class and put two rule-level bridges in their place: every adjective licenses its quality noun with no change of form (the quality-noun rule, mirroring the event-noun rule), and every noun modifies by position when it stands before another noun (already taught for possession in manual ch13; kind, direction, and association are readings of the same slot).

The adjudication principle: the primary class is the class of what the word names first — which in every case matched the existing gloss, so no gloss changed and no corpus gloss line moved. Predication was never at stake: `nai` predicates nouns and adjectives alike (`su sila shea nai`, may the community be at peace).
