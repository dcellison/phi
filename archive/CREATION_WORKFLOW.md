# Phi Word Generation Workflow

This document outlines the official 4-step creative process for generating new Phi vocabulary. This workflow must be followed for every new word to ensure all creations are phonetically diverse, philosophically grounded, and internally consistent with the language's core principles.

### 1. The "Phonetic Mood Board"

Before creating a word, first analyze the English concept and its associated pillars to define a "phonetic feel" or "mood."

-   Pre-select a palette of appropriate sounds (vowels, consonants, and fricatives) that sonically embody the concept's tempered meaning.
-   Consciously avoid sounds that would clash with the concept's philosophical intent (e.g., avoid hard stops for gentle concepts).

### 2. The "Structural Blueprint"

Decide on a target word structure, focusing on variety and avoiding recent patterns.

-   Choose a syllable count (typically two or three).
-   Define the desired features, ensuring that fricative digraphs and/or vowel pairs are used with equal or greater frequency than simple CV syllables. Examples: `FV.V`, `CV.CV.V`, `FV.CV`.

### 3. The "Creative Assembly"

Combine the sounds from the "mood board" with the chosen "blueprint" to generate a list of 2-3 strong candidate words that are phonetically pleasing and conceptually sound.

### 4. The "Anti-Collision Check"

This is the final and most critical step.

-   Maintain a running list of the last 5-10 approved words.
-   Check every new candidate against this list for phonetic similarity.
-   Immediately discard any candidate that is an anagram, a minimal pair (e.g., `sheo`/`shau`), or is otherwise phonetically confusable with a recent word.
-   Select the strongest, most distinct candidate for the final proposal.
