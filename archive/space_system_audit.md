# The space and direction audit (2026-07-06)

The survey behind the *natural ruler* and *sides come from a body* rulings in `/canon.md`: Phi's spatial system examined whole for the first time, in the pattern the time and color audits set. No new words were coined; the gaps closed with two rulings, one missing reference document, and a teaching section.

## What the survey found

**The design is complete; the corpus never moved in.** Sixteen of the twenty-seven prepositions are spatial (place: `mua nia leo phou noe neo sio thei thoa roa`; motion: `kau lue wea phei muo mue thue`; proximity: `pai woe`), all relator-shaped per the charter, all modifier-first. Seven of them had zero corpus presence outside the lexicon: `noe` (behind), `sio` (beside), `thei` (between), `roa` (around), `mue` (out of), `phei` (away), `woe` (far). No manual section taught the spatial system, and `documents/grammar/` had references for particles, complementizers, and numerals but none for prepositions.

**The compass was carved and asleep.** `nitho` (north) and `ronua` (south) are atomic and deliberately value-neutral (their entries refuse up-ness and heat); east and west are the registry compounds `sorae thorui` and `sorae lumae`, sun-anchored because no sun-anchor is hemisphere-neutral for north and south. Corpus presence: `nitho` three files, `ronua` effectively none. All design, no teaching.

**The measure field has a deliberate shape, stated nowhere.** The dimension nouns exist (`raeli` height, `lonai` width, `nusho` depth, with adjectives `raelu`, `losha`, `nulo`), `masue` (measure) is a verb whose own entry gauges cloth against an arm (`shia sheo pelio wetha masue.`) and cites the measurement essay, and the comparative pattern is attested in corpus (`thepalu sheo shelira mo phelora nai.`). But there are no unit words at all: no meter, no mile, no kilogram, no liter, and no word for length as an abstract. The refusal was real and consistent, exactly as clock time's was, and just as unruled: nothing prevented a future "kilometer" from erasing the design.

**Sides had no frame of reference.** `lawe` (left) and `kuri` (right) are carved as a deliberately disjoint pair and their entries show `mua lawe` (on the left), but nothing said whose left. An unanchored absolute left is a real ambiguity and a small door to the kind of view-from-nowhere abstraction Phi avoids.

**Already sound, untouched**: the motion rulings (`kau` allative, `lue` ablative, `wea` toward without arrival, `mua` never a motion endpoint, `kamo` takes `mua`), adjunct order (S PP O V), oblique relative gaps, predicative `ha`/`ra` (taught in ch14 §5), and the east/west registry rows.

## The gaps

1. **The natural ruler unruled.** Unit words absent by design, with the essay's reasoning, but canon silent; the positive pattern (comparison, body standards, journey time, dimension nouns) lived only in one entry's example.
2. **Sides unanchored.** Whose left is `lawe ruela`?
3. **No preposition reference.** The one function-class inventory missing from `documents/grammar/`.
4. **Nothing taught.** No manual home for compass, sides, spatial relators, or the ruler refusal; seven relators corpus-dead.

## The rulings and the artifacts

*The natural ruler* and *sides come from a body* live in `/canon.md`, settled 2026-07-06. The inventory now lives in `documents/grammar/preposition_reference.md`. The teaching lives in manual ch13 (the words of place), which exercises every sleeping relator. No compounds were added: `wi philo thalo` (a two-day walk) is the nouns-describe rule doing ordinary work, not an idiom.
