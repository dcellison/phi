# 10 · moru

*Two more aspects, and they are a pair:* **ki** *closes a doing (complete, done, the tools set down), and* **te** *marks a doing that ceases. One is an arrival, the other a stopping. The storm from chapter seven left some work behind.*

## I

> **pheralu to lepa.**
> **moru to lepa.**
> **phao moru nila.**
> **wheo moru nila.**

| new word | say it | it means |
|---|---|---|
| moru | mo · ru | wall |

*The rain fell, and part of the garden wall fell with it. The parent looks at the wall. The elder looks at the wall. In every language, this is how repairs begin.*

## II

> **phao moru si shiroka.**
> **melu shua. melu moru si shiroka.**
> **lopia lo kerou kolua.**
> **lohau lo miona si nila.**
> **phao moru ki shiroka.**

| new word | say it | it means |
|---|---|---|
| shiroka | shi · ro · ka | repair |
| kerou | ke · ro · u | stone |
| ki | ki | (before the verb: complete, done) |

*Read the first and last lines against each other:* si shiroka, *the repairing still open;* ki shiroka, *the repairing closed. Same wall, same verb: the particle alone moves the work from underway to done. Between them: a friend who shows up, a child who carries stones, and a dog who supervises.*

## III

> **pheralu lepa.**
> **pheralu te lepa.**
> **howeli te shua.**
> **sorae phelo loa.**

| new word | say it | it means |
|---|---|---|
| te | te | (before the verb: it ceases) |

*Rain falls. Rain stops falling. te is the gentlest stop in the language: not finished like* ki, *just no longer happening. The wind lets go too, and the sun takes over the sky.*

## IV

> **phao therilu.**
> **melu therilu.**
> **wheo therilu.**
> **lohau therilu.**
> **misheko rihe.**
> **misheko mua moru thalo.**

| new word | say it | it means |
|---|---|---|
| therilu | the · ri · lu | rest |

*Work complete, everyone rests, and the cat rises, crosses the yard, and walks along the top of the repaired wall, testing it. The wall holds. It has passed the only inspection that matters.*

---

*Four aspects now:* si *holds a doing open,* ki *closes it,* te *lets it cease, and the unmarked verb simply does. Say the wall's two sentences once more and feel the work finish in your mouth.*
