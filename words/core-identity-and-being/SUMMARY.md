## Phonological Patterns

### Two-Syllable Words (112)
The majority of words use simple CV.V or CV.CV patterns, with frequent use of:
- Vowel hiatus: tio, lio, rio, kuo, etc.
- Fricative digraphs: shie, whae, phue, thie, etc.
- Simple CV.CV: mira, ponu, laku, nomi, etc.

### Three-Syllable Words (37)
Complex concepts often use three syllables:
- phelano (consciousness)
- wholenu (commitment)
- werumi (awakening)
- kalumi (equanimity)

## Key Semantic Relationships

1. **Body-Spirit Pairs**: Physical and spiritual aspects often have related words
2. **State-Process Pairs**: Static states paired with dynamic processes
3. **Inner-Outer Pairs**: Internal qualities with external expressions
4. **Individual-Collective**: Personal identity extending to group identity

## Usage Notes

This category provides the essential vocabulary for:
- Self-reference and personal identity
- Describing physical and mental states
- Expressing character and qualities
- Discussing spiritual and philosophical concepts
- Building complex ideas about consciousness and being

The words in this category form the foundation for more complex philosophical and psychological discussions in Phi.
