# Community & Relationships Category Summary

## Phonological Features Used

All words contain at least one of:
- **Fricative digraphs** (ph, th, sh, wh)
- **Vowel hiatus** (two vowels in sequence)
- **Both features combined**

## Philosophical Pillars Distribution

The words in this category draw from all five pillars, with emphasis on:
- **Peace-linguistics**: Communication, conflict resolution, dialogue
- **Buddhist**: Compassion, interdependence, mindfulness in relationships
- **Pre-industrial**: Traditional social structures, ceremonies, elders
- **Solarpunk**: Collaborative networks, mutual aid, collective action
- **Art-nouveau**: Beauty in human connection, love, celebration

## Usage Notes

These words form the foundation for expressing all types of human relationships and social interactions in Phi. They emphasize:
- Connection over isolation
- Mutual support over competition
- Process over hierarchy
- Collective wisdom over individual knowledge
- Healing over harm
