# Fundamental Actions and Movement Category Summary

**Total words created: 125**
- Two-syllable words: 83
- Three-syllable words: 42
- Ratio: Approximately 2:1 (two-syllable to three-syllable)

## Phonological Patterns Used

### Vowel Hiatus Patterns (V.V)
- a.e: lae (flow), pae (fall), rae (rise)
- a.i: shai (dash), phai (bound), whai (bolt), thai (dodge)
- a.o: whao (soar)
- a.u: tau (twist), sau (glide), wau (bend), pau (bounce)
- e.a: phea (prance), thea* (balance/healer - already exists)
- e.i: thei (creep), shei (inch)
- e.o: wheo (totter)
- e.u: teu (drift), seu (sway), rou (surge)
- i.a: hia (pivot), ria (ripple)
- i.e: rie (spin), sie (slide), tie (tilt), wie (weave), whie (whirl)
- i.o: wio (swirl)
- i.u: tiu (flutter)
- o.a: poa (propel), thoa (stagger), shoa (lunge), phoa (crouch)
- u.a: *none created*
- u.e: *none created*
- u.i: nui (dip), kui (dart), pui (lurch), hui (bob), mui (skim), rui (veer), wui (wade), sui (swerve), tui (trudge), lui (flit), shui (coil), phoi (vault), whui (hover), thui (plunge), phui (zip)
- u.o: puo (plod), muo (coast), kuo (scurry), shuo (shuffle), nuo (retreat), huo (sidestep), suo (embark)

### Fricative Digraph Patterns
- ph: phea (prance), phoa (crouch), phoi (vault), phui (zip), phai (bound), phelora (reverberate), pheoli (amble)
- th: thei (creep), thai (dodge), thui (plunge), thoa (stagger), thepura (meander), thokali (scamper), nethola (circulate)
- sh: shei (inch), shai (dash), shui (coil), shuo (shuffle), shoa (lunge), sholae (coordinate), shokela (permeate), sholera (prowl)
- wh: whie (whirl), whao (soar), whai (bolt), whui (hover), wheo (totter), whelumi (cascade), whesuli (slither)

### Three-Syllable Patterns
Various combinations using CV.CV.CV patterns with occasional fricative digraphs:
- Simple CV patterns: meroka (participate), nikolu (manipulate), pelora (navigate)
- With fricatives: sholae (coordinate), phelora (reverberate), whelumi (cascade)
- Mixed patterns: makeri (converge), rameki (emanate), limera (shimmer)

## Semantic Groupings

### Basic Movement
- move, go, come, walk, run, jump, stand, sit

### Directional Movement
- rise, fall, lift, drop, turn, spin, rotate

### Speed Variations
- dart, dash, bolt, zip, creep, inch, trudge, plod

### Flowing Movement
- flow, glide, drift, float, hover, soar

### Rhythmic/Oscillating Movement
- sway, bob, oscillate, undulate, fluctuate

### Force-Based Movement
- push, pull, thrust, propel, plunge, lunge

### Complex Movement Patterns
- weave, spiral, meander, cascade, circulate

### Specialized Movement
- climb, crawl, swim (wade), fly (soar), dance (pirouette)

## Notable Features

1. **Extensive use of vowel hiatus**: Particularly the 'ui' combination appears frequently
2. **Fricative digraphs well-distributed**: All four fricatives (ph, th, sh, wh) are represented
3. **Semantic coherence**: Words for similar concepts often share phonological features
4. **Balance of patterns**: Good mix of simple CV, vowel hiatus, and fricative patterns
5. **Three-syllable words**: Used for more complex or elaborate movements

## Pillar Distribution

The words draw from all five philosophical pillars:
- **Buddhism**: Mindful movements (drift, sway, hover)
- **Solarpunk**: Energetic movements (surge, accelerate, generate)
- **Art Nouveau**: Flowing movements (flow, cascade, shimmer)
- **Peace Linguistics**: Careful movements (dodge, circumvent, retreat)
- **Pre-Industrial**: Natural movements (migrate, prowl, meander)

This category successfully captures the full range of human and natural movement while maintaining the phonological and philosophical integrity of the Phi language.
