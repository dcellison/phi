## Phonological Features

### Sound Distribution
- **Fricative digraphs**: Frequently used (th-, sh-, ph-, wh-)
- **Vowel hiatus patterns**: Common (-oa, -ea, -ia, -eo, -ao, -ua)
- **Liquid consonants**: Prevalent (l, n, m) for flowing qualities
- **Rhotic 'r'**: Minimal use, reserved for special emphasis

### Common Patterns
- CV.CV (simple two-syllable)
- CV.V (with vowel hiatus)
- FV.CV (fricative initial)
- CV.CV.V (three-syllable with final vowel)

## Usage Notes

These abstract quality words can function as:
- **Nouns**: "shialo melu" (beauty [and] empathy)
- **Adjectives**: "shialo kao" (beautiful tree)
- **Some as verbs**: "melu" (to empathize), "theo" (to trust)

The words are designed to promote Phi's core values of peaceful, mindful, sustainable living through their very sound and structure.
