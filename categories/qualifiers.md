# qualifiers

> qualifiers are words or phrases that modify the meaning of other words or phrases by providing additional information, typically about the extent, degree, or intensity of something. they can include adverbs, adverbial phrases, or adverbial clauses that add detail or precision to verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. examples of qualifiers include "very," "extremely," "quite," "somewhat," "too," "rather," and "really." qualifiers help to specify or refine the meaning of other elements in a sentence, adding nuance and clarity to the overall expression.
