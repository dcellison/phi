# The exact opaque frame

The exact frame preserves what Phi must not silently normalize. `patha` announces that Phi interpretation is suspended; the next standalone `patho` returns the utterance to core Phi.

```
mia ne patha Toronto patho nila.
1SG NAME EXT.EXACT [Toronto] EXT.EXACT.CLOSE see.
(I see Toronto.)

mia patha E = mc² patho theo.
1SG EXT.EXACT [E = mc²] EXT.EXACT.CLOSE read.
(I read "E = mc².")

phao patha 5 mg patho to haolu.
parent EXT.EXACT [5 mg] EXT.EXACT.CLOSE PST speak.
(The parent said "5 mg.")
```

Inside the frame, any script, capitalization, punctuation, number, unit, formula, URL, identifier, quotation, or source term may remain as written. The validator does not treat payload tokens as Phi words. It resumes all ordinary checks at `patho`.

A single standalone `patho` closes the frame. If that exact token must occur literally in the payload, double it as `patho patho`; a following single `patho` performs the closure. Everything else inside is opaque, including text that resembles another frame opener.

## Writing and speech

The written exact payload is authoritative. In speech, a speaker may use its source pronunciation, spell it, or describe it through a convention shared with the listener. The boundary survives every mode; the payload may not. This is why the core dictation promise stops at `patha` and resumes at `patho`.

Roman and Tengwar Phi both render the boundary words in their own mode. An exact Chinese name remains Chinese characters; a formula remains mathematical notation; a URL remains a URL. Preserving the source is the purpose of the layer.
