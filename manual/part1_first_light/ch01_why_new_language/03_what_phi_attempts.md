## What Phi attempts, and what it doesn't

We've seen the cracks in inherited languages and the tradition of people who built alternatives. Now the question this book exists to explore is narrower: what kind of practice can a language make available?

Phi's answer is concrete. Modifier-first grammar places participants and relations before the final action. Evidentials let a speaker state how a claim is known. The vocabulary keeps care, sharing, and repair close to hand, while person words remain ungendered and the numeral system resists using exact count as the only account of value. The sound system can return attention to breath and articulation. None of this compels reflection or kindness. Each feature gives the practice somewhere to happen.

And Phi is not a thought experiment on paper. The lexicon holds over nine hundred words, each carrying its own reasoning, and the language has a shelf of literature: the Metta Sutta (*lothea thole*, the Practice of Love), Aesop's *North Wind and the Sun*, the Heart Sutra, chapters of the Tao Te Ching, *The Velveteen Rabbit* entire, and more. Two benchmark fables appear in both forms. Aesop's translation repeats the contest; its transmutation ends with `keloa haolu nela kawhera pholeni thola`, rough speech and coercion bring departure. Part VII is your map to the whole shelf.

Phi carries source texts in two ways. A close translation protects the source's claims while giving them natural Phi grammar. A *transmutation* lets the language reshape the source according to its own distinctions and commitments, then shows the reader what changed. Part VI teaches that second practice; Part VII labels both methods on the text shelf.

### What Phi doesn't attempt

Phi does not aim to be a universal language. It won't replace English, Mandarin, or your mother tongue; it's a second way of thinking, not a substitute for the first.

Nor does it claim philosophical completeness. Its five source traditions are pillars, not final answers: Solarpunk's pragmatic optimism, Buddhist mindfulness and Right Speech, Art Nouveau's organic aesthetics, peace linguistics, pre-industrial wisdom. The building stays open to new rooms.

It doesn't promise to make you a better person, either. A tool can make some choices visible; the work remains yours. Speaking Phi will not dissolve anger. A later chapter shows one transmutation that rebuilds an angry accusation through nonviolent communication, and labels that change as a philosophical choice. Phi offers a practice, not a spell.

That honesty is the last design principle worth naming before you start: this language would rather under-promise than inflate. What it actually does, you're about to experience for yourself. The next chapter starts with the sound of it.
