# Forming plurals

The plural particle **lo** stands before a singular pronoun just as it stands before an unquantified noun.

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| first | **mia** (I, me) | **lo mia** (we, us) |
| second | **thia** (you) | **lo thia** (you all) |
| third | **shia** (another, it) | **lo shia** (they, them) |

## In clauses

**lo mia shea lothea.** — We love peace.

**wa lo thia shea nai.** — Are you all at peace?

**lo shia to shua.** — They arrived.

**mia lo thia whaline.** — I thank you all.

**lo mia lo shia nila.** — We see them.

The first **lo** belongs to the subject and the second to the object. Position keeps the two phrases apart.

## Who belongs to we

**lo mia** does not grammatically distinguish a group that includes the addressee from one that excludes them. Context may settle the membership; when it does not, the speaker can name the participants or state the contrast in another clause.

**mia nela thia** — you and I

**lo mia wepu. thia ma wepu.** — We go. You do not go.

The second example explicitly excludes the addressee from the group meant by **lo mia** in that exchange. The pronoun alone would not.

Three singular forms plus **lo** cover the personal inventory; everyone who will ever stand in a Phi sentence can already be pointed to with them. That regularity is easy to learn. Tracking who belongs to a plural group remains the ordinary work of conversation.
