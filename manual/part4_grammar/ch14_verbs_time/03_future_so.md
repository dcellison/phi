# Future so

**so** places the clause's event or state after the present moment.

**mia lunei so nila.** — I will see the moon.

The narrow channel of **s** opens into rounded **o**. The form is short and easy to hear before another Slot 1 particle; hope, intention, and uncertainty are absent from its sounds.

## Future is a location, not a modal

**mia so theo.** is a future assertion: "I will read." It may report a plan, prediction, promise, schedule, or expectation, but **so** itself settles only time. Possibility and ability take **po**:

**mia so po theo.** — I may be able to read.

Beginning takes **pa**:

**mia so pa theo.** — I will begin to read.

A future statement can be confident or doubtful, welcome or feared. Source, modality, and feeling have their own words when those distinctions matter.

## Examples

| Phi | Gloss | English |
|---|---|---|
| **mia so theo.** | 1SG FUT read | I will read. |
| **thia so shua.** | 2SG FUT come | You will come. |
| **lo mia phialu so nila.** | PL 1SG water FUT see | We will see the water. |

Planning can change what happens next, and some future events lie beyond anyone's control. Grammar does not decide which case a sentence describes. It puts **so** before the verb and leaves causation to the account.
