# The three slots explained

The three slots are a map of scope. They answer a practical question: how much of the sentence does this particle affect?

## Slot 0: the utterance frame

Slot 0 stands at the beginning of an utterance. Its particles tell the listener how to receive the clause that follows:

- **wa** marks a yes/no question.
- **no** marks a directive, which context may make a request or command.
- **lu** marks a realis condition; **lu he** marks an irrealis one.
- **su** marks a wish, hope, or prayer.
- **pi** marks politeness.

An ordinary declarative assertion has no Slot 0 particle. When politeness combines with another frame, it comes first in the documented forms **pi wa** and **pi no**.

## Slot 1: the verb phrase

Slot 1 stands before the verb, with any manner descriptor between the particle block and the verb. Its ranks have one fixed order:

**Tense > Aspect > Voice > Evidentiality > Modality > Negation**

The inventory is closed:

- Tense: past **to**, future **so**; present is unmarked.
- Aspect: complete **ki**, ongoing **si**, beginning **pa**, ending **te**, habitual **ro**.
- Voice: passive **se**, causative **ka**; the one permitted same-rank pair is **se ka**.
- Evidentiality: witnessed **hi**, inferred **ke**, reported **ti**, assumed **ho**.
- Modality: possible or able **po**, necessary **na**.
- Negation: **ma**.

Each rank admits at most one choice in a clause, apart from **se ka**. Two sources need two clauses, and two aspects need two views of the event. The order gives the listener a stable parse without ranking tense as more objective than source or negation as more subjective than voice.

## Slot 2: a constituent

Slot 2 particles stand directly before the word or phrase they modify. They may occur around a noun, a predicate, or another constituent:

- **lo** marks unquantified plurality, and **nu** marks ordinal position.
- **ko** marks focus; **we** adds a constituent; **li** restricts one.
- **mo** forms a comparative, while **mo ko** forms a superlative; **ru** intensifies.
- **ha** and **ra** mark proximal and distal reference.
- **ne** introduces a name atom; **sa**, **ni**, and **le** mark named relationships.

Slot 2 particles nest by scope. Wider relations come earlier: **we** or **li**, then **ha** or **ra**, then **lo** or a numeral, then **ko**, then **ru** or **mo**, then the content word. The proper-name and ordinal pairs keep their own local orders: **ne** precedes an honorific, and **nu** precedes its numeral.

## All three in one sentence

**pi wa thia lo melu so nila.** — Will you please see the friends?

The structure is:

| Part | Form | Work |
|---|---|---|
| Slot 0 | **pi wa** | polite yes/no question |
| Subject | **thia** | you |
| Slot 2 and object | **lo melu** | unquantified plural friends |
| Slot 1 | **so** | future |
| Verb | **nila** | see |

Without a particle, no hidden claim appears. **mia thia nila.** means "I see you" in the present; source, aspect, modality, and negation are all unmarked. A particle appears only when the sentence needs its distinction.
