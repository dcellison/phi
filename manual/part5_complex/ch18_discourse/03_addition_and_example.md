# Addition and example

**sheno** adds another point to the same line of thought. **phisu** gives a concrete case of a general statement. They may occupy the same position, but they ask the next sentence to do different work.

## Addition: `sheno`

**sheno** signals a further point: "furthermore" or "in addition."

```
shelira mioru nai. sheno luphore maeli nai.
forest beautiful be. ADD river quiet be.
(The forest is beautiful. Furthermore the river is quiet.)
```

```
mia shea phaelo. sheno mia lothea phaelo.
1SG peace feel. ADD 1SG love feel.
(I feel peace. Furthermore I feel love.)
```

Unlike **nela**, **sheno** does not join two grammatical elements. It begins a new sentence and places that sentence in the same developing account. The addition may support an argument, expand a description, or simply add another relevant fact.

## Example: `phisu`

**phisu** introduces a concrete example of what has just been said.

```
lo shiro lima nai. phisu ha shiro thuroa.
PL tree alive be. EXMP PROX tree grow.
(Trees are alive. For example this tree grows.)
```

```
mia lo shiro lothea. phisu mia ha whalo shiro lothea.
1SG PL tree love. EXMP 1SG PROX large tree love.
(I love trees. For example I love this large tree.)
```

```
shia welao thumela. phisu shia lo lopia sheluo.
3SG good teach. EXMP 3SG PL child listen.
(They teach well. For example they listen to the children.)
```

An example gives the listener something specific to inspect. It can show how the general claim works or expose where it fails. **phisu** marks it as an example, not as proof.

## The difference between `sheno` and `phisu`

After **shia welao thumela**, the same following clause changes its discourse role according to the marker:

| Phi | English | Relation |
|---|---|---|
| **sheno shia lo lopia sheluo.** | Furthermore, they listen to the children. | another relevant observation |
| **phisu shia lo lopia sheluo.** | For example, they listen to the children. | a proposed instance of teaching well |

The facts in the second sentence have not changed. **sheno** widens the account; **phisu** asks the listener to test the general claim against one case.
