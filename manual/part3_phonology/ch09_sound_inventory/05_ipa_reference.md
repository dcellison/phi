# IPA reference

## Complete pronunciation guide

This section provides a complete reference for Phi's sounds using the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA). Use it to verify pronunciation or look up unfamiliar symbols.

## Vowels

| Letter | IPA | Description | Production |
|--------|-----|-------------|------------|
| a | /ä/ | open central unrounded | Jaw dropped, tongue flat and low, mouth fully open |
| e | /e̞/ | mid front unrounded | Tongue raised to mid-height, positioned forward, lips spread |
| i | /i/ | close front unrounded | Tongue high and forward, close to hard palate, lips spread |
| o | /o̞/ | mid back rounded | Tongue raised to mid-height, pulled back, lips rounded |
| u | /u/ | close back rounded | Tongue high and far back, lips tightly rounded and protruded |

**Key points:**
- All vowels are pure monophthongs (no gliding)
- The symbols /e̞/ and /o̞/ indicate slightly lowered mid vowels
- Vowel quality remains constant throughout production

## Consonants

| Letter | IPA | Type | Place | Manner |
|--------|-----|------|-------|--------|
| m | /m/ | voiced | bilabial | nasal |
| n | /n̪/ | voiced | dental | nasal |
| p | /p/ | voiceless | bilabial | stop (unaspirated) |
| t | /t̪/ | voiceless | dental | stop (unaspirated) |
| k | /k/ | voiceless | velar | stop (unaspirated) |
| l | /l/ | voiced | alveolar | lateral approximant |
| r | /r/ ~ /ɾ/ | voiced | alveolar | trill or tap |
| s | /s/ | voiceless | alveolar | sibilant fricative |
| h | /h/ | voiceless | glottal | fricative |
| w | /w/ | voiced | labio-velar | approximant |

**Key points:**
- Dental consonants /n̪/ and /t̪/ use tongue-to-teeth contact
- Stops are unaspirated (no puff of air after release)
- The /r/ can be realized as either trill or tap

## Fricative digraphs

| Letters | IPA | Type | Place | Manner |
|---------|-----|------|-------|--------|
| ph | /ɸ/ | voiceless | bilabial | fricative |
| th | /θ/ | voiceless | dental | fricative |
| sh | /ʃ/ | voiceless | post-alveolar | fricative |
| wh | /ʍ/ | voiceless | labio-velar | fricative |

**Key points:**
- All four are voiceless (no vocal cord vibration)
- **ph** uses lips only, not teeth
- **th** is always voiceless (as in "think," not "this")
- **wh** combines lip rounding with velar airflow

## Phoneme inventory summary

**Total phonemes:** 19

- 5 vowels: /ä e̞ i o̞ u/
- 10 simple consonants: /m n̪ p t̪ k l r s h w/
- 4 fricative digraphs: /ɸ θ ʃ ʍ/

## IPA symbol quick reference

For readers unfamiliar with IPA notation:

| Symbol | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| /ä/ | open central "a" (as in "father") |
| /e̞/ | mid front "e" (slightly lowered) |
| /i/ | high front "i" (as in "machine") |
| /o̞/ | mid back "o" (slightly lowered) |
| /u/ | high back "u" (as in "food") |
| /n̪/ /t̪/ | dental (tongue touches teeth) |
| /ɸ/ | bilabial fricative (lips only) |
| /θ/ | dental fricative (as in "think") |
| /ʃ/ | post-alveolar fricative (as in "ship") |
| /ʍ/ | voiceless labio-velar (whispered "w") |
| /ɾ/ | alveolar tap (single tongue contact) |
| ~ | indicates free variation between sounds |

## Using this reference

When learning new vocabulary:

1. Look up the word's IPA transcription
2. Identify each sound using the tables above
3. Practice each syllable separately
4. Combine syllables with attention to stress and hiatus

The IPA removes ambiguity. Where English spelling creates confusion (is "read" present or past tense?), IPA notation is precise. Use it as your definitive guide to Phi pronunciation.
