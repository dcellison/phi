# Phi Natural Prototype Adherence Audit — Phase 2

## Methodology

Phase 1 identified 131 words misaligned on **both** domain-sound and pillar-sound axes.
Phase 2 examines each one qualitatively, asking: does the **sound** of this word suggest
its **meaning** to a naive listener? Three possible motivations beyond the framework:

1. **Articulatory iconicity** — the mouth gesture itself mirrors the meaning
2. **Cross-linguistic resonance** — deliberate echo of natural language cognates
3. **Phonesthetic fit** — the overall sound profile (not just initial consonant) fits the meaning

Words that have none of these are **genuinely arbitrary** — revision candidates.

## Strongly motivated (keep as-is)

These words work despite framework mismatch because the sound genuinely evokes the meaning:

### Articulatory iconicity
- `pharoe` (chest) — ph /ɸ/ is literally a breath sound; chest is the breath chamber
- `phulae` (mouth) — ph is a bilabial; mouth is where bilabials are produced
- `phelui` (word) — words are made of breath; ph /ɸ/ = breath
- `phiora` (spirit) — spirit etymologically means "breath" across many languages
- `whinu` (smell) — wh /ʍ/ involves air drawn through the nose
- `howeli` (wind) — h = breath of air; wind is moving air

## Recommendations

### Highest priority revisions (direct contradictions)

These words actively work *against* their initial sound's character:

1. **`shuko` (sturdy)** — `sh` /ʃ/ is the softest, gentlest onset in Phi. "Sturdy" is its
   opposite. Consider `k`-initial (pre-industrial, stone, permanence): e.g., `kuroa`, `keluko`.

2. **`shoruka` (workshop)** — Same problem. A workshop is a place of active making.
   Consider `k`- or `th`-initial.

3. **`paku` (seal/stamp)** — `p` is mapped to peace/softness. Stamping/sealing is decisive
   action. Consider `k`-initial (pre-industrial craftsmanship).

4. **`koeli` (warm/emotional), `komela` (encourage), `kophe` (help)** — The `k`-cluster
   for emotional warmth and aid sits oddly against `k`'s stone/permanence mapping.
   These three could migrate to `sh`-/`n`-/`th`-initial forms.

### Moderate priority (genuinely arbitrary, no contradiction)

The remaining ~30 words don't actively contradict their sound, they just don't particularly
evoke their meaning either. These could be revised opportunistically, especially:

- **Body parts** (`pheloi` muscle, `shelua` shoulder, `whinoa` knee) — body part words
  benefit most from motivated sounds, since learners encounter them early.
- **High-frequency words** (`phiru` exit, `phola` bring, `tone` integral) — common words
  should be maximally sound-symbolic for learnability.
- **Abstract cognition** (`wone` value, `worae` insight, `wuko` careless, `ronai` student,
  `ronua` scholar) — these are core Phi concepts that deserve motivated forms.

### Low priority

- `shao` (three-group) — number words are inherently arbitrary in all languages
- Words in the "moderately motivated" category — they have defensible (if thin) logic
