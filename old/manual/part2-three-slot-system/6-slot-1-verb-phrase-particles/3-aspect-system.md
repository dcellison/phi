# Aspect System

## Eight-Way Aspectual Framework

Phi's aspect system provides sophisticated tools for expressing **how actions unfold in time**. Unlike simple tense (which indicates when), aspect describes the internal temporal structure of events - whether they are ongoing, completed, repeated, or changing state.

## The Complete Aspect Paradigm

| Particle | Aspect | English Gloss | Temporal Structure | Usage Context |
|----------|--------|---------------|-------------------|---------------|
| `we` | Desiderative | "wants to" | Desired action | Expressing intentions |
| `la` | Progressive | "is -ing" | Ongoing action | Current activity |
| `ni` | Present Perfect | "has done" | Past with current relevance | Completed with results |
| `po` | Habitual | "usually does" | Repeated pattern | Regular behavior |
| `pu` | Perfective | "did (completely)" | Completed whole | Finished actions |
| `ri` | Imperfective | "was doing/used to" | Ongoing past | Past background |
| `wi` | Inceptive | "starts doing" | Beginning action | Initiation |
| `wu` | Cessative | "stops doing" | Ending action | Termination |

## Basic Aspectual Categories

### 1. Duration and Completion

#### `la` - Progressive Aspect
**Function**: Marks actions currently in progress or ongoing states.

```
mia la whera.
1SG PROG learn
"I am learning."

ne riwhea la whuru.
INAN wind PROG blow
"The wind is blowing."

sha la na ne lophui whona.
3SG PROG OBJ INAN art look
"S/he is looking at art."
```

**With tense combinations**:
```
mia li la whera.        (past progressive)
1SG PST PROG learn
"I was learning."

mia su la whera.        (future progressive)
1SG FUT PROG learn
"I will be learning."
```

#### `pu` - Perfective Aspect
**Function**: Emphasizes the completion and wholeness of actions.

```
mia pu whera.
1SG PFV learn
"I learned (completely)."

sha li pu na ne whethea whera.
3SG PST PFV OBJ INAN book learn
"S/he had learned from the book (completely)."
```

#### `ri` - Imperfective Aspect
**Function**: Marks ongoing past actions or habitual past states.

```
mia li ri whera.
1SG PST IPFV learn
"I was learning." / "I used to learn."

ne riwhea li ri whuru.
INAN wind PST IPFV blow
"The wind was blowing." / "The wind used to blow."
```

### 2. Temporal Boundaries

#### `wi` - Inceptive Aspect
**Function**: Marks the beginning or initiation of actions.

```
mia wi whera.
1SG INCH learn
"I start learning." / "I begin to learn."

ne riwhea wi whuru.
INAN wind INCH blow
"The wind starts blowing."

sha wi na ne lophui whona.
3SG INCH OBJ INAN art look
"S/he starts looking at art."
```

#### `wu` - Cessative Aspect
**Function**: Marks the ending or termination of actions.

```
mia wu whera.
1SG CESS learn
"I stop learning."

ne riwhea wu whuru.
INAN wind CESS blow
"The wind stops blowing."

sha wu na ne lophui whona.
3SG CESS OBJ INAN art look
"S/he stops looking at art."
```

### 3. Repetition and Patterns

#### `po` - Habitual Aspect
**Function**: Marks regularly repeated actions or characteristic behaviors.

```
mia po whera.
1SG HAB learn
"I usually learn." / "I learn regularly."

ne riwhea po whuru.
INAN wind HAB blow
"The wind usually blows."

sha po shio lo he nowhea whera.
3SG HAB from PL HUM person learn
"S/he usually learns from people."
```

### 4. Current Relevance

#### `ni` - Present Perfect Aspect
**Function**: Marks past actions with continuing relevance to the present.

```
mia ni whera.
1SG PRF learn
"I have learned."

sha ni na ne whethea whera.
3SG PRF OBJ INAN book learn
"S/he has learned from books."

lo mia ni na thueta whisi.
PL 1SG PRF OBJ that see
"We have seen that."
```

### 5. Desire and Intention

#### `we` - Desiderative Aspect
**Function**: Expresses desire, intention, or volition regarding actions.

```
mia we whera.
1SG DES learn
"I want to learn."

sha we na ne lophui whona.
3SG DES OBJ INAN art look
"S/he wants to look at art."

lo mia we shio ne whethea whera.
PL 1SG DES from INAN book learn
"We want to learn from books."
```

## Complex Aspectual Combinations

### Tense + Aspect Interactions

#### Past + Aspect
```
mia li we whera.        (PST + DES)
1SG PST DES learn
"I wanted to learn."

mia li ni whera.        (PST + PRF)
1SG PST PRF learn
"I had learned."

mia li po whera.        (PST + HAB)
1SG PST HAB learn
"I used to learn (habitually)."
```

#### Future + Aspect
```
mia su we whera.        (FUT + DES)
1SG FUT DES learn
"I will want to learn."

mia su po whera.        (FUT + HAB)
1SG FUT HAB learn
"I will usually learn."

mia su wi whera.        (FUT + INCH)
1SG FUT INCH learn
"I will start learning."
```

### Multiple Aspect Particles
Some aspectual combinations create nuanced meanings:

```
mia li wi la whera.     (PST + INCH + PROG)
1SG PST INCH PROG learn
"I was starting to learn."

mia su po wi whera.     (FUT + HAB + INCH)
1SG FUT HAB INCH learn
"I will usually start learning."
```

## Aspectual Contrasts

### Completion vs. Ongoing
```
mia pu whera.           (completed)
1SG PFV learn
"I learned (completely)."

mia la whera.           (ongoing)
1SG PROG learn
"I am learning."
```

### Beginning vs. Ending
```
mia wi whera.           (starting)
1SG INCH learn
"I start learning."

mia wu whera.           (stopping)
1SG CESS learn
"I stop learning."
```

### Habitual vs. One-time
```
mia po whera.           (regular)
1SG HAB learn
"I usually learn."

mia pu whera.           (single event)
1SG PFV learn
"I learned (once, completely)."
```

## Register Variation

### Formal Academic
Rich aspectual marking for precision:
```
hi mia li po pu whera ro mia ta ni riphe phera.
DIR 1SG PST HAB PFV learn INF 1SG PRS PRF important be
"I used to learn completely (I saw this), so I have become important (I infer)."
```

### Informal Conversation
Simplified aspectual marking:
```
mia po whera.
1SG HAB learn
"I usually learn."
```

## Cross-Linguistic Perspectives

### Aspectual Universals
Phi's system reflects cross-linguistic patterns:
- **Slavic**: Perfective/imperfective distinction (`pu`/`ri`)
- **English**: Progressive aspect (`la`)
- **Mandarin**: Aspectual particles vs. tense
- **Bantu**: Complex aspectual systems

### Innovative Features
- **Systematic ordering**: Consistent particle hierarchy
- **Clear semantics**: Each particle has distinct meaning
- **Compositional**: Predictable combinations
- **Learner-friendly**: No irregular forms

## Advanced Usage

### Narrative Aspectual Flow
```
mia wi whera. mia la whera. mia pu whera. mia wu whera.
1SG INCH learn. 1SG PROG learn. 1SG PFV learn. 1SG CESS learn.
"I started learning. I was learning. I learned completely. I stopped learning."
```

### Aspectual Emphasis
```
ma wi mia whera.        (emphasizing start)
EMPH INCH 1SG learn
"I *start* learning."

mia ma pu whera.        (emphasizing completion)
1SG EMPH PFV learn
"I learned *completely*."
```

### Complex Temporal Sequences
```
mia li ri whera. mia ta la whera. mia su wu whera.
1SG PST IPFV learn. 1SG PRS PROG learn. 1SG FUT CESS learn.
"I was learning. I am learning. I will stop learning."
```
