# Tense System

## Three-Way Temporal Reference

Phi employs a straightforward three-way tense system that establishes **temporal reference** for actions and states. Unlike complex natural language tense systems, phi's approach prioritizes clarity and cross-linguistic accessibility while covering all essential temporal relationships.

## The Complete Tense Paradigm

| Particle | Tense | English Gloss | Temporal Reference | Usage Context |
|----------|-------|---------------|-------------------|---------------|
| `li` | Past | "did/was" | Before speech time | Completed actions, past states |
| `ta` | Present | "do/are" | At speech time | Current actions, general truths |
| `su` | Future | "will/shall" | After speech time | Planned actions, predictions |

## Individual Tense Analysis

### 1. `li` - Past Tense

**Function**: Marks actions or states that occurred before the time of speaking.

**Core usage principles**:
- Completed events in the past
- Past states and conditions
- Historical facts and narratives
- Sequential past actions

**Basic past patterns**:
```
mia li whera.
1SG PST learn
"I learned." / "I did learn."

ne riwhea li whuru.
INAN wind PST blow
"The wind blew."

he nowhea li shio ne whethea whera.
HUM person PST from INAN book learn
"The person learned from books."
```

**Past with objects**:
```
sha li na ne lophui whona.
3SG PST OBJ INAN art look
"S/he looked at art."

mia li shio lo he nowhea whera.
1SG PST from PL HUM person learn
"I learned from people."
```

**Narrative sequences**:
```
mia li sharo. mia li ne whethea phina. mia li whera.
1SG PST go. 1SG PST INAN book find. 1SG PST learn.
"I went. I found a book. I learned."
```

### 2. `ta` - Present Tense

**Function**: Marks actions or states occurring at the time of speaking, or general truths.

**Core usage principles**:
- Current ongoing actions
- General truths and facts
- Habitual actions (when combined with `po`)
- States existing at speech time

**Basic present patterns**:
```
mia ta whera.
1SG PRS learn
"I learn." / "I am learning."

ne riwhea ta whuru.
INAN wind PRS blow
"The wind blows." / "The wind is blowing."

sha ta riphe phera.
3SG PRS important be
"S/he is important."
```

**General truths**:
```
ne riwhea ta whuru.
INAN wind PRS blow
"Wind blows." (general fact)

lo he nowhea ta whera.
PL HUM person PRS learn
"People learn." (universal truth)
```

**Optional in informal speech**:
```
mia whera.          (informal)
mia ta whera.       (formal/explicit)
1SG (PRS) learn
"I learn."
```

### 3. `su` - Future Tense

**Function**: Marks actions or states that will occur after the time of speaking.

**Core usage principles**:
- Planned future actions
- Predictions and expectations
- Future intentions
- Conditional consequences (with `tu`)

**Basic future patterns**:
```
mia su whera.
1SG FUT learn
"I will learn."

ne riwhea su whuru.
INAN wind FUT blow
"The wind will blow."

sha su na ne lophui whona.
3SG FUT OBJ INAN art look
"S/he will look at art."
```

**Future planning**:
```
mia su shio ne whethea whera.
1SG FUT from INAN book learn
"I will learn from books."

lo mia su na thueta whisi.
PL 1SG FUT OBJ that see
"We will see that."
```

**Conditional futures** (with `tu`):
```
tu mia li whera, mia su riphe whemo.
COND 1SG PST learn, 1SG FUT important think
"If I had learned, I will think it important."
```

## Register and Formality

### Formal Contexts
All tense particles explicitly marked:
```
so mia li na ne whethea ta whera su na ne lophui whona.
POL 1SG PST OBJ INAN book PRS learn FUT OBJ INAN art look
"I learned from books, am learning, and will look at art." (polite/formal)
```

### Informal Contexts
Present tense often omitted:
```
mia whera.
1SG learn
"I learn." (present understood from context)
```

### Written vs. Spoken
- **Written**: Explicit tense marking preferred
- **Spoken**: Present tense frequently omitted
- **Formal speech**: All tense particles included

## Interaction with Aspect

Tense particles combine systematically with aspect particles:

### Past + Aspect
```
mia li la whera.        (PST + PROG)
1SG PST PROG learn
"I was learning."

mia li pu whera.        (PST + PFV)
1SG PST PFV learn
"I had learned (completely)."
```

### Present + Aspect
```
mia ta la whera.        (PRS + PROG)
1SG PRS PROG learn
"I am learning."

mia ta ni whera.        (PRS + PRF)
1SG PRS PRF learn
"I have learned."
```

### Future + Aspect
```
mia su la whera.        (FUT + PROG)
1SG FUT PROG learn
"I will be learning."

mia su po whera.        (FUT + HAB)
1SG FUT HAB learn
"I will usually learn."
```

## Cross-Linguistic Comparisons

### Simplicity vs. Complexity
**Phi's approach**:
- Three basic distinctions
- Regular particle placement
- No irregular forms

**Natural language variation**:
- English: 12+ tense-aspect combinations
- Spanish: Complex conjugation system
- Mandarin: Aspect-prominent (similar to phi)

### Universal Accessibility
Phi's three-way system maps clearly onto major language families:
- **Germanic**: past/present/future core distinctions
- **Romance**: regular temporal marking
- **East Asian**: particle-based temporal reference
- **Bantu**: systematic temporal particles

## Advanced Usage Patterns

### Narrative Sequencing
```
mia li sharo. ta whisi. su whera.
1SG PST go. PRS see. FUT learn.
"I went. I see. I will learn." (temporal progression)
```

### Temporal Contrast
```
mia li me whera mi mia ta whera.
1SG PST NEG learn CNTR 1SG PRS learn
"I didn't learn, but I (do) learn (now)."
```

### Complex Temporal Reference
```
hi mia li whera ro mia su riphe phera.
DIR 1SG PST learn INF 1SG FUT important be
"I learned (I saw this) so I will be important (I infer)."
```
