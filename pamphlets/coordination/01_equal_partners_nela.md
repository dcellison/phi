# Part 1: Equal partners: `nela`

`nela` joins elements of equal grammatical status. The two sides must be comparable pieces of structure: a noun with a noun, a verb with a verb, a clause with a clause.

```
mia shea nela lothea phaelo.
1SG peace COORD love feel.
(I feel peace and love.)
```

```
mia theo nela sheluo.
1SG read COORD listen.
(I read and listen.)
```

Two objects sharing one verb, two actions sharing one subject. In each, `nela` holds the pair level: no sequence, no cause, no ranking. Peace does not produce love in the first sentence, and the reading does not precede the listening in the second. The conjunction says together, and stops.

### Phrases keep their shape

Longer phrases ride on either side with their internal order untouched:

```
mia mioru peloru nela whalo shiro nila.
1SG beautiful flower COORD large tree see.
(I see a beautiful flower and a large tree.)
```

Each side is a complete noun phrase built exactly as the nouns pamphlet builds them; `nela` stands at the seam and joins the finished wholes.

### Clauses in one sentence

At clause level, each side carries its own subject, objects, and verb, and the period waits for both:

```
mia theo nela thia sheluo.
1SG read COORD 2SG listen.
(I read, and you listen.)
```

```
sileta sulae nai nela howeli phaelu nai.
sun warm be COORD wind peaceful be.
(The sun is warm, and the wind is peaceful.)
```

The first verb completes its clause; `nela` opens the second; one sentence holds the pair. Whether two facts belong in one sentence or two is a real choice, and part 5 takes it up.

### Every join says its name

A list of three or more repeats `nela` at every boundary:

```
shiro nela peloru nela whelina.
tree COORD flower COORD grass.
(Trees and flowers and grass.)
```

Each join is audible, each item worth its own and. A list built this way cannot slur its members together, and any ranking among them would need words of its own.

### Practice: hold the pair level

1. I love friends and family.
2. The tree is beautiful and large.
3. I read, and you listen.
4. Trees and flowers and grass, every joint sounded.

Answers: 1 `mia melu nela lumani lothea.` 2 `shiro mioru nela whalo nai.` 3 `mia theo nela thia sheluo.` 4 `shiro nela peloru nela whelina.` Four pairs and a list, all level, nothing ranked, nothing implied between the partners but the and itself.
