# Appendix: quick reference

## The joiners

| Word | Gloss | Relation | Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| `nela` | COORD | and: level, together | words, phrases, clauses |
| `thona` | ADVRS | but: the second against expectation | words, phrases, clauses |
| `sola` | DISJ | or: the fork, paths open | words, phrases, clauses |
| `we` | ALSO | added alongside the established | at the edge of the added constituent |

Across a sentence boundary, the discourse markers take over: `whekai` for the redirect, `thelao` for the conclusion drawn.

## Working rules

- `nela`, `thona`, and `sola` join comparable structure only: noun with noun, verb with verb, clause with clause.
- A list of three or more sounds `nela` at every boundary.
- `nela` claims together and nothing else; sequence takes the sequence frames, and cause takes `lao` or `thelao`.
- `thona` keeps both sides asserted; the expectation it overturns is the speaker's framing, sayable another way.
- `sola` leaves the fork open; exclusivity, when it matters, is stated in its own sentence.
- `we` reaches back to an established alternative and stands at the outer edge of exactly what it adds: before the subject for a new participant, before the verb for an added action.
- A contrast inside one thought takes `thona`; a turn that redirects the line takes the period and `whekai`.
- Coordinated clauses keep their own tense, negation, and particles, and an established subject may drop in the second clause as anywhere else.
