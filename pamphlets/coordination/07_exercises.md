# Part 7: Exercises

Answers follow at the end. Before checking any form, name the relation: level pair, contrast, fork, addition, or something that wants its own sentence.

## Part A: Name the join

For each item, name the joiner, its level, and what is claimed.

1. `mia shea nela lothea phaelo.`
2. `shiro mioru nai thona thiku nai.`
3. `wa thia theo sola sheluo.`
4. `lo lopia mua thepalu wile. we lohau wile.`
5. `shiro nela peloru nela whelina.`

## Part B: Build the join

Render each English item in Phi.

6. I read and listen.
7. The sun is warm, and the wind is peaceful.
8. They are brave but mindful.
9. I will read, or I will walk.
10. You wish for peace or quiet.
11. A friend comes. A small person comes too.

## Part C: Repair or defend

Some items are ungrammatical, some claim the wrong thing for the stated intent, and some are fine. Judge each.

12. `*shiro nela peloru whelina.` intended as "trees, flowers, and grass."
13. `*shiro we peloru` intended as "trees and flowers."
14. `mia to theo thona to ma shelomu.` intended as "I read, but did not understand."
15. `*mia peloru nela thia theo nila.` intended as "I see the flower and you read."
16. `lohau we wile.` intended as "the dog joins the players."

## Part D: Choose the joiner

Name the relation, then say which joiner carries it and why.

17. I understand, but you do not. One thought.
18. A stretch of evidence has run for three sentences; now the account turns against it.
19. Is the sun warm or the wind cold? One question.
20. The friend came. Now add the child's coming alongside.

## Part E: Translate

21. `mia melu nela lumani lothea.`
22. `shia haolu thona lo miona ma sheluo.`
23. `thia po mia naphe sola mia po miso naphe.`
24. `mia so theo sola mia so thalo.`

---

## Answer key

### Part A

1. `nela` at word level: two objects of the feeling, level, together. 2. `thona` at clause level: both qualities asserted, the smallness set against the expectation the beauty raised. 3. `sola` inside a question: the fork stands within `wa`, and the listener may take either path. 4. `we` opening the second sentence: the dog is added to the established players, participant as news. 5. `nela` at every list boundary: three members, each join audible.

### Part B

6. `mia theo nela sheluo.` 7. `sileta sulae nai nela howeli phaelu nai.` 8. `shia shaelo thona thesua nai.` 9. `mia so theo sola mia so thalo.` 10. `thia shea sola maeli pula.` 11. `melu shua. we thiku miona shua.`

### Part C

12. Ungrammatical for the meaning: the list needs every joint. `shiro nela peloru nela whelina.` 13. Ungrammatical: `we` reaches back, and nothing is established to reach back to. `shiro nela peloru.` 14. Fine, defend it: clause coordination with the second subject dropped while it remains recoverable, tense and negation intact. The machinery composing exactly as designed. 15. Ungrammatical: an object noun cannot pair with a clause. `mia peloru nila nela thia theo.` 16. Grammatical but the wrong edge: it adds playing to the dog's doings. The new participant takes `we` before the subject: `we lohau wile.`

### Part D

17. `thona`, the local contrast inside one sentence: `mia shelomu thona thia ma shelomu.` 18. `whekai` after a period; the turn redirects the whole line, and the conjunction's scope is too small to hold it. 19. `wa` with `sola` inside: `wa sileta sulae nai sola howeli pelui nai.` 20. `we` opening the addition: `melu to shua. we lopia to shua.`

### Part E

21. I love friends and family. 22. They speak, but the people do not listen. 23. You can help me, or I can help myself: two full paths, and the reflexive bending the second one home. 24. I will read, or I will walk: both futures live, the choice not yet made.
