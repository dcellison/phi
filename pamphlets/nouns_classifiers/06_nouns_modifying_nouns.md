# Part 6: Nouns modifying nouns

Put one noun before another and the first modifies the second. This single pattern carries an entire family of meanings:

| Phi | Literal gloss | Likely reading |
|---|---|---|
| `phao shelu` | parent book | the parent's book |
| `shiro lirowa` | tree leaf | the tree's leaf |
| `nitho ruela` | north path | the north path |
| `shiro womu` | tree home | a tree house |
| `pukea ruela` | danger path | a dangerous path |

The first two rows read as possession or part; the next reads as direction; then kind; then character. Nothing in the grammar labels which relation is meant. The nouns stand adjacent, and meaning plus context choose among owner, source, direction, kind, and association, the way "tree house" and "tree leaf" resolve differently in English without anyone consulting a rule.

### When the relation matters

Adjacency offers the relation without certifying it. `sila kerou`, a community stone, might be owned, gathered, or simply lying at the center of the village, and most sentences are happy with that openness. When the exact relation carries weight, a clause names it: who holds the stone, who set it there, who tends it. The phrase associates; the sentence asserts. Keeping those jobs separate is what lets the phrase stay small.

### The one-way bridge

This pattern is the nouns' own contribution to description, and it runs beside the quality-noun rule rather than merging with it. An adjective can stand as its quality; a noun before a noun modifies by association. What no word does is change class: `pukea` stays a noun even while `pukea ruela` does an adjective's practical work, a danger path rather than a dangerous one, and the lexicon lists every word exactly once.

### Practice: read the relation

1. The friend's garden.
2. A tree house.
3. A dangerous path, built from a noun.
4. The community's story, and say what stays open.

Answers: 1 `melu thepalu.` 2 `shiro womu`, the kind of home named by its material and place. 3 `pukea ruela`, danger standing in front as the path's character. 4 `sila nophi`; whether the community owns the story, tells it, or lives inside it stays open, and a following sentence can settle whichever relation the moment needs.
