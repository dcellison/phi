# Part 2: Marking many: `lo`

When plurality matters and no count is on offer, `lo` stands immediately before the noun phrase:

```
mia lo melu nila.
1SG PL friend see.
(I see friends.)
```

`lo melu` claims more than one and stops there: no count, no estimate, just the fact of the many. It is the same particle that built `lo mia`, we, in the pronouns pamphlet, and here it does the same work for every noun in the lexicon.

### One phrase, one quantity marker

Here is the rule that saves every learner a class of errors: a noun phrase takes exactly one quantity marker. A numeral or a quantifier already carries quantity, so `lo` never joins them.

| Correct | Never | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `wi melu` | `*wi lo melu` | two friends |
| `sheloi melu` | `*sheloi lo melu` | many friends |
| `phina shiro` | `*phina lo shiro` | few trees |
| `theula melu` | `*theula lo melu` | all friends |
| `mawha melu` | `*mawha lo melu` | no friends |

English says "the two friends" and "many of the friends," layering its little words, and the reflex follows the translation. Phi's phrase keeps one quantity claim, made once, by whichever word makes it.

### The full menu

Four strategies cover every quantity a phrase can need, and they are alternatives, never teammates:

| Expression | Claim |
|---|---|
| `melu` | friend or friends; number unstated |
| `lo melu` | more than one; no count |
| `ta melu` | exactly one |
| `sheloi melu` | contextually many |

Exact numerals, which the next part opens, extend the third row to any count the ternary system can build. The choosing among rows is a matter of honesty, and part 3 drills it.

### Practice: one marker each

1. I see friends.
2. Trees, explicitly more than one.
3. Two friends. Then say why `lo` stays away.
4. All friends, and again no `lo`.

Answers: 1 `mia lo melu nila.` 2 `lo shiro.` 3 `wi melu`; the numeral already carries the quantity, and the phrase admits one quantity claim. 4 `theula melu`; the quantifier is the claim, whole and alone. One marker, chosen once: the rule fits in four words and covers every phrase you will ever build.
