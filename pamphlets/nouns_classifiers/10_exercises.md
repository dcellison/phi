# Part 10: Exercises

Answers follow at the end. Before checking any form, read the phrase left to right and say what each word narrows.

## Part A: Parse the phrase

Name the job of every word.

1. `mia ha serao shelu`
2. `ta shao themo kerou`
3. `sheloi welao melu`
4. `sila nophi`
5. `lo shiro`

## Part B: Build the phrase

Render each English phrase or sentence in Phi.

6. Friends, explicitly more than one.
7. Exactly two trees, classified as living beings.
8. Few trees.
9. A tree house.
10. My friend's book.
11. I have two baskets, counted and classified.

## Part C: Repair or defend

Some items are ungrammatical, some claim the wrong thing for the stated intent, and some are fine. Judge each.

12. `*sheloi lo melu` intended as "many friends."
13. `*ta himo shao melu` intended as "exactly three friends, classified."
14. `shao wiru` reported after counting the baskets one by one: three.
15. `*serao mia shelu` intended as "my old book."
16. `ta nophe nophi` intended as "one story."

## Part D: Choose the honest claim

Pick the phrase each situation supports, and say why.

17. You counted the baskets: two.
18. You looked over the meadow: flowers, contextually many.
19. Bread for the table tonight, and there is sufficient.
20. An audit records zero friends present; a sigh says there are none. Give both phrases.

## Part E: Translate

21. `mia lo melu nila.`
22. `ta shao mioru thepalu`
23. `pukea ruela`
24. `mia wi themo wiru phelu.`

## Answer key

### Part A

1. `mia` possessor, `ha` pointer, `serao` descriptor, `shelu` the noun: this old book of mine. 2. `ta shao` the exact count, `themo` the classifier after the whole number, `kerou` the noun: exactly three stones, as objects. 3. `sheloi` the quantity judgment, `welao` the descriptor, `melu` the noun: many good friends. 4. `sila` a noun modifying `nophi`: the community's story, the exact relation left open. 5. `lo` unquantified plurality over `shiro`: trees, more than one, no count.

### Part B

6. `lo melu.` 7. `wi lipha shiro.` 8. `phina shiro.` 9. `shiro womu.` 10. `mia melu shelu.` 11. `mia wi themo wiru phelu.`

### Part C

12. Ungrammatical: two quantity markers. `sheloi melu.` 13. Ungrammatical: the classifier interrupted the number. The count completes first: `ta shao himo melu.` 14. Wrong for the claim: the tally supports exactness, so say `ta shao wiru.` Bare `shao` claims a scale you did better than. 15. Ungrammatical for the meaning: the possessor leads. `mia serao shelu.` 16. Fine, defend it: the classifier is optional, correctly placed after the numeral, and `ta nophi` would be equally correct.

### Part D

17. `wi wiru.` The count happened; the phrase reports it. 18. `sheloi peloru.` The judgment is many; `lo peloru` would claim only plurality and waste what the look told you. 19. `henoi napa.` Sufficiency against tonight's purpose, which no exact count states better. 20. `mu melu` for the audit's exact zero; `mawha melu` for the bench where no friends sit. Same absence, two claims, each true to what the moment knows.

### Part E

21. I see friends. 22. Exactly three beautiful gardens. 23. A dangerous path: the danger stands in front as the path's character, a noun doing an adjective's practical work. 24. I have two baskets: the possessed phrase complete inside the clause, and `phelu` making the having a claim of its own.
