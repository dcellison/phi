# Part 5: Translation and commitment

A source, a translation, and an analysis are three different artifacts. Preserve the source separately, label the Phi translation as a translation, and state when an analysis adds interpretation or criticism.

Source statement outside Phi: `The device is safe.`

Possible Phi translation:

```
ha keli shurano nai.
PROX device safe be.
(This device is safe.)
```

This translation commits the writer to a Phi safety claim; it does not prove the source was correct, the device was inspected, the term *safe* had the same scope, or affected people consented to its use. Labeling keeps the seam visible: a reader who cannot check the source at least knows where trust is being asked for. Translation is an accountable act rather than invisible carriage.
