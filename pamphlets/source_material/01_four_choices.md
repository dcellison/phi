# Part 1: Four choices

When an outside expression appears, first decide what kind of need it represents.

| Need | Choice | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Phi already expresses the concept | Use canonical vocabulary | Ordinary Phi syntax |
| The concept is occasional and transparent in parts | Compose an ordinary multiword expression | Every token remains Phi |
| A proper designation needs a Phi form | Use `ne` plus a listed content word or accepted onym of two, three, or four syllables | One Phi name token |
| The source form itself matters | Preserve it outside the Phi passage | Its script, punctuation, notation, and provenance remain intact |

Vocabulary modules make the first choice broader without changing grammar. Separate source presentation keeps the fourth choice honest without creating a third class of half-Phi material.

### Drill

Classify each need: a recurring ecological concept, a one-time description of a tool, a community-approved short name, an exact dosage, a legal quotation, a mathematical formula, and a tradition-specific term whose source form is under discussion.
