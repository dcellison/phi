## Part D: The dictation test

With a partner, or with tomorrow-morning-you: compose a message of eight to twelve words containing at least one `wa`, one `shola тАж sholo` pair, one `ne`, and one `su`. Speak it once, at recitation pace. Have it written down from hearing alone, then compare, letter for letter, period for period. There is no key for Part D тАФ the transcript is the key, and it either matches or it teaches.

## Answer key

**Part A.**

1. `wa` тАФ `wa thia shua.` The mark moved from the end of the English to the front of the Phi, which is the whole system in one move.
2. `kona`, and `no` for the asking тАФ `kona melu. no meilo.` Two little sentences; the comma became the period between them.
3. `shola тАж sholo` with a speaking-verb frame тАФ `wheo shola muila theula howela sholo to haolu.` The colon and both quotation marks arrived as words.
4. `lu`, and the period тАФ `lu pheralu lepa. lo mia mua womu meilo.` English's busiest comma is Phi's one silent mark, promoted.
5. `nela`, twice тАФ `shiro nela peloru nela whelina.` Between every item, so the Oxford comma has nothing left to disambiguate.

**Part B.**

6. `wa thia shua.` тАФ the `?` was `wa`'s work, already done at the first syllable.
7. `kona lopia. kia.` тАФ the vocative is its own utterance; the comma was English notating a boundary Phi gives a period.
8. `ne siora shua.` тАФ position earns no capital; `ne` was already the tallest thing a name gets.
9. `su theula towe nai.` тАФ the wish was announced as a wish by `su`; the `!` added volume to a sentence that carries hope, not volume.
10. `mia mena shia shua meno sano.` тАФ the damage here was an absence: without `meno`, two verbs collide and no one knows whose sentence ended. The only repair in this set that adds a word, because the missing mark was a word.

**Part C.**

11. `ne sulae shola wa phitura phialu sulae nai sholo to thilou.` тАФ the frame verb is `thilou` (inquire), canon's frame for received questions; and enjoy the sentence asking whether `phialu` is `sulae` while `ne sulae` does the asking тАФ one announcement keeps the person and the warmth from ever colliding.
12. `kona lopia. thia hina nila.` тАФ the dash was `kona`'s pause between utterances; the question mark was `hina`, sitting where the answer will.
13. `shai pheralu to nai lo peloru thuroa.` тАФ the fronted-clause comma dissolves: `shai` announced the concession going in, `nai` sealed it coming out, and the flowers grow with no mark between.
