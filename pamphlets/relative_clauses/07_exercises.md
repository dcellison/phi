### Gap identification

1. Subject (the person creates)
2. Object (I read the book)
3. Oblique/locative (I walked in the forest; the stranded `mua` marks the gap)
4. Subject (the flower is beautiful)
5. Oblique/recipient (they spoke to the person; the stranded `wei` marks the gap)
6. Subject (the tree grows)
7. Object (I saw the home)
8. Subject (the person taught the child)

### Error correction

1. Wrong order ‚Üí `rena mia to nila peloru phelora nai`
2. Missing head noun ‚Üí `mia rena shia to kealo nophi theo`
3. False closer ‚Üí `rena mia to theo shelu phelora nai`
4. Word order/structure ‚Üí `mia rena to shua miona sano`
5. `rena` at end ‚Üí `mia rena mia to nila miona sano`
6. The `mia` has no role in the clause; `phelora nai` takes no argument beyond the gap ‚Üí `rena phelora nai peloru thuroa` ("the flower that is beautiful grows")
7. Missing head ‚Üí `rena shia nophi kealo miona phue nai`
8. Missing head ‚Üí `mia rena mia to naphe miona sano`
