# Part 5: Carriage and commitment

Phi's core lexicon embodies choices. Exact reporting prevents those choices from becoming an inability to tell the truth about the world.

## Testimony and history

```
shia patha lord patho to haolu.
3SG EXT.EXACT [lord] EXT.EXACT.CLOSE PST speak.
(They said the word "lord.")
```

The sentence neither grants the title nor hides that it was spoken. Replacing the source with `rena pilu miona` might provide a Phi analysis, but it would not be an exact report of the word used.

## Identity

```
shia patha nonbinary patho nai.
3SG EXT.EXACT [nonbinary] EXT.EXACT.CLOSE be.
(They are nonbinary.)
```

The existing refusal to coin gendered person classes in core Phi must not erase a person's self-description. Exact form carries the requested term. A later core proposal would still require separate analysis.

## Care and safety

An exact dose, date, unit, device code, or legal citation belongs in `patha … patho` whenever approximation could cause harm. Phi's preference for natural measures remains a core practice, not a reason to corrupt medical or technical information.

## Philosophical critique

Use core composition when the analysis is the argument. Use exact form when the inherited category itself is under examination. A careful essay may alternate between them, always marking which operation it is performing.

The frame says *carried from outside*. Evidentials and surrounding Phi sentences say who reported it, what the speaker infers, and whether they agree.
