# Exercises

Answers follow at the end. Parts A through D ask you to work from the table in chapter 2 and the rules in chapters 4 and 5: check your reasoning against the key, not just your answer.

**Part A: Read the tengwa.** Name the sound or the tengwa, whichever the item withholds.

1. What tengwa carries `k`?
2. What sound does hyarmen carry?
3. What tengwa carries `sh`?
4. What sound does vala carry?
5. What tengwa carries `n`?

**Part B: Place the tehta.** For each word, say which vowel rides above which consonant, left to right. None of these five have a hiatus.

6. `melu`
7. `manuwe`
8. `shonela`
9. `keruko`
10. `towe`

**Part C: Find the hiatus.** Each word carries exactly one hiatus. Name the tengwa that carries it and which vowel rides above, which below.

11. `nomei`
12. `hurao`
13. `maeli`
14. `phelui`
15. `whunei`

**Part D: Rómen or órë?** For each word, name the r-form its own `r` takes.

16. `roela`
17. `keruko`
18. `repha`
19. `kuroi`
20. `hurao`

**Part E: Translate into Phi.** Use the bare-verb pattern chapter 1 opened with, or the `nai` pattern chapter 6 closed with.

21. I read a book.
22. I teach a student.
23. The path is quiet.
24. I trust a friend.
25. I write a name.

**Part F: A line of your own.** Choose four or five words from the appendix's vocabulary table and write one short line by hand (a greeting, a single sentence, a name). Check every tengwa and every tehta against the tables in chapters 2 and 3 before you trust it. There is no key for Part F. The check was always going to be your own hand against the page.

## Answer key

**Part A.** 1: calma. 2: h. 3: harma. 4: w. 5: númen.

**Part B.** 6: `melu`: m carries e, l carries u. 7: `manuwe`: m carries a, n carries u, w carries e. 8: `shonela`: sh carries o, n carries e, l carries a. 9: `keruko`: k carries e, r carries u, k carries o. 10: `towe`: t carries o, w carries e.

**Part C.** 11: `nomei`: malta carries the hiatus, e above, i below (númen, first, carries only o). 12: `hurao`: órë carries the hiatus, a above, o below (the r is internal, so órë, not rómen; this item also belongs to Part D). 13: `maeli`: malta carries the hiatus, a above, e below. 14: `phelui`: lambë carries the hiatus, u above, i below. 15: `whunei`: númen carries the hiatus, e above, i below.

**Part D.** 16: `roela`: rómen (word-initial). 17: `keruko`: órë (word-internal). 18: `repha`: rómen (word-initial). 19: `kuroi`: órë (word-internal). 20: `hurao`: órë (word-internal).
