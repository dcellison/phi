# wuloe wetha tupiwa — The Velveteen Rabbit

*Margery Williams's nursery story (1922, public domain) is Phi's eighth text and its longest transmutation: a full narrative arc rather than excerpts, exercising the tense and aspect system, the quotative frames, and the becoming-vocabulary across every scene. It required exactly two new words — `tupiwa`, rabbit, and `wilemo`, toy — and two rulings the notes explain: Real is `shewo` (true), defined in the story by the one who knows; and the nursery magic Fairy's work is done, as in the Babel text, by the world itself — spring completes what love begins. Everything else was already here, waiting: the leather horse (`kori kalora`), the fire-sickness (`thero mokela`), the winter festival (`shila holia`), and the oldest compound in the registry — `mirae phialu`, tears — finally given the scene it was coined for.*

---

## The arrival

```
shila holia to nai.
winter festival PST be.
(It was the winter festival.)

ta wuloe wetha tupiwa mua lopia tholu to nai.
one soft cloth rabbit LOC child space PST be.
(A soft cloth rabbit was in the child's room.)

shia roe wuloe wetha to se pilewa. shia wolea muhena phelu.
3SG INS soft cloth PST PASS make. 3SG wood dust hold.
(He was made with soft cloth. Inside, he held wood-dust.)

lopia shia to nila. lopia shia to lothea.
child 3SG PST see. child 3SG PST love.
(The child saw him. The child loved him.)
```

**Notes:** the velveteen is a composition, `wuloe wetha` — soft cloth — and the sawdust another, `wolea muhena`, wood-dust: the Rabbit's whole materiality assembled from words the lexicon already held. Christmas transmutes to `shila holia`, the winter festival, which is what it is in every home the story reaches.

## The nursery

```
lo keli newu to nai.
PL device new PST be.
(The mechanical toys were new.)

lo keli shola lo mia shewo nai sholo ro haolu.
PL device QUOT.COMP PL 1SG true be QUOT.COMP.CLOSE HAB speak.
(The mechanical toys kept saying: "We are real.")

tupiwa mawha keli phelu. tupiwa nuhe to phaelo.
rabbit NONE device hold. rabbit sadness PST feel.
(The rabbit held no machinery. The rabbit felt sad.)
```

**Notes:** the nursery's snobbery survives translation but loses its teeth. The proud toys are `lo keli`, the devices, and their boast sits in a habitual quotative — they *kept saying* it, which is what pretending is. In the original they claim superiority; in Phi they can only claim to be real, because the language gives them no way to rank themselves above anyone. The claim will be answered by the story.

## The leather horse

```
kori kalora serao to nai. kori kalora sano to nai.
leather horse old PST be. leather horse know PST be.
(The Leather Horse was old. The Leather Horse was wise.)

tupiwa shola hina shewo nai sholo to thilou.
rabbit QUOT.COMP what true be QUOT.COMP.CLOSE PST inquire.
(The rabbit asked: "What is real?")

kori kalora shola shewo lue pilewa ma shua sholo to haolu.
leather horse QUOT.COMP true ABL make NEG come QUOT.COMP.CLOSE PST speak.
(The Leather Horse said: "Real does not come from how you are made.")

shola lu lopia thia ro lothea. thia shewo kelu sholo.
QUOT.COMP COND child 2SG HAB love. 2SG true become QUOT.COMP.CLOSE
("If a child loves you, habitually, you become real.")

shola lu thia shewo nai. kipona thiku nai sholo.
QUOT.COMP COND 2SG true be. pain small be QUOT.COMP.CLOSE
("Once you are real, hurting is a small thing.")

shola lu thia shewo kelu. thia whila ki wepu. li rena ma shelomui miona thia ma phelora nila sholo.
QUOT.COMP COND 2SG true become. 2SG hair PFV go. RESTR REL NEG understand person 2SG NEG beautiful see QUOT.COMP.CLOSE
("By the time you are real, your fur is all gone. But only the people who do not understand see you as unbeautiful.")
```

**Notes:** the story's heart, and the transmutation's best gift arrived free: the Skin Horse's *"a long, long time"* is the habitual aspect. `lu lopia thia ro lothea` — if a child loves you as a habit, the way mornings happen — `thia shewo kelu`. Real is not an event in Phi; it is what the grammar calls `ro`. And the fable's mercy survives in the restrictive particle: only — `li` — those who do not understand can see the worn-bare as unlovely.

## The becoming

```
lue ha shero lopia nua tupiwa ro nulae.
ABL PROX night child COM rabbit HAB sleep.
(From that night, the child slept with the rabbit, every night.)

lo philo to wepu. tupiwa serao to kelu.
PL day PST go. rabbit old PST become.
(The days went. The rabbit grew shabby.)

shia whila to wepu. shia menoa whila to wepu.
3SG hair PST go. 3SG face hair PST go.
(His fur went. His whiskers went.)

lopia shola thia mia wilemo ma nai. thia shewo nai sholo to haolu.
child QUOT.COMP 2SG 1SG toy NEG be. 2SG true be QUOT.COMP.CLOSE PST speak.
(The child said: "You are not my toy. You are real.")

tupiwa siora to phaelo.
rabbit joy PST feel.
(The rabbit felt joy.)
```

**Notes:** the declaration scene is where the new coin meets its limit on purpose — `wilemo` exists precisely so the child can say the Rabbit has outgrown it. The clause-initial pronoun rule holds the line steady: `thia mia wilemo ma nai` can only mean *you are not my toy*, with `mia wilemo` safely in the predicate.

## The wild rabbits

```
mua thepalu wi waeshi tupiwa lue shelira to shua.
LOC garden two wild rabbit ABL forest PST come.
(In the garden, two wild rabbits came from the forest.)

shia shola wa thia po tupi sholo to thilou.
3SG QUOT.COMP Q 2SG POT jump QUOT.COMP.CLOSE PST inquire.
(They asked: "Can you jump?")

tupiwa to po ma tupi. tupiwa to ma lonae.
rabbit PST POT NEG jump. rabbit PST NEG respond.
(The rabbit could not jump. He did not answer.)

lo waeshi tupiwa kau shelira to rashelo.
PL wild rabbit ALL forest PST run.
(The wild rabbits ran back to the forest.)
```

**Notes:** the cruelest scene in the book, kept: the jumper who cannot jump, named by the very verb he fails. The wild rabbits are simply `waeshi tupiwa` — the coin covering both natures, as the story requires it to.

## The fire-sickness

```
lopia thero mokela to howela.
child fire sick PST receive.
(The child caught the fever.)

tupiwa nua shia mua nuwera to manolu. tupiwa wei shia thema to nai.
rabbit COM 3SG LOC bed PST stay. rabbit DAT 3SG guardian PST be.
(The rabbit stayed with him in the bed. The rabbit was his guardian.)

lopia towe to kelu.
child well PST become.
(The child became well.)

rena theala miona shola no lo serao wilemo thape sholo to haolu.
REL heal person QUOT.COMP IMP PL old toy burn QUOT.COMP.CLOSE PST speak.
(The one who heals said: "Burn the old toys.")

tupiwa mua molu to nai. tupiwa sonu to nai.
rabbit LOC container PST be. rabbit alone PST be.
(The rabbit was in a sack. The rabbit was alone.)

ta mirae phialu lue shia mirae to lepa.
one eye water ABL 3SG eye PST fall.
(One tear fell from his eye.)
```

**Notes:** scarlet fever is `thero mokela`, the fire-sickness — composition naming the disease by its burn — and the doctor is the same rank-free pattern as ever: `rena theala miona`, the one who heals, whose order is medical and terrible at once. And here, at last, is the oldest kenning in the compound registry doing the work it was coined for: `mirae phialu`, eye-water. The registry called it *the truest* compound. This is the scene that makes it so.

## The spring

```
mua rena mirae phialu to lepa lokue ta peloru pa thuroa.
LOC REL eye water PST fall place one flower INCH grow.
(In the place where the tear fell, a flower began to grow.)

wirae to shua.
spring PST come.
(Spring came.)

wirae wei theula miona shia shewo ka kelu.
spring DAT UNIV person 3SG true CAUS become.
(Spring made him real — to everyone.)

tupiwa to tupi.
rabbit PST jump.
(The rabbit jumped.)
```

**Notes:** the ruling, kept: no fairy was coined. From the tear a flower; from the flower, spring finishing what the child's love began — the world as the completing agent, exactly as the earth sowed the peoples at Babel. The inchoative `pa` catches the flower at its edge, the causative carries the transformation, and then the shortest sentence in the text pays off the coin: `tupiwa to tupi`. The jumper jumped.

## The seeing

```
wirae turema. lopia mua thepalu to wile.
spring return. child LOC garden PST play.
(Spring returned. The child was playing in the garden.)

ta tupiwa lue shelira to shua. shia lopia to sheru nila.
one rabbit ABL forest PST come. 3SG child PST slow see.
(A rabbit came from the forest. He looked at the child, slowly.)

lopia mena shia ha tupiwa to sano meno to ma sano.
child DECL.COMP 3SG PROX rabbit PST know DECL.COMP.CLOSE PST NEG know.
(The child never knew that he had known this rabbit.)

tupiwa theula thimu shewo to nai.
rabbit UNIV time true PST be.
(The rabbit was real, for all time.)
```

**Notes:** the quiet ending, one season later: recognition that never quite arrives, held in a mena-clause the child cannot close. The last line borrows the journal's oldest date — `theula thimu`, all time — for the only fact in the story that deserves it.

## What the transmutation changed

*Gap log: rabbit → `tupiwa` (coined on `tupi`, jump); toy → `wilemo` (coined on `wile`, play); velveteen → `wuloe wetha` (composed); sawdust → `wolea muhena` (composed); Skin Horse → `kori kalora`, the leather horse; Christmas → `shila holia`, the winter festival; whiskers → `menoa whila` (composed); scarlet fever → `thero mokela`, the fire-sickness; nursery → `lopia tholu`; the doctor → `rena theala miona`, the one who heals; Real → `shewo`, defined in the text by the Leather Horse, with "a long, long time" carried by the habitual `ro`; the nursery magic Fairy → no being coined — the tear, the flower, and spring complete the becoming, the world as agent per the Babel precedent; the Boy → `lopia`, child, ungendered as always. New words: two, for five and a half thousand of the original's.*
