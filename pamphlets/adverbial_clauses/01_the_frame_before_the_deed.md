# Part 1: The frame before the deed

Every adverbial frame in Phi has one shape:

`[relator] [dependent clause] [main clause]`

The relator opens the frame, the dependent clause fills it, and the dependent clause's own verb closes it. No closing word exists for these frames, and none is needed: when the dependent verb has sounded, the frame is complete, and the next word begins the main clause. This is the opposite arrangement from the complement frames, whose `meno`, `welo`, and `sholo` close them audibly; there, the boundary is a word, and here, the boundary is the frame's own final verb.

The set is closed at five: `pheo` (after), `phoe` (before), `lao` (because), `shai` (although), and `lila` (in order to). Canon settles the inventory, and no sixth relator is waiting in a drawer.

### Two look-alikes that are not relators

Two words keep ending up in this family by mistake, and each has its own home.

`lu` opens a condition, but it is a Slot 0 particle, not a clause relator, and its grammar shows the difference: a `lu` condition is one complete sentence with its own period, and the consequence is another. `lu pheralu lepa. lo mia mua womu meilo.` is two sentences. A relator frame and its main clause are one.

`tei` is a preposition, not a clause relator. It stands over a noun phrase naming an endpoint, and no clause hides inside it:

```
mia tei howai thole.
1SG UNTIL evening practice.
(I practice until evening.)
```

Evening is a noun, not an event with a verb. The phrase sits where adjuncts sit, and the sentence has one clause.

### Practice: sort the openers

Say what each opening word is: a clause relator, the Slot 0 condition, or a preposition over a noun phrase.

1. `lao pheralu to nai ruela wirua nai.`
2. `lu thia naphe. mia towe phaelo.`
3. `lo lopia tei howai wile.`
4. `lila mia shonela mia theo.`

Answers: 1 A clause relator: `lao` opens a reason clause whose final `nai` completes the frame before the main clause. 2 The Slot 0 condition: `lu` opens a complete sentence, and the consequence is a second sentence with its own period. 3 A preposition: `tei howai` is a phrase naming the endpoint of the children's playing, and the sentence has one clause. 4 A clause relator: `lila` opens the purpose, and `shonela` completes the frame before the reading begins.
