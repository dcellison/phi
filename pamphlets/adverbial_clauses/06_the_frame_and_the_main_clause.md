# Part 6: The frame and the main clause

A frame changes nothing about the clause it modifies. Once the dependent verb has closed the frame, the main clause begins with its full ordinary rights: subject first, adjunct phrases before the object, Slot 1 before the verb.

### The dependent clause is a whole clause

Inside the frame, the clause is not a reduced or infinitive thing. It carries its own tense, its own negation, its own particles:

```
lao mia to ma sheluo mia to ma shelomu.
BECAUSE 1SG PST NEG listen 1SG PST NEG understand.
(Because I did not listen, I did not understand.)
```

Two clauses, two independent Slot 1 stacks, and neither borrows from the other. When the frame's event and the main event sit in different times, say both times; the relator will not say either for you.

### The main clause keeps its shape

Adjunct phrases in the main clause stand where they always stand, before the object position, and a time phrase sits in its ordinary slot after the subject:

```
lila mia lo silero nila mia sui norawhi thalo.
PURP 1SG PL star see 1SG DUR dusk walk.
(In order to see the stars, I walk at dusk.)
```

The frame does not use up the sentence's ordinary machinery. Everything a plain sentence may carry, a framed sentence may carry too.

### Hearing the seam

With no closer, the seam between frame and main clause is the dependent verb followed by the main subject. Reading aloud, the seam is where a listener's parse turns over: the frame's last verb lands, and the next noun begins something new. The drill is simply to find it.

### Practice: find the seam

Mark where each frame ends and say what closes it.

1. `pheo melu to kamo lo mia to haolu.`
2. `shai pheralu lo peloru thuroa.`
3. `lila lo miona shelomu shia haolu.`

Answers: 1 The frame ends at `kamo`, its own verb; `lo mia` opens the main clause. 2 The frame ends at `pheralu`, because a noun-phrase concession has no verb to wait for; the plural marker on the flowers opens the main clause. 3 The frame ends at `shelomu`, and `shia` begins the speaking. In every case the boundary is audible without a closing word, which is exactly the design: the frame announces itself at its first word and completes itself at its last.
