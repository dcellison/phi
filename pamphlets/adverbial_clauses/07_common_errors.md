# Part 7: Common errors

Most errors in this system are English word order asking for a visa. It does not get one.

### Error 1: the trailing frame

**Wrong:** `*mia theo lila mia shonela.`

English says "I read in order to learn" with the purpose last, and the reflex follows the translation. Canon rules the postposed frame ungrammatical for every relator, purpose included: `lila mia shonela mia theo.` The frame speaks first or not at all.

### Error 2: `lao` where `thelao` belongs

**Wrong for the intended two thoughts:** one long sentence forcing a paragraph of evidence into a `lao` frame.

When the reason deserves its own sentence, give it one, and open the conclusion with `thelao`: `phialu sulae nai. thelao lopia wile.` The relator and the marker split one job by scope, and stuffing a discourse-sized reason into a clause-sized frame serves neither.

### Error 3: purpose awarded to an accident

**Wrong for the meaning:** `*lila ruela wirua nai pheralu to lepa.` intended as "It rained, so the path is wet."

`lila` claims intention, and the rain intended nothing. The wet path takes `lao` with the rain as reason, or two sentences with `thelao`. Grammatical shape cannot repair a false claim about aim.

### Error 4: a clause handed to `tei`

**Wrong:** `*mia tei lopia nulae thole.` intended as "I practice until the child sleeps."

`tei` is a preposition over a noun phrase, and it takes no clause. Name the endpoint as a time, `mia tei shero thole.`, or reframe the sequence with a relator: `pheo lopia nulae mia thole.` puts the falling asleep first and the practicing after it.

### Error 5: the swapped pair

**Wrong for the meaning:** `phoe melu to kamo lo mia to haolu.` intended as "After the friend arrived, we spoke."

That sentence is grammatical and means the opposite order: it makes the arrival the later event. `pheo` introduces the earlier event, `phoe` the later reference point. The frame names the event it holds, so ask which event sits inside it, not which English word came first.

### Error 6: the condition dressed as a frame

**Wrong:** `*lu pheralu lepa lo mia mua womu meilo.` as one sentence.

`lu` is Slot 0, not a relator, and its condition is a complete sentence: `lu pheralu lepa. lo mia mua womu meilo.` The five relators share one sentence with their main clause; the condition never does.

### Error 7: the freedom construction misread as purpose

**Wrong parse:** hearing `thia lila wepu ralu nai.` and waiting for a main clause.

None is coming. `lila wepu` names the respect in which the subject is free, and `ralu nai` completes the clause. The purpose frame and the freedom construction share one word; the arriving `ralu nai` tells you which house you are in.
