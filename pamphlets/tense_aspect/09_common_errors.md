# Part 9: Common errors

Every error in this system is an English habit asking Phi to hold its coat. The repairs are short, and each one teaches a rule worth keeping.

### Error 1: the unmarked past

**Wrong:** `*mia sileta nila.` intended as "I saw the sun."

An unmarked verb is present, always. A clause whose translation is past carries `to` on its own verb: `mia sileta to nila.` The particle is one syllable, and it is never optional; the timeline of every Phi sentence is exactly as marked.

### Error 2: `to` read as "finished"

**Wrong for the meaning:** treating `mia to theo.` as "I finished reading."

`to` gives direction, not closure. The neutral past leaves the view open, and completion is `ki`'s work: `mia to ki theo.` for the reading that stood complete at the past moment. When the finish matters, say the finish.

### Error 3: the English perfect mapped onto `to ki`

**Wrong for the meaning:** `mia to ki nila.` intended as "I have seen."

"Have seen" completes at the present, and the present is unmarked, so the form is bare `ki`: `mia ki nila.` The rule is one line: tense sets the reference moment, `ki` completes at it. Bare `ki`, have done; `to ki`, had done; `so ki`, will have done. English's three constructions are one particle wearing three tenses.

### Error 4: `si` handed a habit

**Wrong for the meaning:** `misheko si nulae.` intended as "the cat sleeps, as cats do."

That sentence is grammatical and means the cat is sleeping right now, mid-nap. The standing pattern takes `ro`: `misheko ro nulae.` `si` looks inside one occasion; `ro` looks across many. Ask which claim is yours before the particle leaves your mouth.

### Error 5: two views stacked

**Wrong:** `*lopia pa si wile.` intended as "the child begins to be playing."

The aspect rank seats one particle per clause. Two views take two clauses, each one clean: `lopia pa wile. lopia si wile.` The beginning is one claim, the flow is another, and the sequence of sentences carries what the stack cannot.

### Error 6: aspect before tense

**Wrong:** `*mia si to theo.`

The Slot 1 order is fixed: tense, then aspect. `mia to si theo.` Point first, then focus. The order is the same in all ten combinations, which is why part 7's table can be drilled to reflex; nothing in it ever rearranges.

### Error 7: `te` read as `ki`

**Wrong for the meaning:** taking `mia te theo.` as "I finished the book."

`te` says the reading ceased; the book may lie open at its middle. Completion is a separate claim with its own particle. A day can honestly contain both, `mia to te theo.` at dusk and `mia to ki theo.` when the last page turned, and Phi keeps the two honest by keeping them apart.
