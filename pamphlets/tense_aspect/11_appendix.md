# Appendix: quick reference

## The seven particles

| Particle | Gloss | Job |
|---|---|---|
| `to` | PST | places the event before the present moment |
| `so` | FUT | places the event after the present moment |
| `si` | IPFV | in progress, inside one occasion |
| `ki` | PFV | complete at the reference time tense supplies |
| `pa` | INCH | beginning |
| `te` | CESS | ceasing |
| `ro` | HAB | recurring across occasions |

An unmarked clause is present, its view unchosen. Tense stands first, aspect second, and the pair sits directly before the verb.

## The ten combinations

| | `si` | `ki` | `pa` | `te` | `ro` |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `to` | was doing | had done | began doing | stopped doing | did habitually then |
| `so` | will be doing | will have done | will begin doing | will stop doing | will do habitually |

## Working rules

- An unmarked verb is present; a clause whose translation is past or future carries `to` or `so` on its own verb.
- `to` and `so` give direction only; every further shape belongs to the aspect rank.
- Tense sets the reference moment and `ki` completes at it: bare `ki` have done, `to ki` had done, `so ki` will have done.
- `si` looks inside one occasion; `ro` looks across many; a habit takes `ro`, always.
- `te` says the event ceased and `ki` says it stands complete; when both are true, each takes its own clause.
- One tense and one view per clause; two views take two clauses.
- Time phrases and frequency phrases stand after the subject, and the particles keep their place beside the verb.
