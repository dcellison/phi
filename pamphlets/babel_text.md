# ta haoluma — the Babel text

*For decades, the conlang community's handshake has been the same: show us your Babel text. Genesis 11 is the story of how one speech became many, and nearly every constructed language has carried it. This is Phi's — the fourth text, and the first to require new vocabulary since the primer began: `haoluma` (language) and `thiwera` (scatter), both coined for it. It is a transmutation, not a translation, and it departs from Genesis deliberately: the notes say exactly where and why.*

---

## The story

### One speech

```
theula muila ta haoluma to phelu.
UNIV earth one language PST hold.
(The whole earth held one language.)

lo miona lue sorae thorui to wepu.
PL person from sun beginning PST go.
(The people journeyed from the east.)

shia wemo to hekawi. shia mua wemo to soki.
3SG plain PST find. 3SG LOC plain PST settle.
(They found a plain, and they settled there.)
```

**Notes:** the opening line is the new word's first sentence in the wild: `haoluma`, language — the whole verb *speak* grounded by `ma`. Phi has gone this long without a word for its own kind; the story about language diversity is the right doorstep to coin it on. "The east" is the compound the registry has always held: `sorae thorui`, the sun's beginning.

### The great village

```
shia shola no lo mueri kerou whea sholo to haolu.
3SG QUOT.COMP IMP PL clay stone make QUOT.COMP.CLOSE PST speak.
(They said: make clay-stones.)

shia roe thero lo mueri kerou to thape.
3SG INS fire PL clay stone PST burn.
(They burned the bricks with fire.)

shia whalo silawo to lorima.
3SG large village PST build.
(They built a great village.)

shia rena mua waero kamo raelu moru to lorima.
3SG REL LOC sky arrive tall wall PST build.
(They built a tall wall that reaches the sky.)

shia shola lo mia nomei whalo so nai sholo to haolu.
3SG QUOT.COMP PL 1SG name large FUT be QUOT.COMP.CLOSE PST speak.
(They said: our name will be great.)
```

**Notes:** no new words here, on purpose. Brick is `mueri kerou`, clay-stone — composition doing what a coin would. The city is `whalo silawo`, a village grown large: Phi's settlement vocabulary is deliberately human-scale, and the story is about what happens when the scale is exceeded. And there is no tower-word: the tower is a relative clause — `rena mua waero kamo raelu moru`, *a tall wall that arrives at the sky* — the grammar reaching upward one modifier at a time, which is how towers are built.

### The sowing

```
ta haoluma wei theula muila henoi to ma nai.
one language DAT UNIV earth ENOUGH PST NEG be.
(One language was not enough for the whole earth.)

muila phea lo thinoe lo miona to thiwera.
earth AS PL seed PL person PST scatter.
(The earth scattered the peoples like seeds.)

ta haoluma lo haoluma to kelu.
one language PL language PST become.
(The one language became many languages.)
```

**Notes:** here the transmutation shows its hand. In Genesis, the scattering is a punishment and the confusion of tongues a penalty. Phi cannot tell it that way honestly — the language has no vocabulary of domination, and its planting idiom already says *give seeds to the earth*. So the second coin does the turning: `thiwera`, scatter, opens on the seed-syllable of `thinoe` — and the peoples are not driven apart but sown, `phea lo thinoe`, like seeds. What Genesis frames as curse, Phi states as husbandry: one crop was not enough for so much ground.

### The name

```
silawo nomei lo haoluma nai.
village name PL language be.
(The village's name is Many-Languages.)
```

**Notes:** Hebrew hears *Babel* against *balal*, to confuse — the name is a pun on what happened there. Phi keeps the custom and drops the judgment: the place is named `lo haoluma`, the Many Languages, which is simply what it became.

### The gardens

```
theula haoluma thepalu nai.
UNIV language garden be.
(Every language is a garden.)

we ha haoluma thepalu nai.
ALSO PROX language garden be.
(This language, too, is a garden.)
```

**Notes:** the coda is Phi's own, with no source in Genesis — the sowing frame carried to its conclusion. If the peoples were scattered as seeds, then every language since is one of the gardens that grew. The last line is the first time Phi has ever referred to itself: `ha haoluma` — this language. It took four texts and nine hundred words for the language to say *I*.

## What the transmutation changed

*Gap log: language → `haoluma` (coined — speak, made whole; deliberately not the tongue: `lathoe` stays anatomy, refusing English's metaphor); scatter → `thiwera` (coined — broadcast wide, the seed-syllable inside it); brick → `mueri kerou` (composed); city → `whalo silawo` (composed — Phi's settlements stay human-scale, and the excess is the story); tower → a relative clause, not a noun; the divine agent → the earth itself, `muila`, acting as a sower rather than a judge; punishment → husbandry — the scattering as sowing, the diversity of tongues as gardens rather than confusion. The traditional reading is one verse away in any Bible; this is what the story says in a language that cannot call diversity a curse.*
