# Part 8: The interaction tables

Phi's particle grammar is closed. Its combinations follow the inventories and ordering rules already described; they do not wait for a corpus example to become grammatical. These tables gather the rules without treating unused combinations as invitations to invent new syntax.

### Table 1: Slot 0 forms

| Form | Reading |
|---|---|
| no particle | declarative assertion |
| `wa` | yes/no question |
| `no` | directive |
| `lu` | realis condition |
| `lu he` | irrealis or counterfactual condition |
| `su` | wish, hope, blessing, or prayer |
| `pi` | polite stance |
| `pi wa` | polite yes/no question |
| `pi no` | polite directive |
| any other multiple Slot 0 sequence | not licensed |

`he` exists only after `lu`. Politeness precedes the question or directive in the two documented combined forms. Slot 0 does not otherwise allow speakers to pile one utterance frame on another.

### Table 2: inside Slot 1

| Pattern | Rule |
|---|---|
| particles from different ranks | combine in fixed rank order when all meanings apply to one event |
| two particles from one rank | not licensed |
| `se ka` | the sole same-rank pair, in that order |
| `ka se` | not licensed |
| `po ma` | cannot; possibility or ability denied |
| `na ma` | must not; necessity applies to refraining |
| `ka ma` | causation denied; the caused event remains open |
| need not | expressed with the freedom construction, not reordered particles |

Tense and aspect combine compositionally: `to ki`, `so ki`, `to si`, `so si`, `to ro`, and `so ro` all follow the same order. The same rule extends across the remaining ranks. A sequence such as `to si ke po ma` is legal if one event truly needs every distinction.

### Table 3: Slot 0 over Slot 1

| Pattern | Example | Reading |
|---|---|---|
| question over a past clause | `wa thia to nila.` | Did you see? |
| directive over negation | `no ponu ma tapu.` | Do not close the door. |
| wish over future | `su shea so shua.` | May peace come. |
| realis condition with its own stack | `lu mia naphe. thia towe so nai.` | If I help, you will be well. |
| polite question | `pi wa thia po shua.` | Could you please come? |
| polite directive | `pi no mia naphe.` | Please help me. |

A licensed Slot 0 frame may contain an ordinary Slot 1 stack. The particles still make their usual claims. A negative directive is therefore a directive whose predicate is negated; it is not a new Slot 0 combination. A condition and its consequence remain separate clauses, each with its own Slot 1 choices.

### Table 4: inside Slot 2

| Pattern | Rule |
|---|---|
| `we` or `li` | one may occupy the widest relation on a phrase; they do not stack together |
| `ha` or `ra` | deixis precedes quantity, focus, degree, and the word |
| `lo` or a numeral or quantifier | one expression of quantity; `lo` does not accompany another quantity |
| `ko` | focus follows quantity and precedes an ordinary degree particle |
| `ru` or `mo` | degree stays next to the quality or action it modifies |
| `mo ko` | fixed superlative construction |
| `nu` + numeral | fixed ordinal construction |
| `ne` + honorific + name | name marker precedes the relationship marker |

### Practice: license or refuse

For each form, say whether it is licensed and give the rule.

1. `so ki`
2. `hi ke`
3. `pi su`
4. `ka po`
5. `po ka`
6. `se ka`
7. `ka se`
8. `no ponu ma tapu.`
9. `lo wi melu`
10. `we li ha melu`

Answers: 1 licensed, future reference time plus completion. 2 not licensed, two evidentials in one rank. 3 not licensed, not one of the documented Slot 0 combinations. 4 licensed, voice before modality. 5 not licensed, modality cannot precede voice. 6 licensed, the fixed passive-causative pair. 7 not licensed, the voice pair has one order. 8 licensed, a directive over a negated predicate. 9 not licensed, a numeral replaces `lo`. 10 not licensed, `we` and `li` do not share one phrase.
