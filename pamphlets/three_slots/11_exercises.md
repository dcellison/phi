## Answer key

### Part A

1 Slot 1. 2 Slot 0. 3 Slot 2. 4 Slot 1. 5 Slot 2. 6 Slot 0 as the second element of `lu he`. 7 Slot 1. 8 Slot 2.

### Part B

9. `mia ruela to ki nila.`
10. `nophi si se kealo.` Aspect precedes voice.
11. `thia so ho po naphe.` Tense precedes evidentiality, which precedes modality.
12. `mia lopia ka na shonela.` Voice precedes modality.
13. `lo mia lohau to ka ma nulae.` The sentence denies causation and leaves the dog's sleeping open.
14. `su shia wei thepalu phialu ro loa.` The wish frames a habitual giving of water.

### Part C

15. `shia to ke wepu.` Tense precedes evidentiality.
16. `mia lopia ka po wile.` Voice precedes modality.
17. `thia so ma shua.` Tense precedes negation.
18. `suliwa hi nai. suliwa ti nai.` Two claimed sources require two clauses.

### Part D

19. Licensed. `to te` presents an ending in the past.
20. Not licensed. The voice pair is `se ka`, in that order.
21. Licensed. `pi no` is a polite directive.
22. Licensed. It marks an assumed source, followed by denied possibility or ability.
23. Not licensed. `si` and `ro` compete for the aspect rank.
24. Licensed. The passive-causative voice pair precedes negation.
25. Not licensed. `pi su` is not a documented Slot 0 combination.
26. Licensed. Slot 0 frames a directive whose Slot 1 predicate is negated.

### Part E

27. The sentence is grammatical but does not say who gave the water. If the speaker is answering about their own act, `mia wei thepalu phialu to ma loa.` responds more directly. If no one can identify the giver and only the water matters, the passive may be appropriate.
28. Use `misheko ro nulae.` for a recurring pattern. Keep `si` for one nap in progress.
29. Use two claims: `mia wi lipha powea phelu. henoi ma nai.` The numeral supplies quantity, and the second sentence states insufficiency.
30. `mia hi remo.` is grammatical if direct perception of the thought is deliberately contrasted with another source. With no such contrast, `mia remo.` says what the context requires without adding an evidential claim.

### Part F

31. `pi wa thia ha lo melu so nila.`
32. `suliwa hi nai. suliwa ti nai.`
33. `lu mia shelu theo. mia so shelomu.`
34. `mia nophi kealo. nophi se kealo.`
