# lothea thole — The Practice of Love

*A transmutation of the Karaniya Metta Sutta into Phi, and the language's
first complete text. This is a transmutation, not a translation: each
verse's meaning is rebuilt from Phi concepts (see
`documents/language_guide.md` §6). Where Phi lacks a word or a doctrine
sits outside its worldview, the adaptation is recorded honestly in the
gap log at the end.*

*The source is the Pali Karaniya Metta Sutta (Sn 1.8), rendered from
its common English translations.*

---

## The text

### Verse 1 — The one who seeks peace

> *This is what should be done by one who is skilled in goodness and
> who knows the path of peace: let them be able and upright, gentle in speech, humble, and not conceited.*

```
[rena shea ruela sano] miona welao na thole.
[REL peace path know] person good NEC practice.
(The one who knows the path of peace must practice goodness.)

su shia theali nela wero nai.
OPT 3SG fitting COORD honest be.
(May they be capable and honest.)

su shia moli haolu.
OPT 3SG gentle speak.
(May they speak gently.)

su shia motu nai. shia thoru ma nai.
OPT 3SG humble be. 3SG proud NEG be.
(May they be humble. They are not proud.)
```

**Notes:** The headless-feeling relative `[rena shea ruela sano] miona`
("the person who knows the path of peace") opens the whole text with a
pre-nominal relative clause: the sutta's addressee announced before
they are named, in perfect modifier-first order. The optative `su`
carries the sutta's "let them / may they" mood natively.

### Verse 2 — Contentment

> *Contented and easily satisfied, unburdened, peaceful and calm,
> wise and skillful.*

```
su shia nuloe nai. su shia henoi sano.
OPT 3SG content be. OPT 3SG ENOUGH know.
(May they be content. May they know enough.)

su shia shena nela phaelu nai. su shia phue nai.
OPT 3SG calm COORD peaceful be. OPT 3SG wise be.
(May they be calm and peaceful. May they be wise.)
```

**Notes:** `shia henoi sano` ("may they know enough(ness)") uses the
quantifier `henoi` (enough) as a knowable object. Sufficiency as a
thing one can *know* is exactly the teaching, and Phi says it in three
words.

### Verse 3 — The wish

> *Let them not do the slightest thing that the wise would later
> reprove. And let them wish: in gladness and in safety, may all
> beings be at ease.*

```
su shia [rena lo phue peloma nila] thena ma phoa.
OPT 3SG [REL PL wise harmful see] thing NEG do.
(May they not do the thing the wise see as harmful.)

su theula [rena lima nai] siora phaelo.
OPT UNIV [REL alive be] joy feel.
(May all that lives feel joy.)

su theula [rena lima nai] towe nai.
OPT UNIV [REL alive be] well be.
(May all that lives be well.)
```

**Notes:** Phi has no word for "beings," and needs none: the headless
relative `[rena lima nai]` ("that which is alive") quantified by
`theula` covers every living thing without carving the world into
categories. This formula recurs as the sutta's refrain.

### Verses 4–5 — Omitting none

> *Whatever living beings there may be — weak or strong, great or
> small, seen or unseen, near or far, born and yet to be born — may
> all beings be at ease.*

```
welua sola kema. whalo sola thiku.
fragile DISJ strong. large DISJ small.
(Fragile or strong. Large or small.)

[rena se nila] sola [rena se ma nila].
[REL PASS see] DISJ [REL PASS NEG see].
(The seen or the unseen.)

noshi sola wuero.
near DISJ far.
(Near or far.)

[rena lima nai] nela [rena wea lioru shua].
[REL alive be] COORD [REL toward life come].
(Those who are alive and those coming toward life.)

su theula [rena lima nai] towe nai.
OPT UNIV [REL alive be] well be.
(May all that lives be well.)
```

**Notes:** "Born and yet to be born" becomes `[rena wea lioru shua]`
("those coming toward life"), routing around the missing word for
"birth" through the preposition `wea` (toward). The passive `se` builds
"the seen and the unseen" as headless relatives.

### Verse 6 — No deception, no contempt

> *Let none deceive another, nor despise any being in any state; let
> none through anger or ill-will wish harm upon another.*

```
su mawha miona wei shia peshu.
OPT NONE person DAT 3SG lie.
(May no person lie to another.)

su mawha miona shia thiku nila.
OPT NONE person 3SG small see.
(May no one see another as small.)

su mawha miona lue korua thero wei shia peloma pula.
OPT NONE person ABL heart fire DAT 3SG harmful wish.
(May no one, from the fire of the heart, wish harm upon another.)
```

**Notes:** Deception uses the dedicated verb `peshu` (lie). Contempt is "seeing another as small"
(`thiku nila`), which names the act more precisely than "despise" does.
Anger, which has no Phi word, becomes `korua thero`, *heart-fire*: a
compound that observes the feeling without condemning the one who
feels it. The new ablative `lue` marks anger as a *source* one acts
from, not an identity.

### Verse 7 — The boundless heart

> *Even as a mother protects with her life her child, her only child,
> so with a boundless heart should one cherish all living beings.*

```
phea [phao ta lopia shorupo].
AS [parent one child protect].
(As a parent protects their only child.)

su thia [mawha norui] korua phelu.
OPT 2SG [no boundary] heart hold.
(May you hold a heart of no boundary.)

su thia theula [rena lima nai] theama.
OPT 2SG UNIV [REL alive be] care.
(May you care for all that lives.)
```

**Notes:** `mawha norui korua` ("a no-boundary heart") builds
"boundless" from the quantifier `mawha` (none) and `norui` (boundary),
with the possessor-first pattern making it a heart *of* no boundary.
The sutta's most famous image survives intact in nine syllables.

### Verse 8 — Radiating in all directions

> *Radiating kindness over the entire world: upwards to the skies,
> downwards to the depths, outwards and unbounded, freed from hatred
> and ill-will.*

```
su thia wei theula muila lothea loa.
OPT 2SG DAT UNIV earth love give.
(May you give love to the whole earth.)

wei leo waero. wei phou nulo. wei theula noa.
DAT ABOVE sky. DAT BELOW deep. DAT UNIV position.
(To the sky above. To the deep below. To every place.)

su thia lue korua thero ralu nai.
OPT 2SG ABL heart fire free be.
(May you be free from the fire of the heart.)
```

**Notes:** The three directional fragments are verbless: poetry in
prepositions, each relation announced before its object. "Freed from
hatred" reuses verse 6's `korua thero` (heart-fire), now with `ralu`
(free) and the ablative `lue`: freedom *from* the fire, not war
against it.

### Verse 9 — Sustaining the practice

> *Whether standing or walking, seated or lying down, free from
> drowsiness, one should sustain this recollection. This is said to
> be the sublime abiding.*

```
thia whano sola thalo sola meilo sola therilu.
2SG stand DISJ walk DISJ sit DISJ rest.
(Whether you stand or walk or sit or rest.)

su thia ha thesua thenoki.
OPT 2SG PROX mindful sustain.
(May you sustain this mindfulness.)

ha shea menui nai.
PROX peace dwell be.
(This is the dwelling of peace.)
```

**Notes:** "Lying down" has no Phi word; `therilu` (rest) covers the
posture's meaning. "The sublime abiding" (brahmavihāra) becomes
`shea menui` (the dwelling of peace), using `menui` (dwell) as a
noun, as Phi's analytic grammar freely allows.

### Verse 10 — Clear seeing

> *By not holding to fixed views, the pure-hearted one, having clarity
> of vision, being freed from all sense desires, is not born again
> into this world.*

```
[shiloa korua] mawha remo kire phelu.
[pure heart] NONE think shape hold.
(The pure heart holds no fixed shape of thought.)

shia nuwi nila.
3SG clear see.
(They see clearly.)

shia shea ruela thole.
3SG peace path practice.
(They walk the path of peace.)
```

**Notes:** "Fixed views" becomes `remo kire` (thought-shapes), held
by no one with a pure heart. The final line adapts rather than
translates: the rebirth doctrine requires vocabulary (and metaphysics)
Phi does not carry, so the text closes where it began, on `shea ruela`,
the path of peace. The circle is deliberate.

## The complete text

```
lothea thole

[rena shea ruela sano] miona welao na thole.
su shia theali nela wero nai. su shia moli haolu.
su shia motu nai. shia thoru ma nai.

su shia nuloe nai. su shia henoi sano.
su shia shena nela phaelu nai. su shia phue nai.

su shia [rena lo phue peloma nila] thena ma phoa.
su theula [rena lima nai] siora phaelo.
su theula [rena lima nai] towe nai.

welua sola kema. whalo sola thiku.
[rena se nila] sola [rena se ma nila]. noshi sola wuero.
[rena lima nai] nela [rena wea lioru shua].
su theula [rena lima nai] towe nai.

su mawha miona wei shia peshu.
su mawha miona shia thiku nila.
su mawha miona lue korua thero wei shia peloma pula.

phea [phao ta lopia shorupo].
su thia [mawha norui] korua phelu.
su thia theula [rena lima nai] theama.

su thia wei theula muila lothea loa.
wei leo waero. wei phou nulo. wei theula noa.
su thia lue korua thero ralu nai.

thia whano sola thalo sola meilo sola therilu.
su thia ha thesua thenoki.
ha shea menui nai.

[shiloa korua] mawha remo kire phelu.
shia nuwi nila.
shia shea ruela thole.
```

## Gap log

Concepts the lexicon could not express directly, and how the
transmutation handled each:

| Concept | Handling | Follow-up? |
|---|---|---|
| anger / hatred | `korua thero` (heart-fire), compositional metaphor | Canonized in `documents/compounds.md` |
| beings | `theula [rena lima nai]` (all that is alive) | No word needed: the relative clause is better than a noun |
| born / birth | `wea lioru shua` (coming toward life) | Resolved: `thowia` (born) exists; the sutta's phrase stands as chosen poetry |
| deceive / lie | `peshu` (lie), the dedicated verb | Resolved |
| despise | `thiku nila` (see as small) | Canonized in `documents/compounds.md` |
| safety / safe | folded into `towe nai` (be well) + `shorupo` (protect) | Resolved: `shurano` (safe) and `pukea` (danger) exist |
| lie down (posture) | `therilu` (rest) | Resolved: `ruemi` (lie down) exists; the sutta's `therilu` stands |
| fixed view / opinion | `remo kire` (thought-shape) | Canonized in `documents/compounds.md` |
| sublime abiding | `shea menui` (dwelling of peace) | Transmutation, intentional |
| rebirth | omitted; text closes on the path of peace | Doctrinal adaptation, recorded here deliberately |

## What the corpus test showed

1. **The grammar held.** Every construction needed (optatives,
   headless relatives, passives inside relatives, disjunctive lists,
   an as-clause, ablative sources, quantified abstractions) already
   existed. No grammar gaps were found.
2. **Headless relatives are Phi's superpower.** `[rena lima nai]`
   (that which lives) and `[rena se ma nila]` (the unseen) do work
   English needs whole noun phrases for.
3. **The emotion domain is the thinnest.** Anger, safety, and grief-
   adjacent vocabulary required workarounds; the nature and wisdom
   domains needed none.
4. **Compositional transmutation beat coinage three times.**
   Heart-fire, seeing-as-small, and thought-shapes are more precise
   than the English words they replace: evidence for the
   transmutation doctrine.
