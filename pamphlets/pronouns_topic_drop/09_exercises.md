# Part 9: Exercises

Answers follow at the end. Before checking any form, say aloud who acts, who receives, and which words the listener already holds.

## Part A: Name the role

For each sentence, name the role of every pronoun.

1. `thia mia nila.`
2. `mia lo thia whaline.`
3. `thia wei mia shua.`
4. `mia lohau welao nai.`
5. `lo mia lo shia nila.`

## Part B: Build the clause

Render each English sentence in Phi.

6. You know yourself.
7. We heal ourselves.
8. They know one another.
9. You and I love each other.
10. That tree is very large.
11. I am here.

## Part C: Repair or defend

Some items are ungrammatical, some are grammatical but claim the wrong thing for the stated intent, and some are fine. Judge each.

12. `mia lohau nai.` intended as "I have a dog."
13. `*mia wiso nila.` intended as "we see each other."
14. `lo mia miso nila.` intended as "we see each other."
15. `*lo ha melu` intended as "these friends."
16. `mia shelu theo. shelomu.` as a two-sentence account.

## Part D: Lighten or restore

Apply topic-drop where it is safe, and restore the subject where it is not.

17. `mia shelu theo. mia shelomu.` Lighten it.
18. `shai shelira thiku nai shelira mioru nai.` Say the subject once.
19. `melu to shua. lopia to shua.` Now say that the child sang.
20. `melu to shua. to therilu.` Safe or not? Say why.

## Part E: Translate

21. `su lo mia lo ha ru thape.`
22. `mia nela thia wiso lothea.`
23. `rena mia to nila peloru mioru nai. thuroa.`
24. `lo mia wepu. thia ma wepu.`

---

## Answer key

### Part A

1. `thia` subject, `mia` object: you see me. 2. `mia` subject, `lo thia` plural object: I thank you all. 3. `thia` subject, `mia` object of the preposition `wei`: you come to me. 4. `mia` possessor inside the subject phrase: my dog is good, and the reading is unambiguous because "I am a good dog" would order the words `mia welao lohau nai.` 5. `lo mia` plural subject, `lo shia` plural object: we see them, the seam falling where the subject phrase ends.

### Part B

6. `thia miso sano.` 7. `lo mia miso theala.` 8. `lo shia wiso sano.` 9. `mia nela thia wiso lothea.` 10. `ra shiro ru whalo nai.` 11. `mia ha nai.`

### Part C

12. Wrong for the meaning: it says "I am a dog." Ownership takes `phelu`: `mia lohau phelu.` 13. Ungrammatical: the reciprocal needs a plural subject. `lo mia wiso nila.` 14. Grammatical but the mirror, not the meeting: it says we see ourselves. `lo mia wiso nila.` 15. Ungrammatical for the meaning: before a noun the deictic leads. `ha lo melu.` 16. Fine, defend it: the first sentence establishes `mia`, the second drops it while it remains recoverable. This is the pattern working exactly as designed.

### Part D

17. `mia shelu theo. shelomu.` Established one period ago, recoverable still: the drop is free. 18. `shai shelira thiku nai mioru nai.` The frame establishes the forest, and the second `shelira` was the one word the sentence could spare. 19. `lopia to meliho.` Two referents stand live, so the singer is named in full. 20. Safe: one referent is established and no rival has entered, so the resting lands on the friend without hesitation.

### Part E

21. Let us burn these thoroughly. `lo ha` points at the bricks with no noun needed. 22. You and I love each other: coordination builds the pair, and `wiso` carries the love between them. 23. The flower that I saw is beautiful. It grows: the relative clause builds the subject once, and the next sentence needs only a verb. 24. We go. You do not go: the contrast clause draws the line that `lo mia` alone leaves open.
