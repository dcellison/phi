# Appendix: quick reference

### The number words

| Phi | Value | Class |
|---|---|---|
| `mu` | 0 | base numeral |
| `ta` | 1 | base numeral |
| `wi` | 2 | base numeral |
| `shao` | 3 | scale unit (three-group) |
| `phoi` | 9 | scale unit (nine-group) |
| `lau` | 27 | scale unit (twenty-seven-group) |
| `rei` | 81 | scale unit (eighty-one-group) |

Composition: largest unit first, digits `ta` or `wi` before each scale, units last, zero places omitted, never a remainder of three, and never a repeated scale. `wi phoi ta shao ta` = 18 + 3 + 1 = 22. The exact range is 0-242; the maximum is `wi rei wi lau wi phoi wi shao wi`. Exact values from 243 upward remain source material outside the Phi passage. A numeral or quantifier already marks quantity and never combines with plural `lo`.

### The four natures

| Classifier | Gloss | Covers | Rule of thumb |
|---|---|---|---|
| `himo` | HUM.CLF | people | explicitly categorizes counted people |
| `lipha` | LIFE.CLF | living beings, living parts, life in waiting | the egg, the ear, the seed |
| `themo` | THING.CLF | the detached and the crafted | the plank, the bowl, the carved ear |
| `nophe` | ABST.CLF | concepts, time, events | the year, the storm-as-event, the walk |

Always optional; nature-*now* decides when a classifier is chosen, and declining to classify is the honest form of not knowing. Foregrounding a category is not a guarantee of regard.

### Exact, about, and beyond

| Form | Means |
|---|---|
| `ta shao wi powea` | five eggs, counted |
| `shao powea` | some three eggs |
| `phoi miona` | nine or so people |
| `lau shiro` | many trees |
| `rei silero` | stars on roughly an eighty-one-group scale; a great many stars |
| `henoi sulopa` | enough soup for the context under discussion |

Quantifier row: `theula` (UNIV), `sheloi` (MANY), `shelami` (MOST), `soli` (SOME), `phina` (FEW), `theli` (EACH), `wheli` (ANY), `mawha` (NONE), `henoi` (ENOUGH). The question: `wia` (HOW MANY), `wia himo miona` (how many people?).

### Position

`nu` (ORD) + cardinal + noun: `nu ta shao philo`, the third day. Position as topic: `[cardinal] noa`, `lopia mua wi noa nai.` An ordinal states position; who set the order, and what follows from it, are separate facts.

### The five verbs of arithmetic

| Verb | Operation | Pattern |
|---|---|---|
| `sholei` | gather (+) | `wi nela ta shao sholei. ta shao wi kelai.` |
| `leiro` | release (−) | `ta phoi nela ta shao leiro. wi shao kelai.` |
| `welura` | spread (×) | `ta shao nela wi welura. wi shao kelai.` |
| `phanoi` | portion (÷) | `ta phoi nela ta shao phanoi. ta shao kelai.` |
| `kelai` | result or equality | closes a result clause; pure equality also uses `nela` between quantities |

`nela` is mandatory between operands, in comparison as in arithmetic: `[quantity 1] nela [quantity 2] sharoi` (exceed) or `thenoi` (fall short), magnitude only, never worth. Addition and subtraction require the same kind of quantity. Multiplication takes a counted quantity plus a bare factor. Division takes a total plus recipients or groups; the quotient inherits the total's kind, and any remainder is stated separately, a fact with a future. Classifiers remain optional. Phi has no negative or fractional numeral syntax.

### The questions of honest counting

*Do I know it exactly? Would the difference change what anyone does? Is it mine to give?* Yeses all the way down: count, and pay the length gladly. Otherwise `shao`, `soli`, or `henoi` may already be the whole truth, and the sentence gets shorter as it gets truer. An exact numeral, an estimate, a quantifier, or a source record can each be the accurate choice.
