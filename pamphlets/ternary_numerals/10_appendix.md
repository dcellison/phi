# Appendix: quick reference

### The number words

| Phi | Value | Class |
|---|---|---|
| `mu` | 0 | base numeral |
| `ta` | 1 | base numeral |
| `wi` | 2 | base numeral |
| `shao` | 3 | scale unit (three-group) |
| `phoi` | 9 | scale unit (nine-group) |
| `lau` | 27 | scale unit (twenty-seven-group) |
| `rei` | 81 | scale unit (eighty-one-group) |

Composition: largest unit first, digits `ta`/`wi` before each unit, remainder last, never a remainder of three, never a repeated unit. `wi phoi ta shao ta` = 18 + 3 + 1 = 22.

### The four natures

| Classifier | Gloss | Covers | Rule of thumb |
|---|---|---|---|
| `himo` | HUM.CLF | people | counting most in need of manners |
| `lipha` | LIFE.CLF | living beings, living parts, life in waiting | the egg, the ear, the seed |
| `themo` | THING.CLF | the detached and the crafted | the plank, the bowl, the carved ear |
| `nophe` | ABST.CLF | concepts, time, events | the year, the storm-as-event, the walk |

Always optional; nature-*now* decides; declining to classify is the honest form of not knowing.

### Exact, about, and beyond

| Form | Means |
|---|---|
| `ta shao wi powea` | five eggs, counted |
| `shao powea` | some three eggs |
| `phoi miona` | nine or so people |
| `lau shiro` | many trees |
| `rei silero` | countless stars |
| `henoi sulopa` | enough soup, the only quantity that mattered |

Quantifier row: `theula` (UNIV), `sheloi` (MANY), `shelami` (MOST), `soli` (SOME), `phina` (FEW), `theli` (EACH), `wheli` (ANY), `mawha` (NONE), `henoi` (ENOUGH). The question: `wia` (HOW MANY), `wia himo miona` (how many people?).

### Position

`nu` (ORD) + cardinal + noun: `nu ta shao philo`, the third day. Position as topic: `[cardinal] noa`, `lopia mua wi noa nai.` Ordinals order things and times; ordering people is for lines that are truly just lines.

### The five verbs of arithmetic

| Verb | Operation | Pattern |
|---|---|---|
| `sholei` | gather (+) | `wi ta shao sholei. ta shao wi kelai.` |
| `leiro` | release (−) | `ta phoi ta shao leiro. wi shao kelai.` |
| `welura` | spread (×) | `ta shao wi welura. wi shao kelai.` |
| `phanoi` | portion (÷) | `ta phoi ta shao phanoi. ta shao kelai.` |
| `kelai` | equals | closes every calculation as its own clause |

Real operands share one classifier; bare mathematics drops them. Remainders are facts with futures, not defects.

### The two questions of honest counting

Before any exact number: *would exactness change what anyone does?* and *is the exact number mine to give?* Two yeses: count. Otherwise: `shao`, `soli`, `henoi`, the sentence gets shorter as it gets truer.
