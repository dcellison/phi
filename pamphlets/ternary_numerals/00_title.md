# Counting in Phi: the ternary numerals and the four natures

## A practice companion to the manual

Phi counts in threes, with three digit words, four scale words, and four optional words for classifying what is counted. The doctrine belongs to the manual (chapter 12 owns the teaching, and `documents/grammar/numeral_reference.md` is the canonical working reference), so this pamphlet supplies practice in reading, producing, estimating, classifying, and calculating without promising a universal learning speed.

Two skills interleave throughout. The first is structural: composing and decomposing quantities on the ternary scale within Phi's exact range of 0-242. The second is contextual: choosing among an exact numeral, a bare scale estimate, a quantifier such as `sheloi` (many) or `henoi` (enough), and separately preserved source material. No one choice is inherently more honest; accuracy means matching the form to what is known and what the situation requires.

By the end of this pamphlet, you will:

- Read and produce quantities through eight as a foundation, work through twenty-six, and recognize the extended exact range through 242 as needed
- Choose among `himo`, `lipha`, `themo`, and `nophe` by the nature-now rule, including the edge cases
- Say *about*, *many*, *a great many*, and *enough*, and explain when an estimate or exact source record is appropriate
- Mark position with `nu`, date a journal, and order a story's days
- Do integer arithmetic in full sentences with the mandatory `nela` boundary and the five verbs
- Count a market day from eggs to ideas while treating classifiers as meaningful options rather than guarantees of respect

This pamphlet assumes the particle system (manual ch9) and basic noun phrases (ch11). It is a companion to manual Part IV, chapter 12, and to the numeral reference.
