# Part 4: Position (`nu`)

Quantity says how many; position says which one. The Slot 2 particle `nu` (ORD) converts any number to a position, standing before it exactly where modifier-first predicts: announce *position-ness*, then the number, then the thing.

### The pattern

```
nu ta lopia
ORD one child
(the first child)

nu wi philo
ORD two day
(the second day)

nu ta shao nophi
ORD one three-group story
(the third story)
```

The number inside is ordinary ternary: `nu ta shao` is *third* because `ta shao` is three. Everything Part 2 built transfers: `nu wi phoi ta` is nineteenth, if a nineteenth ever matters, and the length of saying it is the language's gentle opinion about whether it does. Exact ordinals share the cardinal range through 242; larger exact positions remain source material outside the Phi passage.

### Dating the days

The journal practice (manual ch23) dates entries ordinally, and it is the most natural ordinal drill there is:

```
nu ta shao philo.
ORD one three-group day.
(The third day.)
```

A week of journal dates, aloud: `nu ta philo. nu wi philo. nu ta shao philo. nu ta shao ta philo. nu ta shao wi philo. nu wi shao philo. nu wi shao ta philo.` First day through seventh. Then remember the manual's counsel: date by count *when the count matters*, and by the day's own nature when it does not: `pheralu philo`, a rain-day, is also a date.

### Position as a thing: `noa`

When position itself is the topic (not a marker on a noun but the subject of discussion) the content word `noa` (position) serves compositionally:

```
lopia mua wi noa nai.
child LOC two position be.
(The child is in the second position.)
```

`nu` marks; `noa` names. The working distinction: if English wants *second X*, reach for `nu`; if it wants *the second position* as a place or rank to discuss, reach for `noa`. A rank in Phi is a place in a line; who set the line, and what standing in it costs, are the questions the word leaves open.

### Ordinals and classifiers together

Both can appear, each doing its own announcing, in their own slots:

```
nu ta himo phemi
ORD one HUM.CLF guest
(the first guest — position, then nature, then person)
```

The stack reads outward-in, like everything in Phi: which one, what kind, who. Rare in speech, precise when wanted: the layered form belongs to formal welcome and careful record, and the bare `nu ta phemi` covers the ordinary day.

### Drill: order the story

The velveteen story runs in eighteen scenes; the journal runs in dated days; the market runs in stalls. Produce, aloud: the fifth scene (an event, or a stretch of story? decide and defend), the sixth day, the second trader, the eighth egg, the first storm of the year. Answers and the classifier reasoning in Part 9: the egg and the storm are the instructive two.
