# pronouns

> pronouns are words used in place of nouns to avoid repetition or to refer back to nouns previously mentioned in a sentence or discourse. they represent persons, animals, things, or concepts and can function as subjects, objects, possessives, or reflexives in sentences. examples include "he," "she," "it," "they," "me," "you," "himself," "ourselves," and "whose." pronouns help to make sentences more concise and fluent by replacing specific nouns with more general terms.

## introduction to phi pronouns

phi pronouns represent a deliberate exception to the language's otherwise strict 
phonotactic patterns. unlike other parts of speech that follow specific consonant-vowel 
structures, pronouns maintain simpler, more compact forms due to their extremely high 
frequency in everyday speech.

the phi pronoun system is intentionally minimal, with only three core forms: `mia` (first 
person), `thi` (second person), and `sha` (third person). this simplicity eliminates 
the gender distinctions found in many natural languages, avoiding unnecessary 
complexity and potential social issues. the third person pronoun `sha` refers to all 
entities regardless of gender or animacy.

plurality in pronouns is achieved through the standard particle `lo`, which precedes 
the pronoun to create forms like `lo mia` (we) and `lo thi` (you all). this follows 
phi's consistent approach to pluralization across all nominal elements.

while these pronouns do not conform to the phonotactic patterns that distinguish nouns, 
verbs, adjectives, and other parts of speech, their exceptional status is justified 
by both practical considerations (frequency of use favors brevity) and linguistic 
naturalness (pronouns often maintain irregular forms across natural languages).

these pronoun forms can be combined with various particles to express grammatical 
relationships. for example, `si mia` explicitly marks the pronoun as subject, `na thi` 
marks the second person as object, and `ma sha` provides emphasis on the third person.

| phi word | english translation | function   |
| -------- | ------------------- | ---------- |
| mia      | me                  | 1st person |
| thi      | you                 | 2nd person |
| sha      | it                  | 3rd person |

| form     | 1st person | 2nd person | 3rd person |
| -------- | ---------- | ---------- | ---------- |
| singular | mia        | thi        | sha        |
| plural   | lo mia     | lo thi     | lo sha     |
