# phi vocabulary reference guide by semantic domain

> comprehensive organization of phi's 1,075+ defined words into semantic domains for 
> systematic learning and quick reference. words are grouped by meaning and context 
> rather than part of speech to facilitate natural language acquisition.

## overview

**total vocabulary**: 1,075+ words across 12 parts of speech
- **content words**: 798 (nouns: 389, verbs: 171, adjectives: 133, adverbs: 105)
- **function words**: 277 (particles: 45, prepositions: 53, determiners: 31, conjunctions: 33, classifiers: 37, numbers: 25, interjections: 53)

**organization principle**: semantic domains group related concepts across parts of speech
**usage context**: suitable for all proficiency levels from basic through advanced

## reference notes

### phonotactic patterns by part of speech

**quick identification guide**
- **nouns**: [C/F][V/P][F][P] - longest, most complex patterns
- **verbs**: [F][V][C][V] - start with fricative digraph + consonant
- **adjectives**: [C][V][F][V] - consonant + vowel + fricative + vowel
- **adverbs**: [C][V][C][V][C][V] - three syllables, no fricatives
- **particles**: [C][V] - shortest, simplest patterns

### semantic domain cross-references

many words appear in multiple domains due to semantic overlap:
- color words span physical properties and aesthetic concepts
- body parts connect to physical processes and spatial concepts
- temporal concepts overlap with narrative and logical structures
- social relationships intersect with communication and emotions

### learning progression recommendations

1. **basic**: natural world, body, family, basic actions (domains 1-3)
2. **intermediate**: time, space, objects, mental processes (domains 4-7)
3. **advanced**: work, culture, abstract concepts, logic (domains 8-12)

**total organized vocabulary**: 1,075+ words systematically categorized for efficient learning and reference
