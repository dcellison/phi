## coordination: connecting equal ideas

phi uses coordinating conjunctions to link ideas of equal importance:

### basic coordination patterns

| conjunction | pattern | meaning | usage |
|-------------|---------|---------|-------|
| `nene` | [C][V][C][V] | and | addition |
| `tupo` | [C][V][C][V] | but | contrast |
| `wiho` | [C][V][C][V] | or | alternative |
| `huwa` | [C][V][C][V] | so | result |

### simple coordination

```
mia nuthui phamo nene thi siwhea phose
me  pebble like  and  you house  see
I like pebbles and you see houses

phiato mipho siwhea tupo thueta pisha
this   blue  house  but  that   soft
This house is blue but that one is soft

thi wheisha phose wiho mia phethoa   phamo
you water   see   or   me  vegetable like
You see water or I like vegetables
```

### complex coordination with particles

```
mia li  nuthui phamo nene su  siwhea phose
me  PST pebble like  and  FUT house  see
I liked pebbles and will see houses

thi mo  teshe phola tupo mia me  napine  whona
you more good walk  but  me  NEG quickly look
You walk better but I don't look quickly

phiato hashe siwhea mipho huwa mia phamo
this   green house  blue  so   me  like
This house is green and blue so I like it
```

## subordination: showing relationships

subordinating conjunctions create dependent clauses that modify main ideas:

### causal relationships

| conjunction | meaning | example context |
|-------------|---------|-----------------|
| `renu` | because | showing reasons |
| `tura` | since | temporal/causal |
| `matu` | after | temporal sequence |
| `pimo` | before | temporal sequence |

```
mia siwhea phamo renu    teshe mipho
me  house  like  because good  blue
I like the house because it's good and blue

thi pimo   phola tura  mia nuthui phose
you before walk  since me  pebble see
You walked before since I saw pebbles

mia matu  wheisha phose nuthui phamo
me  after water   see   pebble like
After I see water, I like pebbles
```

### conditional relationships

| conjunction | meaning | usage |
|-------------|---------|-------|
| `wetu` | if | conditions |
| `supi` | whether | indirect questions |
| `wane` | when | temporal conditions |

```
wetu thi hashe  nuthui phose mia phamo
if   you green  pebble see   me  like
If you see green pebbles, I like them

mia me  phemo supi    thi siwhea phamo
me  NEG think whether you house  like
I don't think whether you like houses

wane mia wheisha phose thi phola
when me  water   see   you walk
When I see water, you walk
```

## complex noun phrases in discourse

build sophisticated descriptions using all available tools:

### layered modification

```
thueta wo  teo   hashe teshe nuthui thue mia lu  li  walime  phose
that   few round green good  pebble of   me  REL PST outside see
those few round green good pebbles of mine that I saw outside

phiato pa   mipho siwhea lu  thephoa teshe wheisha phamo wheo phuwe
this   most blue  house  REL person  good  water   like  at   sleep
this bluest house where the person who likes good water sleeps
```

### possession chains

```
hashe nuthui thue siwhea thue thephoa thue mia
green pebble of   house  of   person  of   me
the green pebble of the house of my person

teshe wheisha thue tophe siwhea thue walime
good  water   of   big   house  of   outside
the good water of the big house from outside
```

## discourse organization

### topic introduction with `ha`

```
ha  nuthui, mia phiato mo   teshe  phamo
TOP pebble, me  this   more good   like
Speaking of pebbles, I like this one better

ha  thueta siwhea, thi welime phose
TOP that   house,  you where  see
Speaking of that house, where do you see it?
```

### contrast with `mi`

```
mia nuthui phamo. mi   thi siwhea phose
me  pebble like.  CNTR you house  see
I like pebbles. However, you see houses

phiato teshe. mi   thueta pisha
this   good.  CNTR that   soft
This is good. However, that is soft
```

### emphasis and focus

```
ma  nuthui mia phamo (not other things)
EMP pebble me  like
It's pebbles that I like

mia ma  phamo nuthui (not other actions)
me  EMP like  pebble
I do like pebbles
```

## complex conversation patterns

### multi-turn reasoning

**person a:**
```
welime thi hashe  nuthui phose renu    hamite teshe
where  you green  pebble see   because how    good
Where do you see green pebbles because how good?
```

**person b:**
```
mia wheo tophe siwhea phose renu    phiato mo   mipho huwa pa   teshe
me  at   big   house  see   because this   more blue  so   most good
I see them at the big house because this one is more blue so it's best
```

**person a:**
```
wa thi su  matu  wheisha phose nuthui phamo
Q  you FUT after water   see   pebble like
Will you like pebbles after you see water?
```

**person b:**
```
wetu mia wheisha phose mia su  nuthui phamo. mi   wetu me  phose me  phamo
if   me  water   see   me  FUT pebble like.  CNTR if   NEG see   NEG like
If I see water I will like pebbles. However, if I don't see, I won't like
```

### embedded questions and reports

```
thi phemi wetu mia nuthui phamo
you speak if   me  pebble like
You speak about whether I like pebbles

mia phemo lu  thi welime phola
me  think REL you where  walk
I think about where you walk

thi showo ha  mia pimo   phola nuthui phose
you tell  TOP me  before walk  pebble see
You tell that before I walk, I see pebbles
```

## advanced relative clause combinations

### multiple relative clauses

```
lu  mia phose thephoa lu  li  hashe  nuthui phamo wheo siwhea phuwe
REL me  see   person  REL PST green  pebble like  at   house  sleep
the person whom I see who liked green pebbles sleeps at the house

siwhea lu  thephoa lu  mia phemo wheo teshe wheisha phamo
house  REL person  REL me  think at   good  water   like
the house where the person whom I think about likes good water
```

### relative clauses with coordination

```
lu  mia phose nene thi phemo thephoa teshe
REL me  see   and  you think person  good
the person whom I see and you think about is good

lu  li  phola nene su  phamo thephoa wheo siwhea phuwe
REL PST walk  and  FUT like  person  at   house  sleep
the person who walked and will like sleeps at the house
```

## key insights

1. **systematic building**: complex sentences follow the same SOV + particle patterns
2. **discourse organization**: particles create clear topic and focus structure
3. **embedding principle**: any simple sentence can embed in relative clauses
4. **coordination flexibility**: connect at word, phrase, or clause level
5. **semantic precision**: conjunctions show exact logical relationships
