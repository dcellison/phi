# lesson 06: narrative flow and temporal sequences

> this lesson explores how to construct coherent narratives in phi using temporal
> markers, sequential conjunctions, and systematic story organization. you'll learn
> to tell stories that flow naturally while maintaining phi's systematic clarity.

## building from lesson 05

you now understand:
- **complex coordination**: connecting equal ideas with conjunctions
- **subordination**: showing causal and conditional relationships
- **discourse organization**: topic markers, contrast, emphasis
- **advanced embedding**: multiple relative clauses and possession chains

## temporal framework for narratives

phi organizes time systematically through particles and conjunctions:

### temporal particles (review)

| particle | position | meaning | usage |
|----------|----------|---------|-------|
| `li` | before verb | past | completed actions |
| `ta` | before verb | present | current actions |
| `su` | before verb | future | planned actions |
| `la` | before verb | progressive | ongoing actions |

### temporal conjunctions for sequence

| conjunction | meaning | narrative function |
|-------------|---------|-------------------|
| `pimo` | before | establishing prior events |
| `matu` | after | showing consequences |
| `wane` | when | simultaneous events |
| `tura` | since | causal connections |
| `lina` | until | duration boundaries |
| `runa` | while | parallel actions |

## basic narrative structure

### three-part story organization

**1. setup (background)**
```
li thashoa thephoa wheo shuthai siwhea phuwe
PST day    person at   forest  house  sleep
One day, a person slept at a forest house
```

**2. development (action sequence)**
```
wane thiphoa thuphea nene mia li pheru
when morning come    and  me   PST look
When morning came and I looked

matu pheru mia li hashe nuthui wheo siwhea phose
after look me   PST green pebble at   house see
After looking, I saw green pebbles at the house
```

**3. resolution (outcome)**
```
huwa mia li nuthui phaso nene siwhea shio phola
so   me   PST pebble take  and  house from walk
So I took the pebbles and walked from the house
```

## temporal sequence building

### chronological progression

```
lutane  mia li  thiphoa wheo shuthai phola
firstly me  PST morning at   forest  walk
First, I walked in the forest in the morning

matu  phola mia li  teshe wheisha wheo raethea phose
after walk  me  PST good  water   at   river   see
After walking, I saw good water at the river

tumane  mia li  wheisha phome nene siwhea wheo phuwe
finally me  PST water   drink and  house  at   sleep
Finally, I drank water and slept at the house
```

### parallel and overlapping events

```
runa  mia li  wheisha phome thi li  nuthui wheo shuthai phose
while me  PST water   drink you PST pebble at   forest  see
While I drank water, you saw pebbles in the forest

wane mia li  phuwe  thephoa li  wheo siwhea phemo
when me  PST sleep  person  PST at   house  think
When I slept, the person thought at the house
```

## character development through description

### systematic character introduction

```
lu  mia phose thephoa rashi nene mo   tophe
REL me  see   person  angry and  more big
the person whom I see is angry and bigger

phiato thephoa li  hiphai wheo shuthai siwhea phera nene su  walime  phola renu    teshe riwhea phamo
this   person  PST past   at   forest  house  be    and  FUT outside walk  because good  wind   like
This person was at a forest house in the past and will walk outside because they like good wind
```

### character motivation and goals

```
thephoa phire wheisha shio raethea phano renu    mipho siwhea thue thephoa whiothea phiso
person  want  water   from river   get   because blue  house  of   person  earth    need
The person wants to get water from the river because the person's blue house needs earth

huwa thephoa li  phela wetu me  wheisha phano
so   person  PST plan  if   NEG water   get
So the person planned what if they don't get water
```

## dialogue and reported speech

### direct speech patterns

```
thephoa li  shuso hi  mia hashe  nuthui phamo
person  PST say   DIR me  green  pebble like
The person said that I like green pebbles

mia li  phata hi  thi welime nuthui phose
me  PST reply DIR you where  pebble see
I replied that you see pebbles where
```

### indirect speech with embedding

```
thephoa li  showo ha  mia hashe  nuthui phamo
person  PST tell  TOP me  green  pebble like
The person told that I like green pebbles

mia li  phemo supi    thephoa wheisha phano
me  PST think whether person  water   get
I thought whether the person gets water
```

### dialogue sequences

```
mia li  thisa  hi  hamite thi siwhea phamo
me  PST ask    DIR how    you house  like
I asked how you like houses

thephoa li  phata hi  mia mo   teshe phamo renu    mipho
person  PST reply DIR me  more good  like  because blue
The person replied that I like them better because they're blue

mia li  thisa hi  wa thi wheo tophe siwhea phuwe
me  PST ask   DIR Q  you at   big   house  sleep
I asked whether you sleep at the big house
```

## environmental and setting description

### systematic scene building

```
shuthai mipho nene hashe  liphai thue whiethea
forest  blue  and  green  tree   of   bay
the blue and green forest of trees by the bay

wheo shuthai riwhea mo   pisha nene thoshea pa   mipha
at   forest  wind   more soft  and  light   most beautiful
In the forest, the wind is softer and the light most beautiful

raethea shio whiethea teshe wheisha phola
river   from bay      good  water   flow
Good water flows from the bay through the river
```

### atmospheric details

```
wane thiphoa ta  thuphea shiophea mo   pisha nene phaishoa wheo shuthai pharu  renu    lashea li  phala
when morning TA  come    air      more soft  and  rainbow  at   forest  appear because rain   PST fall
When morning comes, the air becomes softer and rainbows appear in the forest because rain fell

thashoa matu  riwhea ta  phoru nene liphai ta  whami
day     after wind   TA  blow  and  tree   TA  move
After the day, wind blows and trees move
```

## cause and effect in narratives

### building logical sequences

```
renu    thephoa li  hashe  nuthui phamo mia li  siwhea shio phola huwa mia li  wheisha phome mia su  mo   teshe phela
because person  PST green  pebble like  me  PST house  from walk  so   me  PST water   drink me  FUT more good  feel
Because the person liked green pebbles, I walked from the house, so I drank water and I will feel better

wetu mia me  li  wheisha phome mia me  su  siwhea wheo phuwe
if   me  NEG PST water   drink me  NEG FUT house  at   sleep
If I hadn't drunk water, I wouldn't sleep at the house
```

### complex causal chains

```
pimo   thephoa li  nuthui phamo mia li  phemo ha  thi teshe
before person  PST pebble like  me  PST think TOP you good
Before the person liked pebbles, I thought that you were good

matu  phemo mia li  phela wa thi nuthui phamo
after think me  PST plan  Q  you pebble like
After thinking, I planned whether you like pebbles

tumane  mia li  phiso ha  thi nene mia nuthui phano
finally me  PST decide TOP you and  me  pebble get
Finally I decided that you and I get pebbles
```

## complete narrative example

### "the water seeker"

```
li  thashoa thephoa wheo mipho siwhea phuwe renu    siwhea wheisha me  phera nene thephoa teshe wheisha phire
PST day     person  at   blue  house  sleep because house  water   NEG have  and  person  good  water   want
One day, a person slept at a blue house because the house had no water and the person wanted good water

lutane  thephoa li  thiphoa phola nene raethea phano phela
firstly person  PST morning walk  and  river   find  plan
First, the person planned to walk in the morning and find a river

wane thephoa li  shuthai wheo phola riwhea mo  pisha pheru nene hashe  liphai wheo phaishoa phose huwa mo   mipha     phela
when person  PST forest  at   walk  wind   more soft  feel  and  green  tree   at   rainbow  see   so   more beautiful feel
When the person walked in the forest, they felt softer wind and saw rainbows at green trees so they felt more beautiful

matu  phola thephoa li  raethea wheo teshe wheisha phose nene mo   tapine  phano nene siwhea wheo shupo
after walk  person  PST river   at   good  water   see   and  more quickly get   and  house  at   bring
After walking, the person saw good water at the river and got it more quickly and brought it to the house

tumane  thephoa li  siwhea wheo wheisha phiso nene mo  teshe phela renu    phiato mo  teshe thashoa
finally person  PST house  at   water   put   and  more good feel  because this   more good day
Finally, the person put water at the house and felt better because this was a better day
```

## cultural neutrality in narratives

phi stories focus on universal human experiences:

### universal themes
- seeking water, food, shelter
- relationships between people
- interaction with natural environment
- problem-solving and planning
- emotional responses to beauty

### avoiding cultural specificity
- no religious or cultural practices
- no technology beyond basic tools
- no specific geographical references
- no culturally-bound social structures

## key insights from this lesson

1. **temporal scaffolding**: conjunctions create clear narrative flow
2. **systematic description**: use all word types for rich scene-building
3. **embedded complexity**: combine dialogue, description, and action
4. **causal logic**: show clear relationships between events
5. **universal accessibility**: stories work across all cultural contexts
