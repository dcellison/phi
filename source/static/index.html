💡 Grammar Tips

📝 Word Order

SOV Structure: Phi uses Subject-Object-Verb order

Example: mia na ne lowhai ta whesa → "I give an answer"

⏰ Tense & Aspect

Tense particles: li (past), ta (present), su (future)

Aspect particles: la (progressive), ni (perfect), po (habitual)

Example: mia li la whemo → "I was thinking"

🏷️ Classifiers

Shape classifiers: lea (long objects), moi (flat objects), teo (round objects)

Usage: Place before the noun they classify

Example: moi whethea → "flat book", teo noshea → "round fruit"

👤 Animacy Markers

Animacy particles: he (human), pi (animate), ne (inanimate)

Usage: Mark the animacy of subjects and objects

Example: he thephoa → "person (human)", pi mathai → "cat (animate)"

🔍 Evidentiality

Evidence markers: hi (direct), ro (inference), nu (hearsay), ti (reported), mu (memory), pe (presumption)

Usage: Show how you know the information

Example: hi ta ne lowhai whuru → "The wind is blowing (I can see/feel it)"

❓ Questions

Yes/No questions: Start with wa

Wh-questions: Use question words like hamite (how many), hamire (where)

Example: wa thi ta whera → "Are you coming?"

🎯 Emphasis & Politeness

Emphasis: ma particle emphasizes the following word

Politeness: so particle adds politeness to the sentence

Example: so ma he thephoa ta phoni → "The person is very wise (politely said)"

🔗 Derivational Particles

Noun to Verb: se + noun creates a verb

Verb to Noun: ra + verb creates a noun

Example: se lowhai → "to answer" (from lowhai "answer")

📊 Numbers & Quantity

Number particles: wo (few), lo (plural), no (many)

Basic numbers: phi (one), whu (two), pho (four), wha (five)

Example: lo he thephoa → "people (plural)"
