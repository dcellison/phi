# Phi Language Manual Organization

I would organize a Phi language manual in the following structure, using the current documents:

## Part I: Foundations

1. Introduction to Phi
    - Language philosophy and design principles
    - Overview of key features
    - The single part of speech principle

1. Phonology & Phonotactics (from lexicon-rules.md)
    - Sound inventory (consonants, vowels, fricatives)
    - Syllable structure
    - Pronunciation guidelines
    - Word patterns by grammatical function

## Part II: Core Grammar

1. Lexical Categories (overview section with pointers to specific files)
    - Word formation principles
    - Pattern-based word recognition

1. Basic Sentence Structure
    - Word order
    - Essential particles

1. The Noun System (nouns.md, pronouns.md, determiners.md)
    - Noun patterns and categories
    - Pronouns and reference
    - Determiners and specification

1. The Verb System (verbs.md, particles.md)
    - Verb patterns and categories
    - Tense, aspect, mood markers
    - Verbal particles

## Part III: Modifiers and Relationships

1. Modifiers (adjectives.md, adverbs.md)
    - Adjectives and modification
    - Adverbial expressions
    - Comparison and degree

1. Relationships (prepositions.md, conjunctions.md)
    - Spatial and temporal relationships
    - Logical connections
    - Complex relationships

## Part IV: Specialized Systems

1. Classifiers and Counting (classifiers.md)
    - Classifier usage and selection
    - Numerical expressions
    - Quantification

1. Particles and Discourse (particles.md, interjections.md)
    - Functional particles
    - Discourse markers
    - Expressive elements
    - Interjections and emotional language

## Part V: Practical Usage

1. Common Phrases and Patterns
    - Greetings and social expressions
    - Question formation
    - Requests and commands

1. Sample Texts
    - Dialogues
    - Narratives
    - Practical examples

## Appendices

1. Comprehensive Word Lists (organized by category)
2. Pattern Reference Guide (quick reference for word patterns)
3. Usage Notes (special cases and exceptions)
