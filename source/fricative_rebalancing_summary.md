# Fricative Distribution Analysis & Rebalancing Summary

## Key Findings from Deep Analysis

### Root Causes of Imbalance

1. **Phonotactic Constraints Drive Usage Patterns**
   - **Adjectives**: Fricatives only in medial position [C][V][F][V]
   - **Verbs**: Fricatives only in initial position [F][V][C][V]
   - **Nouns**: Fricatives in both initial and medial positions [C/F][V/P][F][P]

2. **Unconscious 'ph' Bias**
   - **ph: 36.6%** of all fricatives (should be 25%)
   - Overused in ALL major POS categories
   - Particularly severe in adjectives (50.4%) and verbs (42.1%)

3. **Systematic 'wh' Avoidance**
   - **wh: 13.5%** of all fricatives (should be 25%)
   - **ZERO usage** in adjectives (0 out of 133 words!)
   - Severely underused in verbs (6.4%)

4. **Position-Specific Patterns**
   - **Initial position**: ph dominates (especially in verbs)
   - **Medial position**: ph heavily preferred (especially in adjectives)
   - **'wh' avoided** in both positions across most POS

### Most Critical Issues

| POS | Issue | Current | Target | Action Needed |
|-----|-------|---------|--------|---------------|
| **Adjectives** | Zero 'wh' usage | 0% wh | 25% wh | +33 'wh' adjectives |
| **Verbs** | Severe 'wh' underuse | 6.4% wh | 25% wh | +31 'wh' verbs |
| **Overall** | 'ph' overuse | 36.6% ph | 25% ph | -115 'ph' instances |
