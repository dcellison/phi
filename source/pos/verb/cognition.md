# cognition

> Cognition verbs describe the fundamental mental activities by which humans perceive, remember, understand, apply, analyze, evaluate, create, reflect, and become aware of the world and themselves. These verbs are universal across cultures and central to peaceful, constructive communication. In Phi, cognition verbs are organized into clear categories—remembering, understanding, applying, analyzing, evaluating, creating, reflecting, and perceiving—each representing a distinct facet of human thought and learning. This structure supports clarity, empathy, and the peaceful exchange of knowledge, aligning with the language’s commitment to disambiguation and peace linguistics.
