# communication

> Communication verbs describe the essential actions by which people share, receive, and co-construct meaning—whether through speaking, listening, writing, asking, greeting, or nonverbal expression. These verbs are universal across cultures and underpin all forms of peaceful, constructive interaction. In Phi, communication verbs are organized into clear categories—expressing and stating, asking and inquiring, responding and replying, describing and explaining, informing and reporting, persuading and convincing, discussing and debating, greeting and parting, requesting and commanding, sharing and distributing, listening and receiving, writing and recording, and nonverbal and paralinguistic communication. This structure supports clarity, dignity, and empathy in every exchange, reflecting the language’s commitment to peace linguistics and global accessibility.
