# perception

> This vocabulary represents fundamental human experiences of sensing, detecting, observing, and interpreting information from the environment found universally across contemporary global cultures. These concepts encompass the full spectrum of sensory awareness, from basic physiological detection through complex cognitive interpretation of stimuli across all sensory modalities. The verbs included here address core perceptual phenomena: visual observation, auditory reception, tactile sensation, olfactory detection, gustatory experience, proprioceptive awareness, and cognitive processing of sensory information. Each verb captures essential aspects of human sensory experience including seeing, hearing, touching, smelling, tasting, feeling, noticing, recognizing, distinguishing, and understanding. This vocabulary celebrates the human capacity for sophisticated sensory discrimination and interpretation that enables survival, communication, aesthetic appreciation, and meaningful interaction with both physical and social environments across all cultural contexts where perception occurs.
