# process

> This vocabulary represents fundamental human experiences of transformation, change, and development found universally across contemporary global cultures. These concepts encompass the full spectrum of process phenomena, from simple physical alterations through complex psychological, social, and spiritual transformations that define the human condition. The verbs included here address core transformative processes: becoming and changing, growing and developing, learning and adapting, healing and integrating, creating and destroying, cycling and flowing, progressing and regressing, and evolving through states of being. Each verb captures essential aspects of human process experience including transformation, metamorphosis, development, evolution, adaptation, maturation, renewal, regeneration, progression, advancement, improvement, refinement, integration, synthesis, resolution, and the dynamic flow of existence through continuous states of becoming. This vocabulary acknowledges that human life is fundamentally processual rather than static, characterized by constant flux, development, and the ongoing journey of transformation from birth through death and the cyclical renewal that defines the perennial philosophy found across world cultures.
