# Section 13: Comparison and Quantification

## Overview

The **comparison and quantification system** provides speakers with precise tools for **degree marking, quantity expression, and comparative analysis**. This system enables nuanced descriptive expression through systematic comparison particles and number distinctions, supporting both analytical precision and everyday descriptive needs.

## Core Function

**Comparison and quantification particles** address fundamental descriptive questions:

- **How much or how many?** → Number particles (`wo`, `lo`, `no`)
- **To what degree?** → Comparison particles (`pa`, `mo`, `sa`, `le`, `re`)
- **Compared to what?** → Comparative constructions with emphasis
- **How should we highlight degrees?** → Integration with emphasis (`ma`)

## The Descriptive Architecture

Phi's comparison system operates through **Slot 2: Core Word Particles** with systematic ordering:

**Case > Animacy > Number > Comparison > Emphasis**

```
na he lo mo ma nowhea
OBJ HUM PL CMPR EMPH person
"*more* people (object, human, plural, emphasized)"
```

This ordering ensures that **grammatical relationships precede semantic categorization, which precedes quantification, which precedes degree marking, which precedes focal prominence**.

## Cross-Linguistic Perspective

### Universal Comparative Phenomena

Most languages encode comparison through:
- **Degree systems**: Positive, comparative, superlative distinctions
- **Number systems**: Singular, plural, sometimes dual or trial
- **Quantification**: Precise vs. approximate quantity expressions
- **Emphasis integration**: Degree marking with focal prominence

### Phi's Systematic Innovations

**Five-way comparison system**: Unusually rich degree distinctions
```
Standard comparative systems: 3-way (positive/comparative/superlative)
Phi comparison system:        5-way (least/less/equal/more/most)
```

**Three-way number system**: Precise quantity distinctions
```
Standard number systems: 2-way (singular/plural)
Phi number system:       3-way (paucal/plural/greater plural)
```

**Systematic emphasis integration**: Comparison particles work predictably with focus
```
mo ma riphe    "*more* important" (comparative + emphasis)
pa ma misha    "*most* beautiful" (superlative + emphasis)
```

## The Complete Comparison System

### Number Particles (3)

| Particle | Function | Quantity Range | Example |
|----------|----------|----------------|---------|
| `wo` | Paucal | 2-5 items | `he wo nowhea` "a few people" |
| `lo` | General Plural | 6+ items (default) | `he lo nowhea` "people" |
| `no` | Greater Plural | 6+ items (many) | `he no nowhea` "many people" |

### Comparison Particles (5)

| Particle | Function | Degree Relationship | Example |
|----------|----------|-------------------|---------|
| `pa` | Superlative | Most (highest degree) | `pa riphe` "most important" |
| `mo` | Comparative | More (higher degree) | `mo riphe` "more important" |
| `sa` | Equative | Equal (same degree) | `sa riphe` "equally important" |
| `le` | Diminished | Less (lower degree) | `le riphe` "less important" |
| `re` | Minimal | Least (lowest degree) | `re riphe` "least important" |

## Functional Specializations

### Academic and Technical Precision

**Comparative analysis**: Academic contexts use systematic comparison
```
so ro ne moruphea mo ma naphe ta phera.
POL INF INAN findings CMPR EMPH precise PRS be
"The findings are politely *more* precise." (I infer)
```

**Superlative evaluation**: Research contexts emphasize extreme degrees
```
ke ne reshathi pa ma riphe ta phera.
KNOW INAN research SPRL EMPH important PRS be
"The research is *most* important." (I know from study)
```

**Quantitative precision**: Technical contexts use specific number marking
```
ne wo moruphea ni pa ma naphe ta phera.
INAN PAUC findings PRF SPRL EMPH precise PRS be
"A few findings have proven *most* precise."
```

### Descriptive Expression

**Quality comparison**: Everyday descriptions use systematic degree marking
```
ne whethea mo ma misha ta phera mi ne lophui le ma houphe ta phera.
INAN book CMPR EMPH beautiful PRS be CNTR INAN art LESS EMPH expensive PRS be
"Books are *more* beautiful, but art is *less* expensive."
```

**Quantity specification**: Counting and estimation with number particles
```
mia ta na he wo nowhea whelu.
1SG PRS OBJ HUM PAUC person love
"I love a few people." (2-5 people)

mia ta na he no nowhea whelu.
1SG PRS OBJ HUM GRPL person love
"I love many people." (6+ people, emphasis on quantity)
```

### Emphatic and Contrastive Description

**Emphasized comparison**: Focus plus degree for maximum impact
```
ma pa riphe ne whethea ta phera.
EMPH SPRL important INAN book PRS be
"*Most* important books are." (emphatic superlative)
```

**Contrastive quantification**: Number distinctions in opposition
```
mia na he wo nowhea whelu mi sha na he no nowhea whelu.
1SG OBJ HUM PAUC person love CNTR 3SG OBJ HUM GRPL person love
"I love a few people, but s/he loves many people."
```

## Systematic Interactions

### Number-Comparison Integration

**Degree of plurality**: Comparison particles can modify number concepts
```
mo ma lo he nowhea ta phera.
CMPR EMPH PL HUM person PRS be
"There are *more* people." (comparative quantity)

pa ma no he nowhea ta phera.
SPRL EMPH GRPL HUM person PRS be
"There are *most* (many) people." (superlative quantity)
```

### Comparison-Emphasis Coordination

**Strategic degree focus**: Emphasis particles highlight comparison
```
Basic Comparison:
mo riphe ta phera.
CMPR important PRS be
"Is more important."

Emphasized Comparison:
mo ma riphe ta phera.
CMPR EMPH important PRS be
"Is *more* important." (focused degree)
```

### Cross-Slot Comparison Effects

**Evidential-comparison coherence**: Different evidentials support different degrees
```
Strong Evidence + High Degree:
hi pa ma riphe ta phera.
DIR SPRL EMPH important PRS be
"Is *most* important." (I directly observe maximum degree)

Weak Evidence + Moderate Degree:
ro mo ma riphe ta phera.
INF CMPR EMPH important PRS be
"Is *more* important." (I infer moderate degree)
```

## Complex Comparative Constructions

### Multi-Element Comparisons

**Systematic comparative relationships**:
```
he nowhea mo ma riphe ta phera mi le ma misha ta phera.
HUM person CMPR EMPH important PRS be CNTR LESS EMPH beautiful PRS be
"The person is *more* important but *less* beautiful."
```

**Graduated comparison sequences**:
```
ne whethea le riphe ta phera.
INAN book LESS important PRS be
"Books are less important."

ne lophui mo riphe ta phera.
INAN art CMPR important PRS be
"Art is more important."

ne ruphea pa riphe ta phera.
INAN information SPRL important PRS be
"Information is most important."
```

### Number-Based Argumentation

**Quantitative evidence in reasoning**:
```
mia ta na he wo nowhea whera mi sha ta na he no nowhea whera.
1SG PRS OBJ HUM PAUC person learn CNTR 3SG PRS OBJ HUM GRPL person learn
"I learn from a few people, but s/he learns from many people."

ro sha mo ma wanume ta whera.
INF 3SG CMPR EMPH well PRS learn
"S/he learns *more* well." (I infer from quantity)
```

## Processing and Cognitive Advantages

### Incremental Degree Processing

**Systematic degree interpretation**:
1. **Basic concept** established by word
2. **Quantity** specified by number particle
3. **Degree** marked by comparison particle
4. **Focus** highlighted by emphasis particle

**Predictable comparison scope**: Degree particles scope over immediately following words

### Acquisition Sequence

**Learning progression**:
**Stage 1**: Master basic number distinctions (`wo`, `lo`, `no`)
**Stage 2**: Learn core comparison particles (`mo`, `pa`)
**Stage 3**: Integrate emphasis with comparison (`mo ma`, `pa ma`)
**Stage 4**: Handle complex multi-element comparative constructions

## Typological Significance

### Cross-Linguistic Comparison Coverage

**Comparative constructions across languages**:
- **English**: good/better/best (3-way + irregular)
- **Latin**: bonus/melior/optimus (3-way + morphological)
- **Phi**: re/le/sa/mo/pa (5-way + systematic particles)

**Number system coverage**:
- **English**: singular/plural (2-way)
- **Arabic**: singular/dual/plural (3-way)
- **Phi**: paucal/plural/greater plural (3-way + semantic precision)

### Phi's Descriptive Advantages

**Systematic compositionality**: All degree and quantity distinctions combine predictably
**Emphasis integration**: Focus marking works with all comparison types
**Cross-category coherence**: Comparison integrates with evidentiality, politeness, and discourse
