# Animacy System

## Three-Way Animacy Hierarchy

Phi employs a systematic animacy marking system that categorizes all nouns based on their inherent semantic properties. This three-way hierarchy reflects universal cognitive distinctions found across human languages while providing essential grammatical information for phi's syntax and semantics.

## The Complete Animacy Paradigm

| Particle | Category | Semantic Range | Cognitive Status | Grammatical Effects |
|----------|----------|----------------|------------------|-------------------|
| `he` | Human | People, personal entities | Highest animacy | Required object marking |
| `pi` | Animate | Animals, living beings | Medium animacy | Preferred object marking |
| `ne` | Inanimate | Objects, concepts, forces | Lowest animacy | Optional object marking |

## Individual Animacy Analysis

### 1. `he` - Human Animacy

**Function**: Marks nouns referring to humans, persons, and human-like entities.

**Core semantic range**:
- Individual people and persons
- Human collectives and groups
- Anthropomorphized entities
- Human roles and social positions

**Basic human marking**:
```
he nowhea ta whera.
HUM person PRS learn
"The person learns."

mia na he nowhea ta whisi.
1SG OBJ HUM person PRS see
"I see the person."

he phiphea na ne whethea ta whera.
HUM student OBJ INAN book PRS learn
"The student learns from books."
```

**Human collectives**:
```
he lo nowhea ta tolime whema.
HUM PL person PRS together work
"The people work together."

si he thephoa na ne ruphea ta whelu.
SUBJ HUM teacher OBJ INAN information PRS give
"The teacher gives information."
```

**Social roles and positions**:
```
he phuwhea na ne monowhea ta thero.
HUM researcher OBJ INAN method PRS require
"The researcher requires the method."

mia na he raushai ta whelu.
1SG OBJ HUM leader PRS love
"I love the leader."
```

### 2. `pi` - Animate Animacy

**Function**: Marks nouns referring to non-human living beings with agency.

**Core semantic range**:
- Individual animals and creatures
- Animal collectives (flocks, herds)
- Plants with perceived agency
- Living systems and organisms

**Basic animate marking**:
```
pi shiphe ta whumi.
ANIM dog PRS run
"The dog runs."

mia na pi shiphe ta whelu.
1SG OBJ ANIM dog PRS love
"I love the dog."

pi thiphe na ne liphea ta wheru.
ANIM bird OBJ INAN seed PRS eat
"The bird eats seeds."
```

**Animal collectives**:
```
pi lo shiphe ta tolime phola.
ANIM PL dog PRS together walk
"The dogs walk together."

si he nowhea na pi lo thiphe ta whisi.
SUBJ HUM person OBJ ANIM PL bird PRS see
"The person sees the birds."
```

**Living systems**:
```
pi luphea ta waphe phera.
ANIM forest PRS warm be
"The forest is warm." (forest as living system)

ne riwhea na pi shiphe ta thelu.
INAN wind OBJ ANIM dog PRS affect
"The wind affects the dog."
```

### 3. `ne` - Inanimate Animacy

**Function**: Marks nouns referring to non-living entities, objects, and concepts.

**Core semantic range**:
- Physical objects and artifacts
- Natural forces and phenomena  
- Abstract concepts and ideas
- Substances and materials

**Basic inanimate marking**:
```
ne whethea ta riphe phera.
INAN book PRS important be
"The book is important."

mia na ne whethea ta whera.
1SG OBJ INAN book PRS learn
"I learn from books."

ne riwhea ta whuru.
INAN wind PRS blow
"The wind blows."
```

**Physical objects**:
```
ne tophea na ne huthea ta phera.
INAN thing OBJ INAN room PRS be
"The thing is in the room."

si he nowhea na ne miphea ta whesi.
SUBJ HUM person OBJ INAN tool PRS create
"The person creates the tool."
```

**Abstract concepts**:
```
ne ruphea ta riphe phera.
INAN information PRS important be
"Information is important."

mia na ne lophui ta wheru.
1SG OBJ INAN art PRS appreciate
"I appreciate art."
```

## Animacy Hierarchy Effects

### Object Marking Requirements
Animacy level affects object marking patterns:

**Human objects** (typically require `na`):
```
mia na he nowhea ta whisi.     (recommended)
1SG OBJ HUM person PRS see
"I see the person."

*mia he nowhea ta whisi.       (marked, less preferred)
1SG HUM person PRS see
```

**Animate objects** (prefer `na`):
```
mia na pi shiphe ta whelu.     (preferred)
1SG OBJ ANIM dog PRS love
"I love the dog."

mia pi shiphe ta whelu.        (acceptable)
1SG ANIM dog PRS love
"I love the dog."
```

**Inanimate objects** (optional `na`):
```
mia na ne whethea ta whera.    (formal)
1SG OBJ INAN book PRS learn
"I learn from books."

mia ne whethea ta whera.       (informal)
1SG INAN book PRS learn
"I learn from books."
```

### Pronoun Reference Patterns
Animacy affects pronoun usage:

```
he nowhea ta whera. sha ta riphe phera.
HUM person PRS learn. 3SG PRS important be
"The person learns. S/he is important." (human → sha)

pi shiphe ta whumi. sha ta napine phera.
ANIM dog PRS run. 3SG PRS fast be
"The dog runs. It is fast." (animate → sha)

ne whethea ta riphe phera. sha ta me wulu phera.
INAN book PRS important be. 3SG PRS NEG old be
"The book is important. It is not old." (inanimate → sha)
```

### Discourse Prominence
Higher animacy = greater discourse prominence:

```
he nowhea nene pi shiphe ta tolime phola.
HUM person and ANIM dog PRS together walk
"The person and dog walk together." (person mentioned first)

si he nowhea na pi shiphe ta thelu mi sha ta whelu.
SUBJ HUM person OBJ ANIM dog PRS care CNTR 3SG PRS love
"The person cares for the dog and s/he loves it." (person = subject)
```

## Borderline Cases and Flexibility

### Anthropomorphized Entities
Entities can shift animacy based on conceptualization:

```
ne shiphai ta whuru.           (wind as force)
INAN wind PRS blow
"The wind blows."

pi shiphai ta whuru.           (wind as agent)
ANIM wind PRS blow
"The wind blows." (personified)
```

### Collective Entities
Groups can vary in animacy marking:

```
ne thephoa ta whema.           (institution)
INAN company PRS work
"The company works."

he thephoa ta whema.           (people in company)
HUM company PRS work
"The company works." (focusing on people)
```

### Abstract Animacy
Some abstracts receive animate treatment:

```
ne ruphea ta sharo.            (information as object)
INAN information PRS go
"Information goes."

pi ruphea ta sharo.            (information as agent)
ANIM information PRS go
"Information goes." (active conception)
```

## Cross-Linguistic Perspectives

### Universal Animacy Patterns
Phi's system reflects cross-linguistic tendencies:
- **Human > animate > inanimate**: Universal hierarchy
- **Object marking**: Higher animacy → more explicit marking
- **Discourse prominence**: Animate entities preferred as subjects

### Typological Comparisons
- **Japanese**: Similar hierarchy effects on particle usage
- **Navajo**: Elaborate animacy distinctions
- **Russian**: Animate/inanimate case differences
- **Swahili**: Noun class system with animacy effects

## Complex Animacy Interactions

### Multiple Animacy Levels
```
si he nowhea na pi shiphe na ne whethea ta whelu.
SUBJ HUM person OBJ ANIM dog OBJ INAN book PRS give
"The person gives books to the dog." (hierarchy: human > animate > inanimate)
```

### Animacy in Questions
```
wa he nowhea na pi shiphe ta whisi.
Q HUM person OBJ ANIM dog PRS see
"Does the person see the dog?"

hamite si he nowhea na ne whethea ta whera.
how SUBJ HUM person OBJ INAN book PRS learn
"How does the person learn from books?"
```

### Animacy in Conditionals
```
tu he nowhea ta whera, sha su riphe phera.
COND HUM person PRS learn, 3SG FUT important be
"If the person learns, s/he will be important."

tu ne riwhea ta whuru, pi lo thiphe su sharo.
COND INAN wind PRS blow, ANIM PL bird FUT go
"If the wind blows, the birds will go."
```

## Pedagogical Guidelines

### Learning Sequence
1. **Master basic distinctions**: human, animate, inanimate
2. **Practice object marking**: high animacy = explicit marking
3. **Understand flexibility**: context can shift animacy
4. **Apply to discourse**: animacy affects topic/subject choice

### Common Errors
- **Over-marking**: Using `he` for non-human animates
- **Under-marking**: Omitting animacy with high-animacy objects
- **Inconsistency**: Switching animacy marking within discourse
