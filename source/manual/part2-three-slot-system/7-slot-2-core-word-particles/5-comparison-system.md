# Comparison System

## Five-Way Comparative Framework

Phi employs a comprehensive comparison system that expresses the full range of comparative relationships between entities. This five-particle framework enables precise degree distinctions and equality comparisons, going beyond simple "more/most" patterns found in many natural languages.

## The Complete Comparison Paradigm

| Particle | Comparison | English Gloss | Relationship Type | Usage Context |
|----------|------------|---------------|-------------------|---------------|
| `pa` | Superlative | "most/best" | Maximum degree | Highest in group |
| `mo` | Comparative | "more/-er" | Greater degree | Binary comparison |
| `sa` | Equality | "as/same" | Equal degree | Equivalent comparison |
| `le` | Lesser | "less" | Reduced degree | Diminished comparison |
| `re` | Least | "least/worst" | Minimum degree | Lowest in group |

*Note: Base/positive degree is unmarked - no particle needed.*

## Individual Comparison Analysis

### Unmarked Positive Degree

**Function**: Default state expressing basic quality without comparison.

**Basic positive patterns**:
```
he nowhea riphe ta phera.
HUM person important PRS be
"The person is important." (neutral statement)

ne whethea misha ta phera.
INAN book beautiful PRS be
"The book is beautiful." (basic quality)

pi shiphe napine ta phera.
ANIM dog fast PRS be
"The dog is fast." (unmarked degree)
```

### 1. `pa` - Superlative

**Function**: Marks the highest degree within a group or context.

**Core usage principles**:
- Maximum degree among multiple items
- "The most X" or "the X-est"
- Implies comparison with entire group
- Often used with excellence and extremes

**Basic superlative patterns**:
```
he nowhea pa riphe ta phera.
HUM person SPRL important PRS be
"The person is most important."

ne whethea pa misha ta phera.
INAN book SPRL beautiful PRS be
"The book is most beautiful."

pi shiphe pa napine ta phera.
ANIM dog SPRL fast PRS be
"The dog is fastest."
```

**Group comparisons**:
```
si he nowhea pa riphe na lo he nowhea ta phera.
SUBJ HUM person SPRL important OBJ PL HUM person PRS be
"The person is the most important among the people."

ne whethea pa misha shio lo ne whethea ta phera.
INAN book SPRL beautiful from PL INAN book PRS be
"The book is the most beautiful of the books."
```

**Excellence and extremes**:
```
mia pa wanume ta whera.
1SG SPRL well PRS learn
"I learn best." (excellence)

ne riwhea pa thilu ta phera.
INAN weather SPRL bad PRS be
"The weather is worst." (negative extreme)
```

### 2. `mo` - Comparative

**Function**: Marks greater degree in binary comparisons.

**Core usage principles**:
- "More X" or "X-er than"
- Binary comparison between two entities
- Implies direct contrast
- Most common comparative form

**Basic comparative patterns**:
```
he nowhea mo riphe ta phera.
HUM person CMPR important PRS be
"The person is more important."

ne whethea mo misha ta phera.
INAN book CMPR beautiful PRS be
"The book is more beautiful."

pi shiphe mo napine ta phera.
ANIM dog CMPR fast PRS be
"The dog is faster."
```

**Binary comparisons**:
```
si he nowhea mo riphe mi he nowhea ta phera.
SUBJ HUM person CMPR important CNTR HUM person PRS be
"The person is more important than the person."

ne whethea mo misha shio ne whethea ta phera.
INAN book CMPR beautiful from INAN book PRS be
"The book is more beautiful than the book."
```

**Graduated comparisons**:
```
mia mo wanume ta whera.
1SG CMPR well PRS learn
"I learn better." (improved performance)

ne riwhea mo waphe ta phera.
INAN weather CMPR warm PRS be
"The weather is warmer." (increased warmth)
```

### 3. `sa` - Equality

**Function**: Marks equal degree between compared entities.

**Core usage principles**:
- "As X as" or "equally X"
- Equal comparison relationships
- Equivalent degree or quality
- Expresses similarity and equivalence

**Basic equality patterns**:
```
he nowhea sa riphe ta phera.
HUM person EQ important PRS be
"The person is as important." (equal importance)

ne whethea sa misha ta phera.
INAN book EQ beautiful PRS be
"The book is as beautiful." (equal beauty)

pi shiphe sa napine ta phera.
ANIM dog EQ fast PRS be
"The dog is as fast." (equal speed)
```

**Explicit equality comparisons**:
```
si he nowhea sa riphe mi he nowhea ta phera.
SUBJ HUM person EQ important CNTR HUM person PRS be
"The person is as important as the person."

ne whethea sa misha shio ne whethea ta phera.
INAN book EQ beautiful from INAN book PRS be
"The book is as beautiful as the book."
```

**Equivalence and similarity**:
```
mia sa wanume ta whera.
1SG EQ well PRS learn
"I learn as well." (equivalent performance)

lo he nowhea sa riphe ta phera.
PL HUM person EQ important PRS be
"The people are equally important." (same level)
```

### 4. `le` - Lesser Comparative

**Function**: Marks reduced degree in binary comparisons.

**Core usage principles**:
- "Less X than"
- Diminished degree comparison
- Opposite of `mo` (more)
- Expresses reduction or decline

**Basic lesser patterns**:
```
he nowhea le riphe ta phera.
HUM person LESS important PRS be
"The person is less important."

ne whethea le misha ta phera.
INAN book LESS beautiful PRS be
"The book is less beautiful."

pi shiphe le napine ta phera.
ANIM dog LESS fast PRS be
"The dog is less fast."
```

**Reduced degree comparisons**:
```
si he nowhea le riphe mi he nowhea ta phera.
SUBJ HUM person LESS important CNTR HUM person PRS be
"The person is less important than the person."

ne whethea le misha shio ne whethea ta phera.
INAN book LESS beautiful from INAN book PRS be
"The book is less beautiful than the book."
```

**Diminishment and decline**:
```
mia le wanume ta whera.
1SG LESS well PRS learn
"I learn less well." (reduced performance)

ne riwhea le waphe ta phera.
INAN weather LESS warm PRS be
"The weather is less warm." (decreased warmth)
```

### 5. `re` - Superlative Least

**Function**: Marks the lowest degree within a group or context.

**Core usage principles**:
- "The least X" or minimum degree
- Lowest among multiple items
- Opposite of `pa` (most)
- Often used with negative qualities

**Basic least patterns**:
```
he nowhea re riphe ta phera.
HUM person LEAST important PRS be
"The person is least important."

ne whethea re misha ta phera.
INAN book LEAST beautiful PRS be
"The book is least beautiful."

pi shiphe re napine ta phera.
ANIM dog LEAST fast PRS be
"The dog is least fast."
```

**Minimum degree comparisons**:
```
si he nowhea re riphe na lo he nowhea ta phera.
SUBJ HUM person LEAST important OBJ PL HUM person PRS be
"The person is the least important among the people."

ne whethea re misha shio lo ne whethea ta phera.
INAN book LEAST beautiful from PL INAN book PRS be
"The book is the least beautiful of the books."
```

**Minimal qualities**:
```
mia re wanume ta whera.
1SG LEAST well PRS learn
"I learn least well." (poorest performance)

ne riwhea re waphe ta phera.
INAN weather LEAST warm PRS be
"The weather is least warm." (coldest)
```

## Comparison Hierarchies and Scales

### Complete Degree Scale
Phi's five-way system creates a complete degree hierarchy:

**`re` (least) < `le` (less) < unmarked (base) < `mo` (more) < `pa` (most)**

```
he nowhea re riphe ta phera.    (least important)
he nowhea le riphe ta phera.    (less important)
he nowhea riphe ta phera.       (important)
he nowhea mo riphe ta phera.    (more important)
he nowhea pa riphe ta phera.    (most important)
```

### Equality Integration
`sa` (equality) can align with any degree level:

```
he nowhea sa re riphe ta phera.     (as least important)
he nowhea sa riphe ta phera.        (as important)
he nowhea sa pa riphe ta phera.     (as most important)
```

## Complex Comparison Patterns

### Multiple Comparisons
```
si he nowhea mo riphe mi le misha ta phera.
SUBJ HUM person CMPR important CNTR LESS beautiful PRS be
"The person is more important but less beautiful."

ne whethea pa misha nene re thilu ta phera.
INAN book SPRL beautiful and LEAST bad PRS be
"The book is most beautiful and least bad."
```

### Comparison Chains
```
si he nowhea mo riphe, he nowhea sa riphe, he nowhea le riphe ta phera.
SUBJ HUM person CMPR important, HUM person EQ important, HUM person LESS important PRS be
"The person is more important, the person equally important, the person less important."
```

### Contextual Comparisons
```
ne whethea mo misha shio phiato mi le misha shio thueta ta phera.
INAN book CMPR beautiful from this CNTR LESS beautiful from that PRS be
"The book is more beautiful than this but less beautiful than that."
```

## Comparison in Different Contexts

### Questions
```
wa he nowhea mo riphe ta phera.
Q HUM person CMPR important PRS be
"Is the person more important?"

hamite ne whethea pa misha ta phera.
how INAN book SPRL beautiful PRS be
"How is the book most beautiful?"
```

### Conditionals
```
tu he nowhea mo riphe ta phera, sha su pa wanume whera.
COND HUM person CMPR important PRS be, 3SG FUT SPRL well learn
"If the person is more important, s/he will learn best."
```

### Discourse Emphasis
```
ma mo riphe he nowhea ta phera.
EMPH CMPR important HUM person PRS be
"The person is *more* important." (emphasized comparison)
```

## Register Variation

### Formal Academic
Precise comparative relationships:
```
hi si he phuwhea pa riphe na lo he phuwhea ta phera.
DIR SUBJ HUM researcher SPRL important OBJ PL HUM researcher PRS be
"The researcher is most important among researchers." (I see this directly)
```

### Informal Conversation
Simplified comparative usage:
```
mo riphe.                       (context understood)
CMPR important
"More important."

pa misha whethea.
SPRL beautiful book
"Most beautiful book."
```

## Cross-Linguistic Perspectives

### Comparative System Typology
- **Three-way systems**: Most common (positive, comparative, superlative)
- **Two-way systems**: Some languages lack superlatives
- **Complex systems**: Multiple comparative strategies
- **Phi innovation**: Systematic five-way distinction with equality

### Universal Patterns
- **More/most**: Found across languages
- **Equality marking**: Common but varied
- **Less/least**: Less universal than more/most
- **Asymmetric marking**: "More" often simpler than "less"

## Learning Guidelines

### Acquisition Sequence
1. **Start with `mo`**: Basic comparative "more than"
2. **Add `pa`**: Superlative "most" for extremes
3. **Master `sa`**: Equality "as...as" for similarity
4. **Learn `le`**: Lesser degree "less than"
5. **Complete with `re`**: Least degree for minimums

### Strategic Usage
- **Use `mo`/`pa`**: Most frequent comparisons
- **Apply `sa`**: When emphasizing equality
- **Choose `le`/`re`**: For reduced degrees
- **Combine wisely**: Multiple comparisons in discourse
