# Vocabulary Gap Inventory

*Phase 3 of the vocabulary quality campaign, 2026-07. Method: ~450
concept probes (Swadesh 207 + Leipzig–Jakarta 100 + a daily-life sweep
+ the Phase 2 reviewers' finds) matched against gloss, concept, AND
description fields of all 804 entries — the `kerou` lesson applied.
Every "missing" claim below was hand-verified after automated
matching.*

**Status (2026-07): complete.** All batches landed — F, A1, A2, B, C,
D, E, G, H — plus the rulings below. The lexicon stands at 899.

## The headline

- **313 concepts: solidly covered.** The core is real — eat (`nuola`),
  drink (`milau`), cloud (`neparu`), all colors, all kinship the
  philosophy wants, the full life-arc, emotions, weather, household
  structure.
- **~30 concepts: covered by design** — by compound, particle, or a
  near-word (documented below so nobody "fixes" them).
- **~85 concepts: genuine gaps**, classified into proposed coinage
  batches.
- **8 concepts: design decisions**, where absence may be intentional.

## The gaps, in proposed batches

### Batch A — core verbs (the Swadesh backbone; highest priority)
**fall, cut, tell, choose, find, forget, close, wait, turn, burn,
wash, push, pull, throw, count, agree, hide, tie, send, swim, fly,
jump, climb, dig, catch, rise, split, measure, float, squeeze, wipe,
rub, grind**

These are not exotic: *the lake has no ripples because nothing can
fall into it.* Several were hit repeatedly by Phase 2 reviewers
rewriting examples. Recommend coining in two waves (A1: the first
fourteen; A2: the rest).

### Batch B — body and bodily life
**body** (the word itself!), **breathe** (verb; `whunei` is the noun),
**fear, pain, sick/ill, hungry, thirsty, shame, bite, swallow, spit,
sweat, tear (crying), cough, spine, thumb, fingernail, navel, liver,
guts, fur**

A mindfulness language that cannot say "I feel fear" or "I am in
pain" cannot do its central job; the Metta Sutta transmutation had to
route around fear once already.

### Batch C — creatures and their parts
**snake, worm, egg, tail, wing, feather, horn**

A bird (`pelori`) with no wing; a nature pillar with no egg.

### Batch D — food and kitchen
**salt** (Swadesh-core), **milk, honey, grain/bread, soup**

### Batch E — nature
**lightning, thunder, mud, ash, dust, smoke, wave, shadow, shallow**

### Batch F — time adverbs (small, urgent)
**yesterday, today, tomorrow, always, never, often**

All six missing; every diary entry and plan needs them. `nosa` (now)
is currently alone in its class.

### Batch G — household and settlement
**table, seat/chair, shoe, comb, boat, village, well (water source)**

### Batch H — social and abstract
**secret, envy/jealousy, luck/fortune, gift (noun), refuse, thought
(noun), statement, loss, generation, tone/timbre, music, noise,
together**

## Design decisions — RULED (2026-07)

1. **woman / man: REFUSED.** Adding gendered person-words would
   exclude people who do not identify within a binary; individuals
   are treated equally and gender is not distinguished anywhere in
   the language. Recorded in CANON.md.
2. **meat: COIN** (Batch D).
3. **dog and companion animals: COIN** (Batch C).
4. **knife: COIN as kitchen/craft tool**; blade-as-weapon refused.
5. **hunt: REFUSED.**
6. **generic "bad": REFUSED and recorded** — things are harmful,
   broken, or unwell; never simply "bad."
7. **marriage: `telu` (partner) suffices** — moved to covered-by-design.
8. **low-register bodily verbs: COIN without squeamishness** (Batch B).
9. **envy/jealousy: COMPOUND, not root** — `phelu pula` (holding-wish),
   registered in COMPOUNDS.md. Follows the `korua thero` / `thiku nila`
   pattern: hostile relational emotions are composed so the mechanism
   stays visible; the state remains sayable in the first person.
10. **thought (noun): covered by design** — `remo` is verb and noun.
11. **statement: covered by design** — `phelui` (word), `haolu`
    (speak), and the complementizer system carry it.
12. **tone/timbre: COMPOUND** — `haoni welisha` (voice-color),
    registered in COMPOUNDS.md; unlocked by the `welisha` coinage.
13. **well: COINED as `phitura`** (Batch G) — the gap list was stale
    here: `towe` is well-as-wellness and `thula` the natural spring,
    so the true gap was the dug well.
14. **color and sound: DISCOVERED GAPS, coined in Batch H** —
    `welisha` (color: every hue existed, not the category) and
    `shonuwa` (sound: `hea` is the hearing, not the phenomenon).

## Design decisions (original analysis — absence may be intent)

1. **woman / man.** Phi is radically non-gendered by design; `miona`
   (person), `telu` (partner), `phao` (parent) carry the social load.
   Are gendered person-words *refused* (like weapons) or a gap?
   The philosophy suggests refused — but bodies exist, and some
   texts will need them. Recommend: a conscious ruling either way,
   recorded in CANON.md.
2. **meat/flesh.** Absent. Pre-industrial diets included meat;
   refusing the word looks less like peace than like euphemism.
   Recommend coining.
3. **dog** (and domestic animals generally: cat was also retired as
   `phue` became "wise"). Companion animals fit every pillar.
   Recommend coining a small set (dog, cat).
4. **knife/blade.** Phase 2 flagged "blade". A kitchen knife is a
   tool (`tenoa` exists, `kiru` chisel exists); a blade is also a
   weapon-part. Recommend: coin *knife* as a food/craft tool,
   refuse *blade-as-weapon* per the weapons policy.
5. **hunt.** Swadesh-core, pre-industrial subsistence — but adjacent
   to the violence refusal. Needs a ruling.
6. **"bad".** Only `peloma` (harmful) exists. A generic evaluative
   "bad" may be refused by the value-neutrality principle (things are
   harmful, broken, or unwell — not "bad"). Recommend: refuse,
   record in CANON.md.
7. **marriage.** `telu` (partner) + ritual vocabulary may suffice;
   a dedicated word implies an institution the culture-neutral
   principle may not want to fix. Needs a ruling.
8. **vomit** (and similar low-register bodily verbs). Swadesh-core;
   medicine needs it. Recommend coining without squeamishness.

## Proposed sequence

F (six words, one sitting) → A1 → A2 → B → C+D → E → G → H, with the
eight decisions ruled on before their respective batches. Every batch
follows the standing protocol: candidates screened with
`validate_examples.py neighbors`, presented for approval, entries
written to the lexicon register standard.
