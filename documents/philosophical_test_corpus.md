# Phi Philosophical Test Corpus

This is an original regression corpus for philosophical Phi. It is deliberately small, value-conflicted, and revisable. Its purpose is to expose missing distinctions and brittle constructions, not to present ideal speakers or prove that Phi produces good decisions.

## 1. Water for the garden

```
A: lo mia thepalu phialu phelona.
PL 1SG garden water require.
(Our garden requires water.)

B: lo mia henoi phialu ma phelu.
PL 1SG ENOUGH water NEG hold.
(We do not have enough water.)

A: mia thepalu sone. whekai mia we luphore sone.
1SG garden value. CONTR 1SG ALSO river value.
(I value the garden. However, I also value the river.)

B: lu lo mia luphore shorupo. lo mia thepalu po numelo.
COND PL 1SG river protect. PL 1SG garden POT nurture.
(If we protect the river, we can nurture the garden.)

A: lo mia wei thepalu phina phialu loa. lo mia luphore shorupo.
PL 1SG DAT garden FEW water give. PL 1SG river protect.
(We give a little water to the garden. We protect the river.)

B: lo mia nawo.
PL 1SG agree.
(We agree.)
```

The dialogue tests a genuine tradeoff rather than treating ecological values as automatically harmonious. The present lexicon can state need, scarcity, two values, a condition, and agreement. It does not yet distinguish a compromise from an optimal or merely acceptable decision; that remains a matrix question.

## 2. Repair or replace

```
A: ha keli serao nai.
PROX device old be.
(This device is old.)

B: ha keli se po shiroka.
PROX device PASS POT repair.
(This device can be repaired.)

A: shiroka riola noetha nai.
repair labor essential be.
(Repair labor is essential.)

B: lo mia wireo sone. lo mia muila we sone.
PL 1SG future value. PL 1SG earth ALSO value.
(We value the future. We also value the earth.)

A: thelao lo mia ha keli kanu.
CONS PL 1SG PROX device choose.
(Therefore, we choose this device.)
```

The conclusion is intentionally defeasible. The premises omit safety, energy use, and the repairer's consent. A later passage should be able to challenge the conclusion without treating repair as sacred merely because it fits a solarpunk value.

## 3. Rain and the river

```
A: mia mena pheralu to lepa meno ti sano.
1SG DECL.COMP rain PST fall DECL.COMP.CLOSE REP know.
(I know by report that rain fell.)

B: mia luphore hi nila. luphore ma hiso nai.
1SG river DIR see. river NEG clean be.
(I see the river directly. The river is not clean.)

A: mia mena pheralu luphore ka peloma kelu meno ke nohero.
1SG DECL.COMP rain river CAUS harmful become DECL.COMP.CLOSE INFER believe.
(I infer that the rain made the river harmful.)

B: mia ma sano. phisu serao keli po peloma nai.
1SG NEG know. EXMP old device POT harmful be.
(I do not know. For example, an old device may be harmful.)

A: mia to kanelu. mia nohero to helui.
1SG PST err. 1SG believe PST change.
(I erred. My belief changed.)
```

This dialogue separates report, direct observation, inference, an alternative possibility, error, and revision. The example does not prove the device caused pollution; its job is to block premature certainty. Phi still lacks a conventional word or construction for *alternative hypothesis*.

## 4. A phrase under critique

```
A: lo mia patha growth at any cost patho to hea.
PL 1SG EXT.EXACT [growth at any cost] EXT.EXACT.CLOSE PST hear.
(We heard the phrase "growth at any cost.")

B: lo mia ha reo shareo.
PL 1SG PROX meaning discuss.
(We discuss this meaning.)

A: mia ma nawo.
1SG NEG agree.
(I do not agree.)

B: muila hina phelona.
earth what require.
(What does the earth require?)

A: muila phialu nela theama phelona.
earth water COORD care require.
(The earth requires water and care.)

B: thelao lo mia muila na shorupo.
CONS PL 1SG earth NEC protect.
(Therefore, we must protect the earth.)
```

The exact frame preserves the wording being criticized. The following core sentences analyze rather than euphemize it. The last inference is still contestable: a future revision should ask whose obligations follow, what protection means, and whether the premises are sufficient.

## 5. Definition under pressure

```
A: ha relo hina reo.
PROX symbol what meaning.
(What does this symbol mean?)

B: ha relo shea reo.
PROX symbol peace meaning.
(This symbol means peace.)

A: pi no reo lilea.
POL IMP meaning clarify.
(Please clarify the meaning.)

B: shea miona lorea.
peace person connect.
(Peace connects people.)

A: mia shelomui. whekai mia ma nawo.
1SG understand. CONTR 1SG NEG agree.
(I understand. However, I do not agree.)

B: phisu sila shea ma phaelo.
EXMP community peace NEG feel.
(For example, the community does not feel peace.)

A: shorela shea ma theula miona lorea.
REPH peace NEG UNIV person connect.
(In other words, peace does not connect every person.)

B: mia nawo.
1SG agree.
(I agree.)
```

This dialogue distinguishes understanding from agreement and treats a definition as revisable. It also exposes a weakness: `shea miona lorea` may be a claim about peace rather than a definition of it. The capability matrix therefore keeps definition under active testing.

## Regression method

After any grammar or philosophical-vocabulary change, validate this file, read every Phi line aloud, paraphrase each section into plain English without looking at its translation, and record any lost distinction. A change passes only if it improves its motivating passage without silently changing the other four.
