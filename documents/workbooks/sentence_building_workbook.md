# Phi Sentence Building Workbook

## Introduction

Welcome! This workbook is designed for one purpose: to build your "muscle memory" for the unique sentence structure of Phi. Fluency isn't just about knowing words; it's about feeling the natural flow and rhythm of a language's grammar.

The exercises here are focused on repetition and pattern recognition. Don't worry about memorizing vocabulary. Focus on the **order** of the words. By the end of this workbook, the Subject-Object-Verb pattern and the use of postpositions should feel much more natural.

`nosa pholea!` (Create mindfully!)

---

## Chapter 1: The Core Pattern (S-V and S-O-V)

**Concept:** The verb always comes last.
- **Subject-Verb:** `mia nima` (I sleep)
- **Subject-Object-Verb:** `sha thola kela` (The other understands the story)

---

### Drill 1: Recognition (Phi to English)

*Translate the following simple sentences. Focus on identifying the Subject, Object, and Verb.*

1.  `weo wepu.`
2.  `thi lothea shipe.`
3.  `sha wela hisu.`
4.  `mia minu shele.`
5.  `weola sela lothea.`

---

### Drill 2: Unscrambling

*Arrange the following words into a grammatically correct Phi sentence.*

1.  `[ nima, sha ]`
2.  `[ weo, shalai, kela ]`
3.  `[ lothea, pi, thi, hisu ]` (Hint: Remember Slot 0 particles!)
4.  `[ thola, to, mia, pholea ]` (Hint: Remember Slot 1 particles!)
5.  `[ ma, weola, kela ]`

---

### Drill 3: Construction (English to Phi)

*Translate the following English sentences into Phi. Remember to put the verb at the end!*

1.  I understand peace.
2.  The community helps the friend.
3.  You saw love.
4.  We create a story.
5.  The other does not sleep.

---

## Chapter 2: Introducing Postpositions

**Concept:** In Phi, words that describe relationships (like "in," "than," "with") come *after* the noun they modify. This is the head-final rule.
- `hauno mu` (house in)
- `noshale thele` (garden than)

---

### Drill 1: Phrase Recognition

*Translate the following phrases.*

1.  `shiro mu`
2.  `whelea nua`
3.  `wothe ro`
4.  `weola phelo`
5.  `sutha leshoa`

---

### Drill 2: Sentence Construction

*Translate the following English sentences into Phi, using postpositions.*

1.  I feel well in the garden. (`wela hisu` = to feel well)
2.  The friend came from the forest.
3.  She builds with stone. (`keto` = stone)
4.  We work for the community. (`we` = for)
5.  He is without fear. (`ketho` = fear)

---

## Chapter 3: Integrating Adverbials

**Concept:** Adverbs (describing when, where, or how an action happens) are placed before the object and the verb. The basic pattern is `S [Adverb] O V`.

---

### Drill: Sentence Expansion

*Take the base sentence `mia thola pholea` (I create a story) and insert the following adverbs correctly.*

1.  `thea` (today)
2.  `nosa` (mindfully)
3.  `lona` (slowly)
4.  `thea nosa` (today, mindfully)

---

## Chapter 4: Putting It All Together

**Concept:** Now let's combine all the elements we've practiced.

---

### Drill: Full Translation

*Translate these more complex sentences into Phi. Take your time and build the sentence piece by piece.*

1.  Today, the community slowly and mindfully creates a beautiful story.
2.  The friend with the book came from the peaceful garden. (`phino` = book)
3.  We do not want to see the old house. (`hauno to` = old house)
4.  Please understand my story about the forest.
5.  (Question) Does the community work for peace?
