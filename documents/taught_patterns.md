# The taught patterns

Every sentence pattern the primer teaches, in one inventory: the pattern's shape, where it is taught, a worked example with its gloss, and the seed of its explanation. Nothing here is new — this document gathers what the twenty-six primer chapters spread across a household's days, so that anything teaching or checking Phi (a person, the validator, the transmutation teacher tool this document was written to feed) can find the whole curriculum in one place. Examples are drawn from the primer wherever possible and are validator-checked, gloss lines included.

Notation: square brackets mark slots (`[subject]`, `[place]`); everything else is literal. The Slot 1 particles stack in the canon order — tense, aspect, voice, evidentiality, modality, negation — one per rank; manner descriptors stand after the Slot 1 block, immediately before the verb.

## I. Being and doing

**P01 · Predication.** `[subject] [predicate] nai.` — the quiet "is," arriving last. The predicate is an adjective, a noun, or a place phrase. Taught in ch. 1.

```
womu thiku nai.
home small be.
(The home is small.)
```

**P02 · The intransitive sentence.** `[subject] [verb].` — doer, then doing. Taught in ch. 2.

```
lopia thalo.
child walk.
(The child walks.)
```

**P03 · The transitive sentence.** `[subject] [object] [verb].` — the acted-on stands between doer and doing; order alone tells who acts. Taught in ch. 2.

```
phao nuora pilewa.
parent food make.
(The parent makes food.)
```

## II. The noun phrase

**P04 · Plural.** `lo [noun]` — one small word makes many. Taught in ch. 3.

```
lo pelori meliho.
PL bird sing.
(The birds sing.)
```

**P05 · The describer before the noun.** `[adjective] [noun]` — announce the quality, deliver the thing. Stackable. Taught in ch. 3.

```
thiku pelori meliho.
small bird sing.
(A small bird sings.)
```

**P06 · Possession.** `[owner] [owned]` — the owner stands before the owned; position is the whole grammar, for pronouns and nouns alike. Taught in ch. 5.

```
lopia nuwera welao nai.
child bed good be.
(The child's bed is good.)
```

**P07 · All and none.** `theula [noun]` gathers every one of a kind; `mawha [noun]` empties it. Taught in ch. 12 and 13.

```
mawha nuora mua wonepa nai.
NONE food LOC table be.
(There is no food on the table.)
```

**P08 · Counting.** `[numeral] [classifier] [noun]` — how many, of what nature, then the thing. Classifiers: himo (people), lipha (living things), themo (objects), nophe (ideas, times, events). Taught in ch. 15.

```
wi himo piru mua silawo nai.
two HUM.CLF trader LOC village be.
(Two traders are in the village.)
```

## III. Questions, answers, address

**P09 · The plain question.** `wa [sentence]` — the statement with `wa` set before it; nothing moves. Taught in ch. 4.

```
wa lopia nulae.
Q child sleep.
(Is the child asleep?)
```

**P10 · Answers.** `lia.` and `tua.` — yes and no, each a complete utterance, usually followed by the confirming sentence. Taught in ch. 4.

**P11 · The spoken ceremony.** `kia.` `mea.` `pao.` `whelani.` — greeting, thanks, parting, welcome: full sentences of one word each, with their own periods. Taught in ch. 4 and 6.

**P12 · The call.** `kona [ne] [name or role].` — the vocative stands outside the sentence, its own utterance; `ne` joins it when a name follows. Taught in ch. 16.

```
kona ne sulae. whelani.
VOC NAME sulae. welcome.
(sulae — welcome.)
```

**P13 · Naming.** `[bearer] nomei ne [name] nai.` — a name is announced by `ne` and glossed as itself, never translated. Taught in ch. 16.

```
melu nomei ne sulae nai.
friend name NAME sulae be.
(The friend's name is sulae.)
```

**P14 · Politeness first.** `pi` at the very front, before even `wa` — respect announced before anything else. Taught in ch. 14 and 16.

```
pi wa mia po mua luphore wishe.
POL Q 1SG POT LOC river swim.
(Please — may I swim in the river?)
```

**P15 · Also.** `we [constituent]` — "also," standing before the thing it adds. Taught in ch. 16 and 17.

```
we lohau nuola.
ALSO dog eat.
(The dog eats too.)
```

## IV. Relations

**P16 · Place.** `mua [place]` — in, at, on; the relation announced, then the place, then the sentence goes on. Taught in ch. 5.

```
misheko mua nuwera nulae.
cat LOC bed sleep.
(The cat sleeps in a bed.)
```

**P17 · Here and there.** `[subject] ha nai.` / `[subject] ra nai.` — the deictics stand alone as locative predicates. Taught in ch. 5.

```
misheko ra nai.
cat DIST be.
(The cat is there.)
```

**P18 · Source.** `lue [place]` — from. Taught in ch. 6.

```
lopia lue womu wepu.
child ABL home go.
(The child goes from home.)
```

**P19 · Recipient.** `wei [receiver]` before the gift and the giving — the giving-sentence, Phi's favorite. Taught in ch. 6.

```
melu wei lopia peloru loa.
friend DAT child flower give.
(The friend gives the child a flower.)
```

**P20 · Goal.** `kau [place]` — toward; the destination announced before a step is taken. Taught in ch. 14.

```
lo mia kau luphore wepu.
PL 1SG ALL river go.
(We go to the river.)
```

## V. Time and aspect

**P21 · Past.** `to [verb]` — the event is over; the particle touches the verb. Taught in ch. 7.

```
pheralu to lepa.
rain PST fall.
(Rain fell.)
```

**P22 · Future.** `so [verb]` — the doing is still ahead; same position. Taught in ch. 8.

```
melu so shua.
friend FUT come.
(The friend will come.)
```

**P23 · Aspect.** `si` ongoing · `ki` complete · `te` ceasing · `pa` beginning · `ro` habitual — one rank, one at a time, after tense if both appear. Taught in ch. 9–11.

```
phao moru ki shiroka.
parent wall PFV repair.
(The parent has repaired the wall.)
```

**P24 · Manner.** `[descriptor] [verb]` — a describing word before a doing describes the doing, standing inside the Slot 1 block, immediately before the verb. Taught in ch. 11 and 18.

```
wheo maeli whunei.
elder quiet breathe.
(The elder breathes quietly.)
```

**P25 · The journal date-line.** A time phrase set down alone, then plain sentences: `ha philo.` today, `luera philo.` yesterday, `wireo philo.` tomorrow, `theula philo.` every day, `nosa.` now. Taught in ch. 12.

```
ha philo. womu maeli nai.
PROX day. home quiet be.
(Today. The house is quiet.)
```

## VI. Negation, permission, request

**P26 · Not.** `ma [verb]` — after the time particles, touching the verb; works on `nai` too. Taught in ch. 13.

```
pheralu ma lepa.
rain NEG fall.
(No rain falls.)
```

**P27 · The gentle imperative.** `no [sentence without subject].` — handed to whoever is listening. Taught in ch. 14.

```
no ponu tapu.
IMP door close.
(Close the door.)
```

**P28 · May and must.** `po [verb]` may, can · `na [verb]` must — and stacked with negation: `po ma` cannot, `na ma` must not. Taught in ch. 14.

```
lopia mua luphore sonu na ma wishe.
child LOC river alone NEC NEG swim.
(The child must not swim in the river alone.)
```

## VII. Joining

**P29 · And, or.** `[X] nela [Y]` joins without ranking; `[X] sola [Y]` offers a choice. Taught in ch. 18.

```
wa siora sulopa sola milura nuola.
Q siora soup DISJ milk eat.
(Will siora have soup or milk?)
```

**P30 · Together.** `nuawe [verb]` — togetherness as manner, before the verb like `maeli`. Taught in ch. 18.

```
lo mia nuawe riola.
PL 1SG together labor.
(We work together.)
```

## VIII. Sentences inside sentences

**P31 · The embedded statement.** `mena [sentence] meno` — a whole sentence bracketed into a thing that can be known, said, told, felt; it keeps its own tense. Taught in ch. 19.

```
siora mena ne sulae so shua meno shane.
siora DECL.COMP NAME sulae FUT come DECL.COMP.CLOSE tell.
(siora tells that sulae will come.)
```

**P32 · The embedded question.** `wela [sentence] welo` — whether, held without asking; precise about which question one cannot answer. Taught in ch. 20.

```
lopia wela pheralu so lepa welo ma sano.
child INT.COMP rain FUT fall INT.COMP.CLOSE NEG know.
(The child does not know whether it will rain.)
```

**P33 · The quotation.** `shola [exact words] sholo` — the words themselves, pronouns, tenses, and commands intact; the frame closes with a verb of speaking or hearing. Taught in ch. 21.

```
mia shola muila theula howela sholo ro remo.
1SG QUOT.COMP earth UNIV receive QUOT.COMP.CLOSE HAB think.
(I still think over her words: the earth receives all.)
```

**P34 · The describing clause.** `rena [clause] [noun]` — a little sentence missing its doer, and the noun steps in at the end to be it; no closer needed. Taught in ch. 22.

```
rena sulopa ro pilewa miona.
REL soup HAB make person.
(The person who habitually makes soup.)
```

## IX. Conditions

**P35 · If.** `lu [condition]. [consequence].` — the condition first, its own small sentence; the consequence follows. Taught in ch. 23.

```
lu pheralu lepa. lo mia mua womu therilu.
COND rain fall. PL 1SG LOC home rest.
(If rain falls, we rest at home.)
```

**P36 · The unreal if.** `lu he [condition]. [consequence].` — wishes and never-weres; one extra syllable buys the imagination. Taught in ch. 23.

```
lu he mia pelori nai. mia wapi.
COND IRR 1SG bird be. 1SG fly.
(If I were a bird, I would fly.)
```

## X. Evidence

**P37 · How you know.** `hi` witnessed · `ke` inferred · `ti` told · `ho` assumed — the evidentials stand in the Slot 1 block and make every statement honest about its source; the unmarked sentence claims no source at all. Taught in ch. 24.

```
mia suliwa hi nila.
1SG snake DIR see.
(I saw the snake myself.)
```

## XI. The wish

**P38 · The optative.** `su [sentence]` — the mood of wishes and blessings, named in the capstone and taught by the texts; the Metta Sutta runs on it. Slot 0, like `wa` and `no` and `lu`.

```
su sila shea nai.
OPT community peace be.
(May the community be at peace.)
```

## Coverage notes

The primer also teaches, without needing patterns of their own: the pronouns `mia`, `thia`, and the many-I `lo mia` (we); nested announcements (`mua mia nuwera` — whose-grammar inside where-grammar, ch. 5); embedded `sola` choices inside `wela … welo` (ch. 20); and the stacking of everything above, since every pattern composes with every other by the one law — announce, then deliver. Transmutation itself — the reframing of concepts rather than the substitution of words — is deliberately not a pattern: it is judgment, taught by the texts, and no inventory can hold it.
