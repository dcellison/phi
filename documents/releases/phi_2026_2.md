# Phi 2026.2: Productive Name Forms

**Status:** ready for maintainer review
**Date:** 2026-07-10

## Purpose

Phi names are now an open grammatical class. A speaker may place one chosen, content-shaped Phi-form onym directly after `ne` without creating a dictionary entry or wrapping the name as guest material on every occurrence.

## Settled behavior

- `ne samira` is a proper-name atom even though `samira` is not in the lexicon.
- A productive onym is one lowercase token of two, three, or four legal Phi syllables, with no duplicated onset-bearing syllable.
- The form receives no gloss, part of speech, semantic rationale, pillar claim, or automatic lexicon status.
- Listed content words remain valid names under `ne`.
- Function words, interjections, and grammatical boundaries are reserved.
- The lexicon minimal-pair ratchet does not govern the open class of personal names.
- Five-or-more-syllable, multi-token, or otherwise non-onym adapted forms continue to use `hasha … hasho`; source-exact forms continue to use `patha … patho`.
- Formal, neutral, portable, and validated Phi keeps `ne`; established conversation may omit it while reference remains unambiguous.

## Compatibility

No existing name construction is removed. Content-word names, guest names, exact names, honorific chains, and the primer household's threshold style remain valid. The threshold pattern is now described as an optional cultural practice built on established discourse rather than a universal rule about family, presence, or death.

The lexicon remains at 937 entries.

## Tooling

- `scripts/name_forms.py` implements the productive onym charter and name-atom scope.
- `scripts/validate_examples.py` accepts valid marked onyms, carries them unchanged into glosses, rejects every listed non-content form as a name, and continues to reject unmarked unknown tokens. Its `name FORM` command validates a proposed onym before publication.
- `scripts/tengwar.py` recognizes and renders short lines containing productive names.
- `scripts/test_name_forms.py` provides focused regression coverage and runs in CI.
