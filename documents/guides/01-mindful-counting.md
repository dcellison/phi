# The Art of Speaking Phi, Part 1: Mindful Counting

Welcome to the Style & Nuance guides. While other documents explain *what* is grammatically correct, these guides explain *why* and *when* to use certain forms to speak Phi with more grace, mindfulness, and precision.

## The Mindful Act of Counting (Noun Classifiers)

In Phi, the act of counting can be more than just stating a number. By using an optional **noun classifier**, you can express a deeper respect for the nature of the thing you are counting.

While it is always grammatically correct to say `[Number] [Noun]`, adding a classifier is a deliberate act of mindfulness.

### The Core Classifiers

Phi uses a minimal set of four core classifiers:

- **`himo`**: For people.
- **`lipha`**: For other living things (plants, animals).
- **`themo`**: For inanimate objects.
- **`nophe`**: For abstract concepts (ideas, stories).

### When and Why to Use a Classifier

The choice to use a classifier is a stylistic one that adds nuance.

#### 1. To Show Respect

Using `himo` when counting people is a powerful sign of respect. It acknowledges their full personhood beyond any role or number.

- **Simple:** `tho lo whelea` (three friends)
- **Mindful/Respectful:** `tho himo whelea` (three-person-friend)

#### 2. To Add Poetic or Philosophical Emphasis

Using a classifier can draw attention to the nature of a thing.

- **Simple:** `shi sula` (four plants)
- **Poetic:** `shi lipha sula` (four-lifeform-plant)
  - This phrasing emphasizes that you see the plants not just as objects, but as vibrant, living beings.

- **Simple:** `shu thola` (one story)
- **Philosophical:** `shu nophe thola` (one-concept-story)
  - This phrasing highlights the story's nature as an abstract idea or a seed of thought.

#### 3. For Simple Clarity

Classifiers can sometimes be used for simple clarity, especially when discussing a mix of different kinds of things.

- `wha themo pota` (two-thing-table -> two tables)

Think of classifiers not as a rule you must follow, but as a tool you can use to make your speech more beautiful, precise, and aligned with the mindful spirit of Phi.
