# The Art of Speaking Phi, Part 3: The Art of Conversation

This guide covers two simple but crucial tools for managing direct, face-to-face conversation in a way that is clear, graceful, and respectful.

## 1. Direct Address (`ko`)

When you wish to get someone's attention or make it clear you are speaking *to* them, you use the vocative particle `ko`. It is placed at the very beginning of your utterance, before all other particles.

**Think of it as "O, friend" or "Hey, friend."**

- **Example:**
  ```
  ko whelea, wa thi wepu?
  VOC friend, Q 2SG go?
  (Friend, are you going?)
  ```

- **Without the vocative:** `wa thi wepu?` (Are you going?)

Using `ko` is not always mandatory if the context is clear, but it is a polite and direct way to initiate conversation or single someone out in a group.

## 2. Overall Politeness (`pi`)

The politeness particle `pi` is one of the most important tools for mindful communication in Phi. Placing `pi` at the beginning of a sentence (after `ko`, if present) softens the entire statement. It changes the tone from a direct assertion to a gentle, respectful offering.

It is absolutely essential for making polite requests.

- **A Direct Command (can sound forceful):**
  ```
  no minu shele
  IMP family help
  (Help the family.)
  ```

- **A Polite Request:**
  ```
  pi no minu shele
  POL IMP family help
  (Please, help the family.)
  ```

You can also use `pi` to soften a statement or question, especially when discussing a sensitive topic or asking for a personal opinion. It signals that you are trying to be mindful of the other person's feelings.

- **A direct question:** `wa thi nolou hisu?` (Are you feeling sad?)
- **A more gentle, respectful question:** `pi wa thi nolou hisu?` (If you don't mind my asking, are you feeling sad?)

Mastering `ko` and `pi` will allow you to navigate conversations with the grace and respect that are central to the Phi worldview.
