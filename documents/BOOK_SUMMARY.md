# Phi Language Book Summary

This document summarizes the content of the Phi language book (`/book/`) for reference during development.

---

## Structure Overview

The book has **THREE PARTS**:

| Part | Title | Chapters | Focus |
|------|-------|----------|-------|
| 1 | The Soul of the Language | 1-7 | Philosophy |
| 2 | The Grammar of Being | 8-12 | Practical grammar |
| 3 | Weaving Thoughts Together | 13 | Complex structures |

## Part 1: The Soul of the Language (Philosophy)

### Chapter 1: An Invitation to a More Mindful World
- Language as invisible architecture shaping worldview
- SVO languages encode individualism and hierarchical agency
- Combat metaphors dominate disagreement discourse
- Alternative structures can foster different values

### Chapter 2: The Five Pillars of Wisdom

**2.1 Solarpunk Values**
- Radical, pragmatic optimism
- Sustainability as symbiosis
- Decentralized power, beauty as core value
- Key vocab: `hashua` (cycle), `sila` (community), `wela` (good/beautiful), `phalowa` (solar energy)

**2.2 Buddhist-Inspired Concepts**
- Mindfulness (`nosa`) as grammatical practice
- Right Speech and compassionate expression
- Dependent origination - everything interconnected
- Key vocab: `nosa` (mindfulness), `lothea` (compassion/love), `phale` (boundary/anger), `sui` (fear as alertness)

**2.3 Art Nouveau Aesthetics**
- Organic curves, "whiplash line"
- Total work of art integrated throughout life
- Key vocab: `shilei` (justice), `thepalu` (garden), `lopia` (beautiful), `shiro` (forest)

**2.4 Peace Linguistics Practices**
- Grammar of nonviolence
- Requests preferred over commands
- Passive voice de-escalates blame
- Key vocab: `no` (request), `se` (passive), `shilei` (justice as restoration), `shephoa` (forgiveness)

**2.5 Pre-Industrial Wisdom**
- Universal animacy - no hard who/what distinction
- Cyclical time vs. linear time
- Natural prototypes for categories
- Classifiers: `himo` (human), `lipha` (living), `themo` (inanimate), `nophe` (abstract)
- Colors: `sutha` (yellow), `shupha` (red), `lipha` (green), `lisho` (blue), `muila` (brown)

### Chapter 3: The Philosophy of Sound
- Phonetics as applied philosophy
- Fricatives (ph, th, sh, wh) for abstract concepts
- Liquids (l, r, w) for flow and connection
- Nasals (m, n) for grounding
- Vowel symbolism: `a` (presence), `i` (insight), `u` (grounding), `e` (balance), `o` (harmony)

### Chapter 4: The Philosophy of Meaning
- Compositional transparency
- Living web of concepts (mycelial network)
- Words as seeds that grow
- Examples: `lothea` = lo + thea, `thela` = no + thela

### Chapter 5: The Philosophy of Grammar
- SOV enforces reflection
- Verb-final creates "sacred space" for conscious choice
- Three-slot particle system
- Head-final principle throughout

### Chapter 6: The Social Philosophy of Language
- Language as community architecture
- Respect without hierarchy
- Conversation as collaborative creation
- Evidential system promotes intellectual humility

### Chapter 7: The Philosophy of Learning
- Learning as sacred journey
- Grammar as contemplative practice
- Seasons of learning (planting, growth, integration, harvest)
- Transformation through practice

## Part 2: The Grammar of Being (Practical)

### Chapter 8: The Sounds of Phi

**Vowels** (all pure, no diphthongs):
- `a` /ä/ - presence, acceptance
- `e` /e̞/ - balance, equilibrium
- `i` /i/ - insight, clarity
- `o` /o̞/ - warm harmony
- `u` /u/ - grounding, depth

**Consonants** (10 simple):
- Stops: `p`, `t` (dental), `k`
- Nasals: `m`, `n` (dental)
- Liquids: `l`, `r`
- Sibilant: `s`
- Ephemeral: `w`, `h`

**Fricative Digraphs** (4 - abstract concepts):
- `ph` /ɸ/ - contemplation
- `th` /θ/ - weighing ideas
- `sh` /ʃ/ - collective voice
- `wh` /ʍ/ - vast space, community

**Golden Rule:** Vowel hiatus - adjacent vowels always separate syllables (e.g., `ha.u.no`)

### Chapter 9: The Mindful Sentence

**Three Pillars:**
1. Verb-Final Foundation (SOV)
2. Particle System (transparent signposts)
3. Framing Principle (intention first)

**Particle System:**
- Slot 0: `wa` (question), `no` (request), `lu` (conditional), `pi` (politeness)
- Slot 1: Tense (`to`/`so`), Aspect (`ki`/`si`/`pa`/`te`), Voice (`se`/`ka`), Modality (`po`/`na`)
- Slot 2: `lo` (plural), `ha`/`ra` (deixis), `ko` (focus)

### Chapter 10: Nouns and Their Modifiers
- Head-final: modifiers before noun
- Possession: possessor before possessed
- Zero-article system
- Quantifiers: `theula` (all), `sheloi` (many), `soli` (several), `phina` (few), `mawha` (none)

### Chapter 11: Verbs and the Flow of Time
- Present unmarked (mindful default)
- Past `to` - completed events
- Future `so` - possibility/intention
- Aspects: `ki` (progressive), `si` (perfective), `pa` (inchoative), `te` (habitual)

### Chapter 12: Verbs of Voice and Modality
- Passive `se` - focus on receiver, de-escalates blame
- Causative `ka` - explicit chains of influence
- Possibility `po` - epistemic humility
- Necessity `na` - logical requirement

## Part 3: Weaving Thoughts Together

### Chapter 13: Coordination and Discourse

**Simple Conjunctions:**
- `nela` - and (harmonic combination)
- `thona` - but (balanced contrast)
- `sola` - or (open possibility)

**Correlatives:**
- `nela...nela` - both-and
- `sola...sola` - either-or
- `ma...thona` - not-but
- `shai...nela` - graduated

**Discourse Adverbs:**
- `thelao` - therefore
- `kewai` - however
- `sheno` - furthermore
- `phisu` - for example
