# Philosophical Conversation and Repair

These are recommended conventions built from existing Phi resources. They are not new particles or proof of spontaneous usage. The maintainer should use them in original dialogues, revise them when they fail, and coin nothing until a recurring repair need cannot be served compositionally.

## Core repair sequence

1. Mark the gap without shame.
2. Ask whether the problem is hearing, wording, meaning, evidence, or agreement.
3. Reformulate one claim rather than repeating an entire argument unchanged.
4. Confirm understanding separately from agreement.
5. Retract or suspend a claim explicitly when necessary.

## Recommended forms

### General pardon or repetition request

```
hao.
pardon.
(Pardon? Please say that again or clarify.)
```

`hao` deliberately leaves the kind of gap to context. Follow it with a more specific request when repetition alone would not help.

### Clarify the meaning

```
pi no reo lilea.
POL IMP meaning clarify.
(Please clarify the meaning.)
```

### State non-knowledge or uncertainty

```
mia ma sano.
1SG NEG know.
(I do not know.)
```

This is the preferred suspension form until corpus use demonstrates a need to distinguish confidence levels lexically. `ho` may mark an assumption, but assumption is not a general uncertainty scale.

### Admit error and reformulate

```
mia to kanelu. shorela mia ma sano.
1SG PST err. REPH 1SG NEG know.
(I erred. In other words, I do not know.)
```

### Withdraw a prior formulation

```
mia phelui naweri.
1SG word refuse.
(I withdraw my word.)
```

This is intentionally marked provisional: `naweri` is stronger than English *retract*. Use it in the regression corpus and replace the convention if speakers consistently hear moral refusal rather than withdrawal.

### Confirm understanding without agreement

```
mia shelomu. whekai mia ma nawo.
1SG understand. CONTR 1SG NEG agree.
(I understand. However, I do not agree.)
```

### Invite a response and hand off the turn

```
mia ki haolu. thia po lonae.
1SG PFV speak. 2SG POT respond.
(I have spoken. You may respond.)
```

### Request time or suspend discussion

```
pi no hasi.
POL IMP wait.
(Please wait.)
```

A fuller suspension should state what is waiting: a decision, more evidence, rest, or another person's contribution.

### Consent and refusal

```
mia nawo.
1SG agree.
(I agree.)

mia naweri.
1SG refuse.
(I refuse.)
```

Agreement with a proposition should use `mena … meno`; agreement with a person uses `nua`. Bare forms rely on an already established topic.

### Request a source form

```
pi wa thia phelui po haolu.
POL Q 2SG word POT speak.
(Could you say the word?)
```

If spelling, script, or notation matters, present the source artifact separately in writing or establish an outside spelling convention in conversation. Phi does not yet have a core verb meaning *spell*; repeated need for finer requests belongs in the capability log and may motivate ordinary coinage.

## Argument practice

A complete practice dialogue should include a claim, its evidential status, a request for clarification, an example, an objection, a reformulation, a separate understanding check, and either agreement, disagreement, or suspended judgment. No exercise should require consensus as the only successful ending.

## Known gaps

Current conventions do not cleanly distinguish retraction from refusal, example from counterexample, logical consequence from observed consequence, confidence levels, or temporary topic suspension from ending a discussion. These are corpus tasks. Do not solve them by adding particles until several original dialogues show the same structural need.
