# 4. Coordination and Subordination

This document covers the rules for connecting clauses to form more complex sentences, either by joining them as equals (coordination) or by making one dependent on the other (subordination).

## 4.1. Coordination
Coordination links two or more equivalent grammatical elements.

**Basic (`nela`):** Used for "and."
```
mia sela nela weola lothea
1SG peace COORD community love
(I love peace and community.)
```

**Adversative (`thoa`):** Used for "but."
```
mia lothea kela thoa sha ma kela
1SG love understand BUT 3SG NEG understand
(I understand love, but the other does not.)
```

**Alternative (`soa`):** Used for "or."
```
wa [sha miro] soa [philo]
Q [3SG beautiful] OR [calm]
(Is it beautiful or calm?)
```

### 4.1.1. Correlative Constructions
To add emphasis to a coordinated phrase, a logical adverb may be placed before the main verb.

- **Both... and...:** Use `nela` to link the items and add the adverb `sila` (together).
  ```
  mia [sela nela weola] sila lothea
  1SG [peace and community] together love
  (I love both peace and community.)
  ```
- **Either... or...:** Use `soa` to link the items and add the adverb `sona` (as a choice).
  ```
  thi [thola soa sela] sona po shele
  2SG [story or peace] as-a-choice POSS receive
  (You can have either the story or the peace.)
  ```
- **Neither... nor...:** Use `soa` and `sona`, and negate the main verb with `ma`.
  ```
  mia [thola soa sela] sona ma kela
  1SG [story or peace] as-a-choice NEG understand
  (I understand neither the story nor the peace.)
  ```

### 4.1.2. Discourse Adverbs
To connect sentences and signal the logical relationship between them, Phi uses a class of discourse adverbs. These adverbs are placed at the beginning of a sentence to modify its meaning in relation to the preceding context.

**Sentence Position:** Discourse adverbs appear *after* any Slot 0 sentence frame particle (like `wa` or `pi`) but *before* the subject of the sentence. The standard order is: `[Slot 0 Particle] [Discourse Adverb] [Subject]...`

**Common Discourse Adverbs:**
- `thelao` (therefore, for this reason)
- `kewai` (however, on the other hand)
- `sheno` (in addition, furthermore)
- `phisu` (for example, for instance)

## 4.2. Subordination
Subordination makes one clause dependent on another.

### 4.2.1. Indirect Speech (Clause as Object)
To report the content of speech indirectly, the reported statement is treated as the direct object of a verb of speech or thought.
```
mia [thi to thopa] kela
1SG [2SG PST speak] understand
(I understand what you said.)
```

### 4.2.2. Direct Speech (Quotation)
To report speech verbatim, the quotation is treated as the direct object, marked by the preceding Slot 2 quotative particle `sho`.
```
sha sho "thino wela" thopa
3SG QUOT "the sun is beautiful" say
(She says, "The sun is beautiful.")
```

### 4.2.3. Temporal Clauses (head-final)
A temporal clause is formed by stating the clause and then marking it with a preposition that functions as a temporal subordinator.
```
[mia pholea] pheo mia sela hisu
[1SG create] AFTER 1SG peace feel
(After I create, I feel peace.)

[mia shele] phoe mia nolu
[1SG help] BEFORE 1SG think
(Before I help, I think.)
```

### 4.2.4. Causal and Concessive Clauses
Causal ("because") and concessive ("although") clauses are formed by using the prepositions `lao` and `shai` respectively, followed by a nominalized clause (using `nai`).

**Structure:** `[Preposition [Clause] nai], [Main Clause]`

- **Causal:**
  ```
  [sha to wepu] nai lao, mia nolou hisu
  [3SG PST leave] NOM BECAUSE, 1SG sadness feel
  (Because she left, I feel sad.)
  ```
- **Concessive:**
  ```
  [shiro to wela ma sio] nai shai, mia ketho ma hisu
  [forest PST beautiful NEG be] NOM ALTHOUGH, 1SG fear NEG feel
  (Although the forest was not beautiful, I felt no fear.)
  ```

### 4.2.5. Indirect Questions
An indirect question is formed by using a clause containing an interrogative pronoun (e.g., `sua`, `kua`) as the direct object of a verb like `kela` (to know) or `kewha` (to ask). The main sentence remains a statement.

**Structure:** `S [S-interrogative O V] V`

**Examples:**
- **Expressing knowledge of a question:**
  ```
  mia [sua to wepu] kela
  1SG [who PST leave] know
  (I know who left.)
  ```
- **Expressing uncertainty:**
  ```
  mia [kua thola sio] ma kela
  1SG [where story is] NEG know
  (I don't know where the story is.)
  ```
