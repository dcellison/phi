# Phi Preposition Reference

This document is the authoritative inventory of Phi's 27 prepositions, completing the reference set beside the particle, complementizer, and numeral references. For the rulings that govern them, see `/canon.md`; for the teaching, see manual ch11 (the words of place) and ch19 (prepositions over clauses).

## The shape and the position

Every preposition is a hiatus disyllable, (C)V.V, the relator shape of the function-word charter. Every preposition precedes its object (modifier-first), and the whole phrase stands in the adjunct slot: S PP O V, circumstances before the thing acted on. When the relativized element is a preposition's object, the preposition remains and its object is gapped (`rena mia mua ___ to thalo shelira`, the forest that I walked in); the preposition never drops and never moves.

Prepositions over clauses are the four clause-relators `lao` (BECAUSE), `pheo` (POST), `phoe` (ANT), `shai` (CONC), documented with the subordinate-clause grammar; `pheo` and `phoe` also stand over counted time noun phrases (`pheo wi philo`, in two days).

## Place

| Word | Gloss | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| `mua` | LOC | in, inside, at (never a motion endpoint) | `mia mua shero sheluo.` (I listen in the night.) |
| `nia` | ON | on, upon, surface contact | `shumu nia kerou thuroa.` (The moss grows on the stone.) |
| `leo` | ABOVE | above, over | `neparu leo tomora nai.` (The cloud is above the mountain.) |
| `phou` | BELOW | under, below | `lo shalu phou repha wepu.` (The fish pass below the bridge.) |
| `neo` | FRONT | in front of, facing | `wheo neo womu nai.` (The elder is in front of the house.) |
| `noe` | BEHIND | behind, at the rear of | `misheko noe tomi nulae.` (The cat sleeps behind the pot.) |
| `sio` | BESIDE | beside, alongside | `lo peloru sio ruela thuroa.` (The flowers grow beside the path.) |
| `thei` | BETWEEN | between (two) | `womu thei wi shiro nai.` (The house stands between two trees.) |
| `thoa` | AMONG | among (many) | `wheo thoa lo lopia nai.` (The elder is among the children.) |
| `roa` | AROUND | around, encircling | `lo lopia roa thero rotiku.` (The children dance around the fire.) |

## Motion

| Word | Gloss | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| `kau` | ALL | to, the goal of motion (arrival promised) | `mia kau silawo wepu.` (I go to the village.) |
| `lue` | ABL | from, source | `melu lue nitho shua.` (The friend comes from the north.) |
| `wea` | TOWARD | toward, without promised arrival | `mia wea sorae thorui thalo.` (I walk toward the east.) |
| `phei` | AWAY | away from, receding | `lohau phei thero thalo.` (The dog walks away from the fire.) |
| `muo` | INTO | into, entering | `mia muo shelira wepu.` (I go into the forest.) |
| `mue` | OUT.OF | out of, exiting | `mia mue womu wepu.` (I go out of the house.) |
| `thue` | THROUGH | through, by way of | `shalu thue phialu wepu.` (The fish goes through the water.) |

The carves are ruled: `kau` promises arrival, `wea` does not; `mua` is where an action happens and never a motion endpoint; `kamo` (arrive) takes `mua`, because arrival is an event at a place.

## Proximity

| Word | Gloss | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| `pai` | NEAR | near, close to | `shelira pai silawo nai.` (The forest is near the village.) |
| `woe` | FAR | far from | `tomora woe silawo nai.` (The mountain is far from the village.) |

What counts as near depends on scale, and the pair carries no measurements; see the natural-ruler ruling in `/canon.md`.

## Time

| Word | Gloss | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| `sui` | DUR | during, throughout | `mia sui kelua theo.` (I read during the morning.) |
| `tei` | UNTIL | until, up to | `lopia tei norawhi wile.` (The child plays until dusk.) |

With the day-cycle nouns these are Phi's time-telling; see the natural-clock ruling and manual ch14.

## Relation

| Word | Gloss | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| `nua` | COM | with, in company of | `mia nua melu thalo.` (I walk with the friend.) |
| `roe` | INS | with, by means of | `mia roe kiru wolea kati.` (I cut the wood with the chisel.) |
| `wei` | DAT | to, for (recipient or beneficiary) | `mia wei thia loami loa.` (I give you a gift.) |
| `sheo` | THAN | than, compared to | `tomora sheo shiro mo raelu nai.` (The mountain is taller than the tree.) |
| `phea` | AS | as, in the manner of | `lopia phea pelori meliho.` (The child sings like a bird.) |
| `whuo` | WITHOUT | without, in absence of | `mia whuo lumoi thalo.` (I walk without a lantern.) |

## The compass, for reference

Direction words are nouns, not prepositions, and ride inside preposition phrases: `nitho` (north) and `ronua` (south) are atomic and value-neutral; east and west are the registry compounds `sorae thorui` and `sorae lumae`, anchored to the sun because no sun-anchor is hemisphere-neutral for north and south. Usage: `wea nitho` (northward), `lue ronua` (from the south), `mua sorae thorui` (in the east).
