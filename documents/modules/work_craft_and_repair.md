# Work, Craft, and Repair

**Status:** **Established vocabulary module** with 28 module roots coined here, 70 current memberships, and base-vocabulary `winora` plan and `hasoru` pending; ordinary Phi grammar.

## Charter

This module gives Phi speakers optional vocabulary for labor, work organization, making, craft, practical learning, evaluation, material logistics, and repair. It supports discussion of skilled and shared activity without treating purposeful labor, a prior commitment, method knowledge, a technical instruction, a credential, or a repaired condition as proof of consent, competence, fairness, safety, authority, or final approval.

The module is not an employment system, occupational classification, apprenticeship contract, safety standard, quality-management system, professional credential, guild charter, or economic doctrine. It does not define which work is valuable, who must perform it, who owns its materials or result, how anyone should be compensated, or who has authority to assign, inspect, accept, or reject it.

The speaker-facing guide is [Work, Craft, and Repair](../../manual/part7_reference/modules/work_craft_and_repair.md), and the complete current optional inventory appears in the [module lexicon](../../manual/part7_reference/lexicon/by_module.md#work-craft-and-repair). The vocabulary table below records every root established for this profile, including base-vocabulary `winora` plan and `hasoru` pending. The generated inventory also includes older roots classified after the profile pass and words shared with other learning paths. Learning-path placement changes where a learner finds a word, not what it means. Canonical definitions remain in `vocabulary/content/`.

## Boundaries

- Phi retains one grammar. The module adds no task syntax, occupational classifier, command form, parser mode, inflection, or incompatible construction.
- A module word is ordinary Phi vocabulary. Speakers outside the module may ask for a core paraphrase and are not obliged to learn its specialized terms.
- `riola` names purposeful effort that contributes beyond the self and explicitly contrasts that ideal with drudgery. It must not be treated as a neutral label for coerced, alienated, exploitative, unwanted, meaningless, or harmful labor when that framing would erase the worker's judgment.
- `pilewa`, `theko`, `kealo`, and `lorima` name forms of making, skilled craft, creation, and building. They do not establish good design, demonstrated competence, consent, ecological benefit, safe practice, ownership, or responsibility for later consequences.
- `molawi`, `wenola`, `shorema`, `phowe`, `naphe`, `panoru`, and `pheloma` describe cooperation, collaboration, contribution, sharing, help, support, and service with positive or relational meanings. Their use does not prove equal power, shared control, voluntary participation, adequate compensation, or freedom to leave.
- `seru` is a self-binding commitment, `thonai` is willingly carried responsibility, `rilowa` reports assignment, and `na` marks contextual necessity. None by itself establishes legitimate authority, accepted obligation, capacity, compensation, liability, or a command that another person must obey.
- `thesani`, `pokera`, `nukesa`, `kiranu`, `sikanu`, and `kanuwa` distinguish skill, competence, expertise, qualification, certification, and authorization. None can substitute for another, and none supplies present consent, universal ability, moral worth, safe outcome, or authority over a learner.
- Pain, fatigue, inability, rest, refusal, assistance, timing, and an adapted environment remain bodily and access relations, not evidence that a person is defective or unfit. They overlap [Medical and Bodily Care](medical_and_bodily_care.md) and [Accessibility and Participation](accessibility_and_participation.md), and no diagnosis is required to state a work limit or requested change.
- `shiroka`, `whori`, `talome`, and `thiranu` distinguish repair, mending, restoration, and maintenance. The Systems module can separately name a fault, specification, inspection, failure, and reliability claim, but none of these establishes warranty, safe return to use, final approval, or acceptance by an affected person.
- `somaki` testing, `kisome` assessment, Systems `nilaki` inspection, Commons `tukiro` review, `sikanu` certification, acceptance, and approval remain distinct acts. A matching dimension, completed action, calibrated instrument, test, assessment, inspection, or written record is not automatically certification, sign-off, safe return to use, or permission.
- A `keritha`, `temu`, `laenu`, or `sila` may describe a workshop, guild, commons, or community without defining employer, owner, regulator, membership, jurisdiction, representation, decision procedure, or authority to assign work. Those relations overlap [Commons and Collective Governance](commons_and_collective_governance.md).
- Tools, devices, maintenance, technical state, and safe service overlap [Systems and Shared Infrastructure](systems_and_shared_infrastructure.md). Material sourcing, extraction, waste, reuse, and ecological consequences overlap [Ecological Systems and Material Life](ecological_systems_and_material_life.md). Domestic work and small repair overlap [Household and Daily Life](household_and_daily_life.md).
- Causal responsibility, competing values, justification, disagreement, uncertainty, and revision overlap [Philosophical Reasoning](philosophical_reasoning.md). A valued craft tradition or solarpunk intention must not choose a conclusion before the practical and social relations are examined.
- Craft knowledge belonging to a person, community, people, lineage, or knowledge tradition requires source-faithful naming and attribution when a core paraphrase would erase origin, authority, identity, restrictions, or contested history. Phi's preference for transparent composition does not authorize appropriation or universalization.

## Earlier roots classified here

Twelve older roots now share this module's learning path. `keritha` workshop and `phaewi` studio name the places. `kolai` pigment and `phanu` canvas name artistic materials, while `kiru` chisel and `weloa` loom name dedicated tools. The roles are `moloi` apprentice, `kima` scribe, `piru` trader, `nunoa` forager, and `telo` farmer; `temu` guild names their possible association. Forager and farmer also belong to Ecological Systems. Guild also belongs to Commons.

The established [Commons and Collective Governance module](commons_and_collective_governance.md) supplies `wemari` member, `sithora` role, `loatho` delegate, `karami` authority, `kanuwa` authorize, `nasholu` rule, `kanuro` decision, `phenori` ownership, `naseru` obligation, `lothoni` accountable, and `tukiro` review. These words describe the institution around work; the task and a worker's competence still need their own clauses.

## First Vocabulary Pass

The first pass admits 28 optional roots for recurring work distinctions and places plan and pending in base vocabulary. It does not attempt to reproduce an English employment vocabulary or catalogue every tool. It concentrates on how work is organized and judged, what it does to people, and where its materials came from. Exact artifacts keep their source identity and force, especially in law, economics, credentials, and safety.

| Family | Roots established for this profile | What the pass now distinguishes |
|---|---|---|
| Work units and status | `riporu` task, `rinoka` project, `winora` plan, `wepuri` progress, `hasoru` pending, `noraku` blocked, `lumethi` deadline, `thimora` schedule | A task from labor or obligation; a project from an institution or indefinite aspiration; a plan from a promise, design, schedule, or specification; progress from generic improvement; pending from unstarted; blocked from refusal or failure; deadlines and schedules from exact source timestamps and accepted commitments. |
| Coordination and transition | `rilowa` assign, `wephari` coordinate, `someru` supervise, `sethoni` handoff | Assignment from authority, acceptance, and obligation; coordination from control or supervision; supervision from legitimate rank or ownership of labor; handoff from receipt, acceptance, ownership transfer, and abandonment of accountability. |
| Skill and standing | `thesani` skill, `pokera` competent, `nukesa` expert, `kiranu` qualified | Learned practical capacity from present performance; competence from expertise, credentials, reliability, or permission; expertise from infallibility or authority; qualification from competence, authorization, and consent. |
| Learning and formal judgment | `thunaro` train, `kisome` assess, `sikanu` certify | Structured training from teaching, practice, discipline, assessment, or certification; assessment from observation, testing, approval, and judgment of human worth; certification from truth, competence, authorization, safety, and exact credential artifacts. |
| Design, testing, and evaluation | `noporu` design, `somaki` test, `kirero` quality | Design from making, planning, pattern, drawing, and exact specification; a defined test from observation, assessment, inspection, proof, or approval; criterion-relative and multidimensional quality from generic goodness, beauty, compliance, or human worth. |
| Labor conditions and power | `ritako` workload, `rimawu` drudgery, `pilora` exploit | Workload from technical load, obligation, capacity, fairness, fatigue, and harm; drudgery from all repetitive work or proven exploitation; exploitation from every use, exchange, inequality, error, or disliked result. |
| Materials and logistics | `muphera` stock, `sitawi` inventory, `sephori` supply, `katemu` offcut, `luseri` provenance | Held material from ownership, availability, and inventory; a time-bounded inventory record from the physical stock; supplying from giving, adequate provision, receipt, and control; offcuts from inherent waste; provenance from proof, ownership, authenticity, or ethical approval. |

All 30 roots established in this pass follow the complete vocabulary schema, pass the lexical validator, and have no character edit-distance-one or flagged existing phonetic neighbors in the pre-coinage audit. Twenty-eight carry `work-craft-and-repair`; `winora` and `hasoru` carry no module field and remain in base vocabulary.

## Concepts Kept Compositional or External

The pass keeps several expressions compositional because their parts expose agency, source, scope, bodily consequence, or cross-module boundaries that a single label could hide. Exact categories remain outside Phi when a translated approximation would lose their operative identity or force.

| Concept | Phi strategy | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Request and offer | Use a polite whole-clause question that names the proposed action, participant, conditions, and relevant alternative | Preserves the addressee's answer and prevents a work noun from making the act obligatory. |
| Step or work sequence | Name the ordered `lo riporu` tasks, actions, `pherami` dependencies, conditions, and transitions | Keeps a procedural step tied to the actual action instead of adding an abstract sequencing label. |
| Completion | Use existing `sholu` complete with the task or project and state the applicable criterion, remaining work, uncertainty, and affected-person acceptance separately | Completion under one criterion does not establish quality, safety, approval, payment, warranty, or the end of every responsibility. |
| Job, occupation, and profession | Use the exact source category where institutional identity matters, then describe the person's `sithora` role, tasks, skills, commitments, authority, compensation, and work relations in Phi | Prevents one broad word from collapsing employment status, vocation, social identity, credential, and current work. |
| Novice and learner status | Use `moloi` apprentice or `thunai` student where those roles fit, or state the person's current practice, skill, and competence evidence | Avoids turning limited experience in one domain into a fixed identity or judgment of worth. |
| License and exact credential | Preserve the source title, issuer, identifier, scope, and validity outside Phi; use `kiranu`, `sikanu`, and `kanuwa` only for the qualification, certification, and authorization relations that actually apply | Keeps legal or professional identity exact and prevents a credential from proving competence or consent. |
| Drawing, specification, dimension, and tolerance | Preserve the exact artifact, notation, revision, unit, and value outside Phi; use `noporu` design and Systems `kisholu` specification around it | Technical fidelity depends on the source artifact, while Phi names its practical and evidential role. |
| Inspection, approval, sign-off, and safe return to use | Use Systems `nilaki` inspection, Commons `tukiro` review and `kanuwa` authorization, module `kisome` assessment and `sikanu` certification, plus explicit acceptance and safety claims | These are different acts by potentially different people under different criteria and must not be compressed into a generic check. |
| Fatigue, ergonomic fit, accommodation, and stop-work relation | Preserve bodily self-report and use Medical or Accessibility vocabulary and Commons authority terms where applicable | Work status and technical capacity must not medicalize a person or override pain, refusal, access needs, or requested change. |
| Wage, price, payment, compensation, contract, employment, debt, profit, warranty, liability, and collective agreement | Preserve the exact source term or artifact, then describe transfers, commitments, authority claims, ownership, burdens, benefits, disagreement, and consequences in Phi | These concepts are institution-specific and often legally operative; a broad Phi paraphrase could erase material force or contested meaning. |
| Protective equipment, powered tools, software, automation, and modern technical objects | Preserve exact identities and instructions outside Phi and describe the tool, system, function, hazard, use, and work relation with core, Systems, and module vocabulary | A catalogue of modern object names would age quickly and would not replace exact technical identification. |

## Scenario Evidence

The [Work, Craft, and Repair vocabulary audit](../evaluation/work_craft_repair_vocabulary_audit.md) supplies the inventory evidence behind this organization. It distinguishes dense concrete coverage from previously thin work relations, value-loaded framing, source-material handling, and areas that still benefit from independent use.

- [The wheel, the chisel, and the promised repair](../evaluation/philosophical_test_corpus.md#15-the-wheel-the-chisel-and-the-promised-repair) is the primary before-state scenario. The module can now distinguish its repair project, tasks, plan, assignment, supervision, skills, competence, workload, testing, assessment, and handoff while retaining refusal, pain, bounded learning consent, source specifications, and unresolved safe return to use as separate relations.
- [Repair or replace](../evaluation/philosophical_test_corpus.md#2-repair-or-replace) supplies value boundary evidence: repair can be preferred for ecological and practical reasons without becoming sacred or overriding the repairer's consent, safety, or the condition of the object.
- [The marker that was not reported](../evaluation/philosophical_test_corpus.md#12-the-marker-that-was-not-reported) can now name a repair task, assignment, blocked or pending status, workload, supervision, test, quality criterion, and handoff while preserving Systems faults and inspections and Commons obligations and accountability as distinct relations.
- [A reading, a boundary, and care](../evaluation/philosophical_test_corpus.md#8-a-reading-a-boundary-and-care) and [A doorway, a route, and written words](../evaluation/philosophical_test_corpus.md#9-a-doorway-a-route-and-written-words) supply bodily and access boundary evidence: asking, refusal, self-report, assistance, and changed practical conditions remain relevant outside employment or craft institutions.
- [The fallen tree and the living grove](../evaluation/philosophical_test_corpus.md#14-the-fallen-tree-and-the-living-grove) can distinguish stock, offcuts, supply, inventory, and provenance around retained material while ecological resource, waste, habitat, and decomposition relations remain separate.
- [The Old Table](household_and_daily_life.md#before-state-scenario-the-old-table) can now frame small repair as a task or project with a plan, design, skill, test, and quality judgment while domestic labor allocation, ownership, and household hierarchy remain separate questions.

These fictional or pedagogical regression texts were written by one maintainer. They demonstrate available compositions and recurring failures; they cannot establish trade competence, procedural safety, fair labor, or another worker's acceptance of Phi's values. They remain valid before-state texts for the planned active-corpus revisit rather than being rewritten merely to display new words.

## Source Material Guidance

Keep dimensions, tolerances, formulas, material grades, component and tool identifiers, drawings, procedures, safety instructions, warnings, standards, regulations, inspection criteria, certifications, licenses, role titles, employment classifications, wages, rates, prices, contracts, schedules, records, testimony, legal categories, and terms belonging to a craft or knowledge tradition outside the Phi passage when source form matters. Preserve spelling, script, notation, precision, version, and provenance rather than silently normalizing or translating them.

Use existing vocabulary, a transparent expression, a module term, or a fully admitted new word when Phi needs the concept. A temporary pronunciation aid remains an outside conversational convention and does not acquire a Phi technical definition, credential, authority, or labor relation.

A workshop, guild, cooperative, project, product, tool, process, or other named entity may use `ne` with a listed content name or a valid Phi-form onym when the bearer or relevant naming community accepts it. The name identifies the referent; it does not translate institutional type, ownership, employment status, certification, jurisdiction, or technical function.

Describe the practical relations in Phi where useful: who proposes, requests, performs, observes, receives, or is affected by which action; what method, tool, material, condition, and source are involved; what was chosen, accepted, refused, or committed; what capacity or access change matters; what evidence supports completion or safety; what uncertainty remains; and who claims authority to decide or review. Do not claim that this analysis exhausts the separately preserved source term.

A separately presented phrase such as `qualified person`, `inspection`, `personal protective equipment`, `collective bargaining agreement`, or `24 mm +/- 0.1 mm` preserves a comparison target or operative source phrase. It does not establish qualification, complete an inspection, supply suitable protection, make an agreement voluntary or fair, prove a tolerance was met, or authorize work.

## Continued Development

Use the module in craft narratives, repair records, project discussions, learning relationships, labor criticism, shared maintenance, material histories, and stories about making. Coin another word when a recurring work concept deserves lexical presence or when the available compound is cumbersome, ambiguous, or aesthetically wrong for Phi. Retain a compound when its parts expose a useful action, criterion, source, authority claim, bodily boundary, transfer, or responsibility.

Future use should particularly test non-workshop coordination, source-faithful wage and contract disputes, household and care labor, software and automated work, protective systems, learner exit, contested certification, and material provenance across more than one community. These are opportunities for development, not admission gates for vocabulary.

Once the remaining classification decisions are settled, revisit the existing corpus as a whole. Update a text when the expanded vocabulary makes its meaning clearer, more direct, or less evasive; preserve it when the original composition remains natural or when it is intentionally retained as before-state evidence. Every later entry must still satisfy the vocabulary schema, phonology, collision checks, semantic contrasts, examples, and reference generation.
