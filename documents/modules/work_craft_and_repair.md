# Work, Craft, and Repair

**Status:** **Profile** - domain-oriented work organization, zero new roots.

## Charter

This profile gathers existing Phi resources for labor, making, craft, tools, materials, practical learning, work-sharing, maintenance, repair, measurement, safety, and decisions about completion. It tests whether speakers can describe skilled and shared activity without treating purposeful labor, a prior commitment, method knowledge, a technical instruction, or a repaired condition as proof of consent, competence, fairness, safety, or final approval.

The profile is not an employment system, occupational classification, apprenticeship contract, safety standard, quality-management system, professional credential, guild charter, or economic doctrine. It does not define which work is valuable, who must perform it, who owns its materials or result, how anyone should be compensated, or who has authority to assign, inspect, accept, or reject it.

## Boundaries

- Phi retains one grammar. Work discussion uses ordinary clauses, event nouns, coordination, evidentials, modality, conditions, discourse repair, and separately preserved source material; this profile adds no task syntax, occupational classifier, command form, parser mode, or incompatible construction.
- `riola` names purposeful effort that contributes beyond the self and explicitly contrasts that ideal with drudgery. It must not be treated as a neutral label for coerced, alienated, exploitative, unwanted, meaningless, or harmful labor when that framing would erase the worker's judgment.
- `pilewa`, `theko`, `kealo`, and `lorima` name forms of making, skilled craft, creation, and building. They do not establish good design, demonstrated competence, consent, ecological benefit, safe practice, ownership, or responsibility for later consequences.
- `molawi`, `wenola`, `shorema`, `phowe`, `naphe`, `panoru`, and `pheloma` describe cooperation, collaboration, contribution, sharing, help, support, and service with positive or relational meanings. Their use does not prove equal power, shared control, voluntary participation, adequate compensation, or freedom to leave.
- `seru` is a self-binding commitment, `thonai` is willingly carried responsibility, and `na` marks contextual necessity. None encodes an assigned task, lawful duty, role authority, liability, practical capacity, or a command that another person must obey.
- `moloi`, `thunai`, `thole`, `thumela`, `shonela`, `sano`, and `norae` can identify an apprentice, student, practice, teaching, learning, knowledge, and method. They do not define qualification, certification, demonstrated skill, supervision, permission to use a tool, or authority over a learner.
- Pain, fatigue, inability, rest, refusal, assistance, timing, and an adapted environment remain bodily and access relations, not evidence that a person is defective or unfit. They overlap [Medical and Bodily Care](medical_and_bodily_care.md) and [Accessibility and Participation](accessibility_and_participation.md), and no diagnosis is required to state a work limit or requested change.
- `shiroka`, `whori`, `talome`, and `thiranu` distinguish repair, mending, restoration, and maintenance. The Systems module can separately name a fault, specification, inspection, failure, and reliability claim, but none of these establishes warranty, safe return to use, final approval, or acceptance by an affected person.
- Measurement, observation, precision, calibration, inspection, and exact technical carriage support checking while remaining distinct acts. A matching dimension, completed action, calibrated instrument, inspection, or written record is not automatically certification, sign-off, approval, or permission for use.
- A `keritha`, `temu`, `laenu`, or `sila` may describe a workshop, guild, commons, or community without defining employer, owner, regulator, membership, jurisdiction, representation, decision procedure, or authority to assign work. Those relations overlap [Commons and Collective Governance](commons_and_collective_governance.md).
- Tools, devices, maintenance, technical state, and safe service overlap [Systems and Shared Infrastructure](systems_and_shared_infrastructure.md). Material sourcing, extraction, waste, reuse, and ecological consequences overlap [Ecological Systems and Material Life](ecological_systems_and_material_life.md). Domestic work and small repair overlap [Household and Daily Life](household_and_daily_life.md).
- Causal responsibility, competing values, justification, disagreement, uncertainty, and revision overlap [Philosophical Reasoning](philosophical_reasoning.md). A valued craft tradition or solarpunk intention must not choose a conclusion before the practical and social relations are examined.
- Craft knowledge belonging to a person, community, people, lineage, or knowledge tradition requires source-faithful naming and attribution when a core paraphrase would erase origin, authority, identity, restrictions, or contested history. Phi's preference for transparent composition does not authorize appropriation or universalization.

Work speakers may use these ordinary Phi words wherever their technical definitions fit, but none assigns a task, establishes competence or authority, overrides bodily self-report, certifies safety, or approves return to use.

Work speakers may use these terms to trace material and environmental relations, but resource and waste status do not establish ownership or disposal authority, exposure does not establish dose or harm, and ecological monitoring does not certify a workplace or authorize surveillance.

The established [Commons and Collective Governance module](commons_and_collective_governance.md) supplies `wemakori` member, `silathora` role, `loathora` delegate, `kanorami` authority, `kanuloa` authorize, `nasholu` rule, `kanusholu` decision, `phelunori` ownership, `naserumi` obligation, `lonathoni` accountable, `siranomi` record, `turekiro` review, `kanuwhera` coerce, and `whekapelo` retaliate. ## Current Practices and Limits

| Need | Current Phi strategy | Important limit |
|---|---|---|
| Describe work without a task root | State the person, concrete action, object, purpose, time, condition, and result in ordinary clauses; use the event noun of the action when useful. Use `riola` only when its purposeful-contribution framing is intended. | A sequence of action clauses does not conventionalize a task, job, assignment, dependency, project boundary, progress state, or neutral category of labor. |
| Describe making or repair | Name the material, tool, method, actions, observed change, resulting `mirela` state, and any `nilaki` inspection separately. | A completed action, repaired state, or inspection does not establish good design, compliance, durability, acceptance, or responsibility for recurrence. |
| Describe practical knowledge | State method knowledge, prior practice, observation, explanation, understanding, and present ability separately. Preserve an exact credential or standard when source identity matters. | Knowledge, practice, ability, qualification, certification, authorization, and competence for this action remain different relations. |
| Divide shared work | Name each participant and concrete contribution; state proposal, choice, acceptance, refusal, commitment, order, and any functional `pherami` dependencies explicitly; record the result where needed. | A technical dependency does not assign the work or identify coverage, coordination authority, substitution, supervision, omission, and handoff. |
| Ask before tool use, teaching, or assistance | Use a polite whole-clause question for the specific observation, explanation, touch, transfer, assistance, or tool action; preserve refusal and an alternative. | `po` also marks practical possibility. Scope, duration, revocation, supervised use, evaluation, and training consent remain contextual. |
| State capacity and safety | Preserve first-person pain, rest, inability, refusal, or requested change; name the tool, action, `pemaru` hazard, any `pelorawi` exposure, safety condition, timing, and exact source instruction. | Technical `henora` capacity does not describe a person's present ability or consent. Exposure does not establish dose, harm, suitable protection, applicable authority, retaliation protection, or stop-work authority. |
| Check dimensions or fit | Measure, compare, `masuro` calibrate where relevant, `nilaki` inspect against stated criteria, carry exact values and tolerance ranges, and state remaining uncertainty. | A matching dimension, calibration, or inspection is not a complete quality finding, certification, sign-off, or approval for use. |
| Describe completion and handoff | State every action performed, the resulting condition, remaining work, who receives the object or information, who accepts or refuses it, and what will be reviewed later. | Phi has no conventional work status, completion criterion, handoff, acceptance test, warranty, or final-responsibility relation. |
| Discuss compensation, ownership, or institutional terms | Preserve the exact wage, rate, price, contract, credential, role title, rule, or ownership category in source material outside the Phi passage; then describe concrete transfers, commitments, claimed authority, affected people, and disagreement in Phi. | A Phi paraphrase may erase legal or economic force, while a source record does not make the arrangement legitimate, fair, voluntary, understood, or binding. |
| Criticize a work relation | State desire, refusal, necessity, claimed authority, constraint, harm, benefit, transfer, consequence, and testimony directly; preserve source wording exactly when changing it would soften the relation. | Positive roots for labor, craft, service, cooperation, commitment, and responsibility may sanitize coercion or exploitation if they replace rather than analyze the contested relation. |

## Scenario Evidence

The [Work, Craft, and Repair vocabulary audit](../work_craft_repair_vocabulary_audit.md) supplies the inventory evidence behind this organization. It distinguishes dense concrete coverage from thin work relations, value-loaded framing, source-material handling, and gaps that still require independent use.

- [The wheel, the chisel, and the promised repair](../philosophical_test_corpus.md#15-the-wheel-the-chisel-and-the-promised-repair) is the primary profile scenario. It tests a prior commitment, method knowledge, craft practice, apprenticeship, imposed sharp-tool work, refusal, pain, inability, bounded learning consent, self-selected contributions, source specifications, recording, and unresolved safe return to use.
- [Repair or replace](../philosophical_test_corpus.md#2-repair-or-replace) supplies value boundary evidence: repair can be preferred for ecological and practical reasons without becoming sacred or overriding the repairer's consent, safety, or the condition of the object.
- [The marker that was not reported](../philosophical_test_corpus.md#12-the-marker-that-was-not-reported) supplies governance and infrastructure boundary evidence: causation, warning knowledge, maintenance commitment, imposed obligation, shared repair, and accountability remain distinct.
- [A reading, a boundary, and care](../philosophical_test_corpus.md#8-a-reading-a-boundary-and-care) and [A doorway, a route, and written words](../philosophical_test_corpus.md#9-a-doorway-a-route-and-written-words) supply bodily and access boundary evidence: asking, refusal, self-report, assistance, and changed practical conditions remain relevant outside employment or craft institutions.
- [The fallen tree and the living grove](../philosophical_test_corpus.md#14-the-fallen-tree-and-the-living-grove) supplies material-life boundary evidence: moving, retaining, reusing, or transforming material requires ecological and safety analysis beyond calling it a resource, waste, or craft input.
- [The Old Table](household_and_daily_life.md#profile-scenario-the-old-table) supplies a minimal household contrast: small repair is directly expressible, while domestic labor allocation, ownership, hierarchy, and completion remain outside that short scene.

These are fictional or pedagogical regression texts written by one maintainer. They demonstrate available compositions and recurring failures, not competence in a trade, the safety of a procedure, legal or economic adequacy, fair labor conditions, successful access, or another worker's acceptance of Phi's values.

## Source Material Guidance

Keep dimensions, tolerances, formulas, material grades, component and tool identifiers, drawings, procedures, safety instructions, warnings, standards, regulations, inspection criteria, certifications, licenses, role titles, employment classifications, wages, rates, prices, contracts, schedules, records, testimony, legal categories, and terms belonging to a craft or knowledge tradition outside the Phi passage when source form matters. Preserve spelling, script, notation, precision, version, and provenance rather than silently normalizing or translating them.

Use existing vocabulary, a transparent expression, a module term, or a fully admitted new word when Phi needs the concept. A temporary pronunciation aid remains an outside conversational convention and does not acquire a Phi technical definition, credential, authority, or labor relation.

A workshop, guild, cooperative, project, product, tool, process, or other named entity may use `ne` with a listed content name or a valid Phi-form onym when the bearer or relevant naming community accepts it. The name identifies the referent; it does not translate institutional type, ownership, employment status, certification, jurisdiction, or technical function.

Describe the practical relations in Phi where useful: who proposes, requests, performs, observes, receives, or is affected by which action; what method, tool, material, condition, and source are involved; what was chosen, accepted, refused, or committed; what capacity or access change matters; what evidence supports completion or safety; what uncertainty remains; and who claims authority to decide or review. Do not claim that this analysis exhausts the separately preserved source term.

A separately presented phrase such as `qualified person`, `inspection`, `personal protective equipment`, `collective bargaining agreement`, or `24 mm +/- 0.1 mm` preserves a comparison target or operative source phrase. It does not establish qualification, complete an inspection, supply suitable protection, make an agreement voluntary or fair, prove a tolerance was met, or authorize work.

## Vocabulary Pass Starting Point

An original non-workshop work-sharing scenario can provide examples for the first work module terms. It may include dependent actions, a request and claimed assignment or authority, refusal or revised commitment, method knowledge, limited capacity, authorization, an exact specification or economic term, transfer of work or information, and disputed completion or responsibility.

Use the profile and scenario to inventory participants, actions, dependencies, requests, authority claims, methods, capacity statements, permissions, refusals, commitments, exact sources, transfers, completion claims, acceptance, uncertainty, and follow-up responsibility.

Retain transparent compositions where they reveal the work relation and coin module words wherever stable terms improve discussion. No delayed or repeated-failure prerequisite applies.
