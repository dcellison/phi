# Medical and Bodily Care

**Status:** **Established vocabulary module** with 30 roots coined here, 51 current memberships, and shared-core `lesawi` consent; ordinary Phi grammar.

## Charter

This module gives Phi speakers optional vocabulary for reporting bodily experience, distinguishing health observations and conclusions, discussing interventions and illness course, preserving exact health information, and examining individual and public-health choices. It is a language-use module, not medical advice, a clinical protocol, a diagnostic system, or a credential.

The module gives priority to a person's own report, explicit explanation, source fidelity, and consent. It does not imply that sickness reduces personhood, that measurement replaces care, that a helper possesses authority, or that Phi vocabulary can replace qualified interpretation of medical information.

The speaker-facing guide is [Medical and Bodily Care](../../manual/part7_reference/modules/medical_and_bodily_care.md), and the complete current inventory appears in the [module lexicon](../../manual/part7_reference/lexicon/by_module.md#medical-and-bodily-care). The vocabulary table below records the roots coined for this profile; the generated inventory also includes older roots classified after the profile pass and words shared with other learning paths. A module classification changes where a learner finds a word, not what it means. Canonical definitions remain in `vocabulary/content/`.

## Boundaries

- Disability and accessibility are not subsets of illness or medicine. The scenarios organized by [Accessibility and Participation](accessibility_and_participation.md) inform this module because they test consent and environmental barriers, but their participants need no diagnosis and do not become medical subjects.
- Ordinary pain, rest, touch, food, water, breath, support, and refusal remain shared core resources because they are needed far beyond medical settings.
- `lesawi` consent is also shared core rather than optional medical vocabulary. Consent is freely given for a stated scope and may be withheld or withdrawn; the word does not prove explanation, understanding, capacity under an applicable standard, freedom from coercion, legal sufficiency, or continued agreement.
- `phamori` symptoms preserve the person's own experience, while `hisaro` clinical findings preserve a stated observation or method. Neither has automatic priority in every conclusion, and neither becomes a diagnosis without an explicit `kithero` act and its evidence.
- Disease, infection, inflammation, allergy, immunity, contagiousness, and exposure remain separate relations. None licenses purity language, stigma, exclusion, blame, or claims about a person's worth.
- Treatment, intervention, dose, vaccination, isolation, quarantine, and triage do not contain consent, authority, suitability, benefit, fairness, or legitimacy in their definitions. State those relations separately.
- Heat, cold, smoke, contaminated water, food conditions, and other environmental exposures overlap [Ecological Systems and Material Life](ecological_systems_and_material_life.md). An ecological finding does not establish an individual diagnosis, and a bodily report does not by itself establish an ecological source or system effect.
- Pain, fatigue, rest, capacity, safety, assistance, and changed participation in work overlap [Work, Craft, and Repair](work_craft_and_repair.md). A work role, task, commitment, method claim, or exact safety instruction does not establish diagnosis, bodily capacity, consent, clinical interpretation, or fitness for work.
- Mental and emotional care may overlap with bodily care, but this module does not claim that current bodily vocabulary adequately covers mental health practice.
- Reproductive health, sexuality, gender, abuse, coercion, and testimony require their own respectful scenarios before this module can state how much belongs in shared core, another profile, or source material outside the Phi passage.
- No word listed here grants a person a professional role, expertise, authority, or permission to act on someone else.

## Earlier roots classified here

`heloa` remedy, `thelai` balm, and `wenu` tonic now appear in the Medical learning path. Each names a particular preparation or curative relation. Medicine and healing stay in base vocabulary, alongside care and bodily self-report. A speaker can still describe pain or sickness and ask for help before learning these narrower nouns.

## Optional Cross-Module Dependencies

The established [Ecological Systems and Material Life module](ecological_systems_and_material_life.md) supplies `hisophi` contaminant, `pekira` toxic, `nomiki` concentration, `perawi` expose, `phisuri` sample, `mothami` baseline, `herani` trend, `reteru` model, `somethi` monitor, and `sumeri` temperature. Each keeps its ecological definition: a contaminant marks a relation to an expected condition, toxicity a capacity for harm, exposure contact, a sample a selected portion, and monitoring repeated observation. Clinical meaning and permission come from Medical terms and the surrounding account. Temperature identifies the parameter; `suloru` fever remains a bodily condition, and the exact reading stays in its source record.

The established [Systems and Shared Infrastructure module](systems_and_shared_infrastructure.md) supplies `mirela` state, `pherami` dependency, `henora` technical capacity, `masuro` calibration, `nilaki` inspection, `koluri` reliability, `pemaru` hazard, and `kisholu` specification. Medical speakers may use these words for devices, records, supply systems, and technical procedures, but a body's capacity is not a machine rating, a clinical examination is not a technical inspection, and device reliability does not establish a clinically valid interpretation or suitable intervention.

The established [Work, Craft, and Repair module](work_craft_and_repair.md) supplies `lumethi` deadline, `thimora` schedule, `sethoni` handoff, `pokera` competent, `kiranu` qualified, `kisome` assess, `somaki` test, and `ritako` workload. Medical speakers may use these words where their definitions fit. `somaki` is the procedure rather than its finding, and `sethoni` is a transfer rather than proof of continuity. A care plan still needs its own consent and authorization.

Claims, evidence, reasons, and criteria are already base vocabulary. The established [Philosophical Reasoning module](philosophical_reasoning.md) adds inference, validity, definitions, doubt, confidence, tradeoffs, and compromise. These resources help examine diagnosis, prognosis, risk, public-health reasoning, and competing care values without making a formal argument clinically sound or an agreed compromise medically appropriate.

The established [Commons and Collective Governance module](commons_and_collective_governance.md) supplies `shalori` represent, `sithora` role, `karami` authority, `kanuwa` authorize, `nasholu` rule, `kanuro` decision, `naseru` obligation, `lothoni` accountable, `shanewi` notify, and `tukiro` review. Medical speakers may use these words for institutional relations, while base `sirami` record, `kawhera` coerce, and `whepelo` retaliate remain available beyond institutions. Authorization belongs to the institution; consent belongs to the person. A record can be incomplete or inaccessible, and a governance decision is not a diagnosis.

## First Vocabulary Pass

The first pass admits 30 optional medical words for recurring distinctions whose ordinary paraphrases were cumbersome, ambiguous, or unable to sustain health-focused discussion. It also admits `lesawi` consent to shared core because consent is a general autonomy relation rather than optional medical knowledge. The pass does not reproduce a diagnostic catalogue, professional hierarchy, public-health code, or pharmacological reference; exact names, values, products, protocols, classifications, credentials, and legal terms remain outside Phi when their source identity matters.

| Family | Roots coined for this profile | What the pass now distinguishes |
|---|---|---|
| Experience and observation | `phamori` symptom, `hisaro` clinical finding | A person's experienced and reported symptom from a method-produced observation, measurement, response, image interpretation, or test result; neither from diagnosis or proof of cause. |
| Condition and course | `kaworu` injury, `mokanu` disease, `peshiro` infection, `thephaku` inflammation, `suloru` fever, `katheo` acute, `nurawi` chronic, `pelaku` severity | Bodily damage from disease classification; infection from exposure, contamination, inflammation, and contagiousness; fever from warmth and exact measurement; clinical course from severity, danger, prognosis, and human worth. |
| Evaluation | `kithero` diagnose, `wireki` prognosis, `soranu` screen | A sourced clinical conclusion from symptoms and findings; expected course from diagnosis or destiny; screening from testing, diagnosis, monitoring, surveillance, and compulsory participation. |
| Intervention and effects | `nephoru` treat, `thekamu` intervene, `mikasu` dose, `porewi` side effect, `whekaro` adverse effect, `whemaki` allergy, `nathoku` contraindicated | Treatment from care and cure; intervention from source-specific procedure and benefit; a dose from concentration, schedule, course, and effect; additional effects from harmful effects and allergy; clinical advice against an intervention from impossibility, refusal, prohibition, and universal harm. |
| Change over time | `helanu` recover, `morashi` relapse | Person-defined and potentially partial recovery from cure, restoration to a social norm, and independence; relapse from blame, a new condition, every recurrence, and proof of treatment failure. |
| Transmission and public health | `sewaro` transmit, `welathi` contagious, `weshaku` outbreak, `shorenu` immune, `shuneki` vaccinate, `sonari` isolate, `teshori` quarantine, `kithawu` triage | Transmission from exposure and infection; contagiousness from personal danger or blame; outbreak from exact epidemic category; immunity from invulnerability or vaccination; protective actions from consent, authority, legitimacy, coercion, abandonment, and human-worth ranking. |

All 30 roots coined in the medical pass and the shared-core consent entry follow the complete vocabulary schema, pass the lexical validator, and have no character edit-distance-one or flagged phonetic neighbors in the pre-coinage audit. The 30 roots carry `medical-and-bodily-care`; `lesawi` deliberately carries no module field and remains in base vocabulary.

## Concepts Kept Compositional or External

The pass keeps several expressions compositional because their parts expose information, agency, source, uncertainty, authority, access, or bodily consequence that a single label could hide. Exact categories remain outside Phi when a translated approximation would lose their operative identity or force.

| Concept | Phi strategy | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Informed consent | Use `lesawi` with explicit proposal, purpose, relevant information, alternatives, expected benefits and burdens, understanding, questions, practical freedom, scope, duration, and withdrawal | No adjective or recorded signature can prove that a consent process was informed, voluntary, understood, current, or sufficient. |
| Permission before touch or care | Ask a polite whole-clause question naming the exact action, then state `lesawi`, refusal, or another answer and what action follows | Keeps consent attached to one proposed action rather than turning it into blanket permission. |
| Generic health condition | Use `mirela` state, `mokela` sick, a specific module term, or the exact source classification | Avoids a vague clinical umbrella that could silently convert disability, difference, pregnancy, distress, or social judgment into disease. |
| Clinical test and test result | Use Work `somaki` test, the exact method and source artifact, and `hisaro` for the resulting clinical finding | Separates the procedure, raw result, interpreted finding, threshold, diagnosis, and recommendation. |
| Clinical threshold | Use `kirema` criterion plus the exact source value, unit, population, method, and interpretation | A threshold is source- and purpose-dependent and does not automatically establish danger, diagnosis, or action. |
| Clinical risk | State the possible `peloma` harm, affected person or population, time horizon, evidence, alternatives, uncertainty, and exact probability or category externally | Keeps probability, severity, uncertainty, value, and acceptable burden distinct rather than compressing them into one warning word. |
| False-positive and false-negative result | State the test or screen result, later evidence, applicable criterion, and whether the result supported a conclusion that was not present or failed to support one that was | Makes the claimed error and reference standard explicit. |
| Procedure, surgery, therapy, and exact intervention | Use general `thekamu` intervention or `nephoru` treatment around the exact source name and description | Preserves technical identity without making every intervention equivalent or creating a catalogue of source labels. |
| Prescription, medicine identity, formulation, route, and course | Preserve the exact artifact and use `nepha`, `mikasu`, `thimora`, and action clauses where their definitions fit | Exact product, amount, route, timing, issuer, and instructions are safety-relevant source information. |
| Remission, recurrence, worsening, and improvement | State the relevant symptom, condition, finding, criteria, time, and observed change; use `morashi` only for relapse within the same understood course | These course relations depend on a baseline, prior state, diagnosis, and source definition. |
| Patient, clinician, caregiver, interpreter, and other care roles | Use `miona` person with Commons `sithora` role, Work competence and qualification terms, `shalori` representation where it actually applies, and exact professional titles externally | Keeps personhood separate from temporary role, credential, authority, communication support, dependence, and consent. |
| Care plan and clinical handoff | Use `theama winora` care planning and Work `sethoni` handoff with the people, information, unresolved needs, receipt, understanding, consent, and accountability stated | A plan does not authorize intervention, and passing information does not prove accepted responsibility or continuity of care. |
| Clinical specimen | Use Ecological `phisuri` sample with its bodily source, collection method, time, handling, identifier, and exact source terminology | Prevents a generic sample from claiming representativeness, integrity, ownership, consent, or diagnostic meaning. |
| Urgency and emergency | State the possible harm, time sensitivity, bodily state, available care, constraints, evidence, and necessary action; use `kithawu` only when prioritization occurs | Urgency is not severity alone, and an emergency label must not silently grant unlimited authority or erase consent and access. |
| Prevention and infection control | State the infectious agent or uncertainty, pathway, proposed protective action, expected effect, burden, consent, authority, support, and review | Keeps protection from becoming a blanket justification for surveillance, isolation, quarantine, exclusion, or coercion. |
| Exact public-health categories and protocols | Preserve case definitions, outbreak or epidemic categories, isolation and quarantine rules, reporting duties, thresholds, dates, jurisdictions, and source wording externally | Module terms identify general relations but do not recreate legal or scientific force. |
| Mental health, reproductive health, sexuality, gender, abuse, and coercive care | Preserve testimony and exact source terms, use existing emotion, body, consent, governance, and care resources, and develop respectful scenarios before deciding further vocabulary | The Medical module must not absorb identity, disability, social conflict, or sensitive experience into an untested clinical taxonomy. |

## Scenario Evidence

- [A reading, a boundary, and care](../evaluation/philosophical_test_corpus.md#8-a-reading-a-boundary-and-care) is the primary before-state scenario. The module can now distinguish its symptom, clinical finding, possible severity, diagnosis, prognosis, treatment, intervention, dose, effects, and consent relations while retaining the exact temperature record, uncertainty, refusal, explanation, and difference between data and care.
- [A doorway, a route, and written words](../evaluation/philosophical_test_corpus.md#9-a-doorway-a-route-and-written-words) supplies boundary evidence: consent and support recur in access work, but disability and participation must not be absorbed into a medical category.

These scenarios supplied useful compositions and a before-state for vocabulary development. They do not establish a medical standard, and the original dialogues remain valid before-state regression texts rather than being rewritten merely to display the new words. They remain available for the planned active-corpus revisit.

## Source Material Guidance

Keep spelling, script, values, units, identifiers, medicine labels, source instructions, quotations, and health records outside the Phi passage whenever their exact form matters. Preserve the artifact through the surrounding document, interface, or conversation without silent conversion, rounding, translation, or normalization. A Phi explanation may accompany it but must not be presented as equivalent when it omits a medically relevant distinction.

Use a valid Phi word, transparent expression, or module term when Phi needs the concept. Use `ne` plus a bearer-approved onym of two, three, or four syllables for a name inside Phi. A source name or technical term that does not fit those choices remains outside the Phi passage rather than becoming a temporary lexical class.

Separate preservation supplies no clinical interpretation. If speakers do not understand a reading, instruction, or term, Phi should state that lack of understanding rather than infer a recommendation from the artifact.

## Continued Development

Use the module in care conversations, health narratives, recovery accounts, source-faithful record discussions, treatment decisions, public-health disagreements, and stories about bodies changing over time. Coin another word when a recurring medical concept deserves lexical presence or when the available composition is cumbersome, ambiguous, or aesthetically wrong for Phi. Retain a composition when its parts expose a useful source, criterion, uncertainty, consent boundary, access need, authority claim, effect, or responsibility.

Future use should particularly test mental and emotional care, reproductive and sexual health, chronic care outside institutions, emergency communication, uncertain diagnosis, medication changes, disability boundaries, adverse-effect reporting, infection-control support, and public-health disagreement. These are opportunities for careful development, not evidence gates or permission to classify identities and testimony without respectful scenarios.

Once the remaining classification decisions are settled, revisit the existing corpus as a whole. Update a text when expanded vocabulary makes its meaning clearer, safer, more direct, or less evasive; preserve it when the original composition remains natural or when it is intentionally retained as before-state evidence. Every later entry must still satisfy the vocabulary schema and phonology, along with collision and semantic checks. Its examples and generated references must remain in sync.
