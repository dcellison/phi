## Lesson 4: Talking About Time

So far, all of our sentences have been in the **present tense**. Phi handles the present tense by default—you don't need to add any special words.

But how do we talk about the past or the future? Phi does this with simple **tense particles**. These are small words that come directly **before the verb** to change its tense.

### The Past Tense (`to`)

The particle for the past tense is `to`. To change a sentence from the present to the past, you simply place `to` before the verb.

Let's look at a present tense sentence:
*   `mia lothea kela.` (I understand love.)

Now, let's make it past tense:

*   **Phi:** `mia lothea to kela.`
*   **Gloss:** `1SG love PST understand.`
*   **Translation:** I understood love.

It's that simple. The particle `to` signals that the action of understanding happened in the past.

### The Future Tense (`so`)

The particle for the future tense is `so`. It works exactly the same way.

Let's say "The community will sleep":

*   **Phi:** `weola so nima.`
*   **Gloss:** `community FUT sleep.`
*   **Translation:** The community will sleep.

### Using Tense with Questions

Tense particles work perfectly with questions. The question particle `wa` still comes at the very beginning of the sentence, and the tense particle still comes right before the verb.

Let's ask, "Did you see the friend?":

*   **Phi:** `wa thi whelea to shipe.`
*   **Gloss:** `Q 2SG friend PST see.`
*   **Translation:** Did you see the friend?

### Lesson 4 Summary

*   Phi's default tense is the **present**.
*   To form the **past tense**, place the particle `to` before the verb.
*   To form the **future tense**, place the particle `so` before the verb.
*   Tense particles fit seamlessly into the sentence structures we've already learned.

You can now describe events not just in the present, but across time. In the next lesson, we will explore more advanced particles that describe *how* an action happens (aspect).
