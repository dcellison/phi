## Lesson 5: Describing Actions with Aspect

In the last lesson, we learned about **tense**, which tells us *when* an action happens (past, present, future). Now, we will learn about **aspect**, which tells us *how* an action unfolds in time. Is it ongoing? Is it just beginning? Is it complete?

Like tense particles, aspect particles are **Slot 1** particles, meaning they come directly before the verb.

### Ongoing Actions (`si` - Imperfective)

The most common aspect particle is `si`, which is similar to adding "-ing" in English. It describes an action that is continuous or in progress.

*   **Phi:** `mia si nima.`
*   **Gloss:** `1SG IPFV sleep.`
*   **Translation:** I am sleeping.

You can combine this with tense. If the `si` comes after the past tense particle `to`, it describes an action that *was* in progress.

*   **Phi:** `mia to si nima.`
*   **Gloss:** `1SG PST IPFV sleep.`
*   **Translation:** I was sleeping.

### Completed Actions (`ki` - Perfective)

The particle `ki` describes an action that is viewed as a single, completed whole. It's often translated as "have done" something.

*   **Phi:** `mia to ki kela.`
*   **Gloss:** `1SG PST PFV understand.`
*   **Translation:** I have understood. (The act of understanding is complete).

### Beginning and Ending Actions (`pa` and `te`)

Phi also has beautiful particles to describe the beginning and end of an action.
*   `pa`: Marks the beginning of an action (inchoative).
*   `te`: Marks the end of an action (cessative).

Let's see them in action:

*   **Phi:** `mia pa kela.`
*   **Gloss:** `1SG INCH understand.`
*   **Translation:** I begin to understand.

*   **Phi:** `mia so te nima.`
*   **Gloss:** `1SG FUT CESS sleep.`
*   **Translation:** I will stop sleeping.

### Lesson 5 Summary

*   **Aspect particles** describe *how* an action unfolds and come before the verb.
*   `si`: Marks an ongoing or continuous action.
*   `ki`: Marks a completed or whole action.
*   `pa`: Marks the beginning of an action.
*   `te`: Marks the end of an action.

You can now express a rich new layer of meaning, describing the flow of actions with precision and elegance. In the next lesson, we will see how to combine these particles for even more nuance.
