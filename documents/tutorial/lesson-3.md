## Lesson 3: How to Ask Questions

Phi has two simple and consistent ways to ask questions.

### 1. Yes/No Questions

To turn any statement into a yes/no question, you simply add the **question particle `wa`** to the very beginning of the sentence.

Let's take a statement we already know:
*   `weola wela nio.` (The community is beautiful.)

To ask, "Is the community beautiful?", we just add `wa`:

*   **Phi:** `wa weola wela nio.`
*   **Gloss:** `Q community good/beautiful be.`
*   **Translation:** Is the community beautiful?

The answer to this question would be a simple `lia` (yes) or `ma` (no).

### 2. Content Questions (what, who, where, etc.)

To ask for more information, we use **interrogative pronouns**. These are words that stand in for the information you want to know. In Phi, these words are treated just like any other noun or pronoun—they are placed in the subject or object slot of a normal, verb-final sentence.

Here are the most common interrogative pronouns:
*   `hina`: what
*   `sua`: who
*   `kua`: where
*   `thela`: how
*   `misa`: why

Let's see them in action. Notice how the sentence structure is always verb-final.

**Asking "what?":**
To ask "What do you understand?", you place `hina` in the object position.

*   **Phi:** `thi hina kela.`
*   **Gloss:** `2SG what understand.`
*   **Translation:** What do you understand?

**Asking "who?":**
To ask "Who understands love?", you place `sua` in the subject position.

*   **Phi:** `sua lothea kela.`
*   **Gloss:** `who love understand.`
*   **Translation:** Who understands love?

**Asking "where?":**
To ask "Where does the community sleep?", you place `kua` in the location (adverbial) slot.

*   **Phi:** `weola kua nima.`
*   **Gloss:** `community where sleep.`
*   **Translation:** Where does the community sleep?

### Lesson 3 Summary

*   To ask a yes/no question, add `wa` to the beginning of a statement.
*   To ask a content question, place the appropriate interrogative word (`hina`, `sua`, etc.) in the normal subject or object position of a verb-final sentence.

You can now not only make statements, but also ask questions to learn more about the world. In our next lesson, we will cover how to talk about the past and the future.
