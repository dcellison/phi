## Lesson 11: Building Complex Sentences

Welcome to the final stage of learning Phi's core grammar! In this lesson, we will learn how to create more complex sentences by embedding one clause inside another. This is called **subordination**.

A subordinate clause is a small sentence that acts as a descriptive part of a larger sentence. In Phi, true to its head-final nature, subordinate clauses and phrases generally come **before** the main clause they modify.

### Temporal Clauses (when, before, after)

You can use a full sentence to describe the time when something happens. To do this, you use a temporal preposition that functions as a subordinator, followed by the complete clause.

Let's learn two temporal prepositions:
*   `phoe`: before
*   `pheo`: after

To say, "Before I help, I think," we use the preposition `phoe` followed by the clause "I help" (`mia shele`). This whole phrase then comes before the main clause, "I think" (`mia nolu`).

*   **Phi:** `phoe [mia shele], mia nolu.`
*   **Gloss:** `BEFORE [1SG help], 1SG think.`
*   **Translation:** Before I help, I think.

Here's an example with `pheo`:

*   **Phi:** `pheo [mia pholea], mia hisu sela.`
*   **Gloss:** `AFTER [1SG create], 1SG feel peace.`
*   **Translation:** After I create, I feel peace.

### Causal Clauses (because)

You can also use a clause to explain the *reason* or *cause* of an action. To do this, you use the causal preposition `lao`, followed by the "reason" clause that has been turned into a noun using the nominalizer `nai`.

Let's break down: "Because she left, I feel sad."

1.  **The "Reason" Clause:** `sha to wepu` (She left.)
2.  **Turn it into a Noun:** `[sha to wepu] nai` (The leaving of her)
3.  **Add the Causal Preposition:** `lao [[sha to wepu] nai]` (Because of her leaving)
4.  **Add the Main Clause:** `...mia nolou hisu.` (...I feel sad.)

Putting it all together:

*   **Phi:** `lao [[sha to wepu] nai], mia nolou hisu.`

This structure, `lao [Clause nai]`, is the standard way to express "because..."

### Lesson 11 Summary

*   A **subordinate clause** is a small sentence that describes a part of a larger one.
*   Subordinate clauses generally come **before** the main clause.
*   Use `phoe [clause]` for "before" and `pheo [clause]` for "after."
*   Use `lao [[clause] nai]` to create a clause that explains the reason for an action.

You are now able to express very complex and specific ideas about time and causality. In our next lesson, we will cover the final and most powerful type of clause.
