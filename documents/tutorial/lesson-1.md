## Lesson 1: The Core Sentence & The Verb-Final Rule

The single most important rule in Phi is that **the verb always comes last.** This is the foundation of the language's mindful, deliberate rhythm.

### The Subject and the Verb

A sentence needs a subject (who or what is doing the action) and a verb (the action itself).

Let's learn two nouns and one verb:
*   `mia`: I, me (the self)
*   `weola`: community
*   `nima`: to sleep

To say "I sleep," you place the subject first, and the verb last.

*   **Phi:** `mia nima.`
*   **Gloss:** `1SG sleep.`
*   **Translation:** I sleep.

To say "The community sleeps," it's the same pattern:

*   **Phi:** `weola nima.`
*   **Gloss:** `community sleep.`
*   **Translation:** The community sleeps.

### Describing Things with `sio`

How do you describe something? In Phi, we are always explicit. To say something *is* a certain way, we use the verb `sio` (to be/to exist).

Let's learn one adjective:
*   `wela`: good / beautiful / harmonious

To say "The community is beautiful," you state the subject, then the quality, and end with the verb `sio`.

*   **Phi:** `weola wela sio.`
*   **Gloss:** `community good/beautiful be.`
*   **Translation:** The community is beautiful.

This `Subject-Adjective-Verb` structure is a core pattern built on the verb-final rule.

### Lesson 1 Summary

*   The verb is always the final word in a Phi sentence.
*   The simplest sentence pattern is `Subject-Verb`.
*   To describe something, use the pattern `Subject-Adjective-sio`.

Congratulations! You can now form basic, complete sentences in Phi. In the next lesson, we will introduce objects and postpositions.
