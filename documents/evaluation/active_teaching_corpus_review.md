# Active teaching corpus review

This ledger tracks the teaching-material portion of roadmap item SEM-09D. The literary shelf has its own [active text corpus review](active_text_corpus_review.md); this record follows the manual, primer, pamphlets, and current project documents as they are checked against the completed lexicon and present canon. Files in `archive/` remain outside scope.

## Review states

| State | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Reviewed | The whole file has been checked in context for its teaching claim, Phi examples, current vocabulary, learning role, and active dependencies. |
| Preserved | An example or construction was examined and deliberately kept. |
| Revised | Current semantics, canon, or the needs of a learner required a change. |
| Pending | The file has not yet received the complete contextual pass. |

## Review method

| Check | Question |
|---|---|
| Teaching claim | Does the explanation state what Phi does without turning a design intention into a cognitive guarantee or universal linguistic fact? |
| Lexicon semantics | Does every cited word carry its current definition, including the distinctions from nearby words? |
| Sound and articulation | Do IPA, syllable divisions, stress, and physical instructions agree with the lexicon and phonetics reference? Is sound symbolism kept optional, local, and whole-word? |
| Phi form | Is every utterance lowercase and period-only, with canonical words, grammar, and natural English? |
| Social use | Would the example be a credible thing for people in the stated relationship to say? |
| Vocabulary level | Does general teaching prefer base vocabulary while a module chapter uses optional terms when its domain earns them? |
| Composition | Does a transparent expression remain natural and useful, or does a current direct word now say the thought better? |
| Dependencies | Do repeated examples, reference notes, exercises, and generated pages still agree after a revision? |
| Validation | Do the targeted validator, standalone validator, Markdown checks, and site build pass? |

New English prose is not a reason to disturb a valid Phi passage. A line changes when present vocabulary, grammar, source fidelity, naturalness, or its teaching job makes the current form worse. A noticed lexical gap receives a durable decision before its batch closes.

## Part I: First Light

All nine files under `manual/part1_first_light/` are reviewed. The pass found no missing concept and used no optional module vocabulary. Its substantial changes repair teaching drift rather than expand the language.

| File | State | Finding and disposition |
|---|---|---|
| `ch01_why_new_language/01_invisible_architecture.md` | Revised | The old chapter assigned worldviews to word order and treated English grammar as a cause of ownership and separation. The new account stays with choices made while speaking, admits that Phi cannot force attention, and uses base `panuri` (resource) to show a relational distinction the lexicon actually makes. |
| `ch01_why_new_language/02_language_laboratory.md` | Revised | Esperanto is an intended bridge with a largely European lexical centre. Lojban makes syntactic structure recoverable while leaving semantic and pragmatic ambiguity alive. Toki Pona's mindfulness belongs to reported experience rather than a compelled result. Phi is one experiment among them, and effects beyond speaking remain untested. |
| `ch01_why_new_language/03_what_phi_attempts.md` | Revised | The lexicon size, literary shelf, translation and transmutation account, and five-pillar boundary now describe the current project. The reviewed *North Wind and the Sun* moral remains exact. |
| `ch01_why_new_language/04_how_to_use_this_book.md` | Revised | The route through the manual is current, and the pacing advice now states the settled distinction: unhurried speech is freedom from needless haste, not a prescribed speed. |
| `ch02_first_words/01_what_phi_sounds_like.md` | Revised | Five sample words now follow canonical IPA and articulatory notes. Old claims that sounds contain meanings or make anger difficult are gone; optional whole-word mnemonics remain where the lexicon supports them. |
| `ch02_first_words/02_your_first_sentence.md` | Revised with Phi preserved | All six Phi clauses remain. The lesson no longer claims that verb-final order forces reflection or removes ambiguity, and the three word explanations follow their actual articulation instead of assigning meanings to syllables. |
| `ch02_first_words/03_the_music_of_vowel_hiatus.md` | Revised | The typological claim now allows both diphthong and hiatus outside Phi. | `ch02_first_words/04_ten_essential_words.md` | Revised | The first set now supports an actual meeting: `wa`, `towe`, and `pao` replace `sano`, `loa`, and `womu`. The entry for `sano` had wrongly taught understand, and the old peace examples confused inward `shea phaelo` with social `shea nai`. |
| `ch02_first_words/05_a_simple_conversation.md` | Revised | The old exchange declared friendship, inner peace, home-love, and mutual love without establishing the relationship. Two established friends now exchange a greeting, a wellbeing question, and a farewell in lowercase, period-only Phi. |

### Lexical result

Existing base words, function words, and interjections cover every repaired lesson, so no lexical decision remains open. `towe` supplies the ordinary wellbeing question, `phaelo` keeps inward peace embodied as a feeling, and `panuri` already carries the relational account of a resource.
