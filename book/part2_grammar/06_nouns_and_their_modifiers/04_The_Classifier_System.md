## 6.4 The Soul of the Noun: The Classifier System

Beyond specifying *which* noun or *how many*, the Phi language offers a final, optional layer of descriptive nuance: the classifier system. This is perhaps one of the most direct expressions of the language's philosophical core, a grammatical feature designed not for mandatory clarity, but for elective mindfulness. In Phi, it is perfectly grammatical to simply count objects. But a more conscious and respectful way to speak is to first acknowledge the *intrinsic nature* of the thing being counted. A classifier is a special noun that is placed after a number but before the noun it modifies, effectively sorting the world into categories of being. The system invites the speaker to pause and consider the very soul of the noun they are about to name, asking not just "how many?" but first, "what *kind* of thing is this?"
