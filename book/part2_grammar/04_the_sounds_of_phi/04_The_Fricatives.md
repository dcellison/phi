## 4.4 The Fricative Digraphs

A digraph is a pair of letters representing a single sound. Phi uses four fricative digraphs, which create a characteristic "breathy" or "airy" texture. They are made by constricting, but not stopping, the airflow.

---

**`ph`** (/ɸ/)

This is a **voiceless bilabial fricative**. It is **not** the `/f/` sound of English "fish" (which is made with the teeth and lip). The Phi `ph` is made only with the lips.

1.  Bring your lips close together, as if to say 'p', but do not seal them.
2.  Push air through the small, relaxed gap.
3.  A good way to practice is to imagine blowing out a candle with a soft, controlled puff of air.

**Practice Words:**
*   `phola` (power-to-create)
*   `phale` (a call for a boundary)
*   `phela` (to grow)

---

**`th`** (/θ/)

This is a **voiceless dental fricative**. It is crucial that this sound is **always voiceless** (no vocal cord vibration), like the `th` in "think," and never voiced like the `th` in "this."

1.  Place the tip of your tongue either lightly between your teeth or just behind your upper front teeth.
2.  Push air through the narrow gap.

*   **English:** **th**in, **th**ought, **th**ree

**Practice Words:**
*   `thola` (story)
*   `thopa` (to speak)
*   `thelao` (therefore)

---

**`sh`** (/ʃ/)

This is a **voiceless palato-alveolar fricative**, a soft, rushing sound.

1.  Raise the body of your tongue towards the hard palate (the roof of your mouth).
2.  Push air through the wide channel that is formed.

*   **English:** **sh**e, **sh**ine, hu**sh**

**Practice Words:**
*   `sha` (the other)
*   `shele` (to give/receive)
*   `shiro` (forest)

---

**`wh`** (/ʍ/)

This is a **voiceless labial-velar fricative**. While now rare in English, it is essential in Phi. Think of it as spelling "hw."

1.  Round your lips as if to say the word "who."
2.  Before voicing the vowel, push a distinct, audible puff of breath through your rounded lips. It should feel like a whispered 'h' right before a 'w' sound.

*   **English (some dialects):** **wh**ich, **wh**ales

**Practice Words:**
*   `whio` (air/breath)
*   `whelea` (friend)
*   `whiso` (to be reciprocal)
