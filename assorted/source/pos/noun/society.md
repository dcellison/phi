# people and relationships

*core human social connections*

> This vocabulary represents fundamental human social connections through community-centered and earth-connected principles. Unlike hierarchical or individualistic social structures, these concepts emphasize cooperative relationships, shared responsibility, and practical knowledge sharing. Each category focuses on how people relate to each other in sustainable, supportive communities that value collective wisdom over individual accumulation, seasonal awareness over industrial extraction, and collaborative problem-solving over competitive dominance.
>
> From basic life stages that honor growth and wisdom, to family bonds that extend beyond biological connections, to community roles that serve collective benefit, to traditional skills that preserve practical knowledge - this collection embodies how language can foster cooperation, mutual aid, and earth-connected living. The vocabulary emphasizes practical community organization, traditional craft knowledge, seasonal resource management, and collaborative decision-making that represents humanity's more harmonious approaches to social organization.
>
> These concepts naturally resist the language of violence, hierarchy, and exploitation by focusing on relationships that strengthen community resilience, preserve practical wisdom, and maintain sustainable connections to the land and each other.
