# motion

> This vocabulary represents fundamental human experiences of movement, displacement, and locomotion found universally across contemporary global cultures. These concepts encompass the full spectrum of physical motion, from basic ambulatory actions like walking and running through complex navigational movements involving vehicles, natural environments, and three-dimensional space. The verbs included here address core kinetic phenomena: terrestrial locomotion, aquatic movement, aerial transportation, directional changes, speed variations, postural adjustments, and spatial relationships. Each verb captures essential aspects of human mobility including walking, running, jumping, climbing, swimming, flying, driving, steering, accelerating, turning, rising, falling, approaching, and departing. This vocabulary celebrates the human capacity for diverse movement patterns that enable exploration, survival, recreation, work, and social interaction across all environments and situations where motion occurs.
