# existence

> This vocabulary represents fundamental human experiences of being, living, dying, appearing, disappearing, and transitioning through states of existence found universally across contemporary global cultures. These concepts encompass the full cycle of life, from birth and emergence through growth and flourishing, to decay and death, emphasizing the profound ontological processes that define sentient experience. The verbs included here address core existential phenomena: coming into being, maintaining life, experiencing consciousness, undergoing transformation, facing mortality, and transitioning between states of existence. Each verb captures essential aspects of the human condition including breathing, living, thriving, surviving, aging, dying, appearing, vanishing, becoming, remaining, lasting, and ceasing to be. This vocabulary acknowledges the universal experiences that connect all human cultures: the mystery of birth, the struggle for survival, the awareness of mortality, and the search for meaning within the bounded span of individual existence.
