# Section 9: Evidentiality System

> How phi grammatically marks the source of information

## Overview

Evidentiality is a grammatical system that indicates **how the speaker knows** the information they are presenting. It's one of phi's most sophisticated and distinctive features, requiring speakers to explicitly mark the source of their knowledge.

## Quick Reference

### Phi's Six Evidential Particles

| Particle | Type | Meaning | Example Context |
|----------|------|---------|-----------------|
| `hi` | Direct Evidence | "I saw/experienced" | Direct sensory experience |
| `ro` | Inference | "I conclude/deduce" | Logical reasoning from evidence |
| `nu` | Hearsay | "I heard that" | Second-hand information |
| `ti` | Reported Speech | "X said that" | Attributed to specific source |
| `mu` | Memory | "I remember" | Personal recollection |
| `pe` | Presumption | "I assume/expect" | General knowledge/assumption |

## Key Principles

1. **Mandatory Marking**: Evidentiality is required in formal contexts
2. **Single Selection**: Only one evidential per clause
3. **Sentence-Initial**: Evidentials appear in Slot 0 position
4. **Scope**: Evidentials apply to the entire proposition
