# Section 12: Discourse Management

## Overview

The **discourse management system** provides speakers with systematic tools for **information flow and discourse coherence** across utterances. Phi's discourse particles and information structure mechanisms enable clear topic establishment, contrast marking, and strategic information presentation across extended texts and conversations.

## Core Function

**Discourse management particles** address essential information structure questions:

- **What are we talking about?** → Topic marking (`ha`)
- **How does this relate to previous information?** → Contrast marking (`mi`)
- **What information is most important?** → Emphasis and focus (`ma`)
- **How is information distributed across clauses?** → Cross-slot information structure

## The Information Architecture

Phi's discourse system operates through **integrated mechanisms across multiple slots**:

### Primary Discourse Particles (Slot 0)
**Topic establishment**: `ha` (final position in Slot 0)
```
ha ne whethea, mia ta whera.
TOP INAN book, 1SG PRS learn
"As for books, I learn."
```

**Contrast marking**: `mi` (conjunction between clauses)
```
mia ta whera mi sha ta whema.
1SG PRS learn CNTR 3SG PRS work
"I learn, but s/he works."
```

### Information Structure Effects (Slot 2)
**Focal emphasis**: `ma` (final position in Slot 2, immediately before words)
```
mia ta ma ne whethea whera.
1SG PRS EMPH INAN book learn
"I learn from *books*."
```

### Cross-Slot Discourse Integration
**Multi-level information management**: Discourse particles work with all three slots
```
so ha ne whethea, hi mia ta ma la whera mi sha me whera.
POL TOP INAN book, DIR 1SG PRS EMPH PROG learn CNTR 3SG NEG learn
"Politely, as for books, I *am* learning, but s/he doesn't learn."
```

## Cross-Linguistic Perspective

### Universal Discourse Phenomena

Most languages manage discourse through:
- **Topic marking**: Special positions or particles for discourse topics
- **Focus structures**: Emphasis, clefting, or prosodic prominence
- **Contrast mechanisms**: Conjunctions, particles, or word order
- **Information structure**: Given vs. new information management

### Phi's Systematic Approach

**Clear structural positions**: Each discourse function has specific syntactic location
```
Topic:    Slot 0 final position (ha)
Contrast: Inter-clausal conjunction (mi)  
Focus:    Slot 2 final position (ma)
```

**Integrated architecture**: Discourse particles work systematically with all other particles
```
Cross-slot discourse: [Slot 0: ha] [Slot 1: temporal] [Slot 2: ma] [Word]
```

**Predictable scope**: Discourse particles have well-defined semantic scope
```
ha [topic domain]    (sentence/clause-level topic)
mi [contrast domain] (clause-to-clause contrast)
ma [focus domain]    (word-level emphasis)
```

## The Complete Discourse System

### Topic Management

| Function | Particle | Position | Scope | Example |
|----------|----------|----------|-------|---------|
| Topic establishment | `ha` | Slot 0 final | Clause/sentence | `ha mia ta whera` "As for that, I learn" |
| Topic continuation | (implicit) | - | Discourse | Context-maintained topics |
| Topic shift | `ha` + new topic | Slot 0 final | New discourse segment | `ha ne lophui, mia ta misha` "As for art, I'm beautiful" |

### Contrast and Opposition

| Function | Particle | Position | Scope | Example |
|----------|----------|----------|-------|---------|
| Simple contrast | `mi` | Inter-clausal | Clause to clause | `mia whera mi sha whema` "I learn but s/he works" |
| Topic contrast | `ha...mi` | Slot 0 + conjunction | Cross-topic | `ha mia, mi sha` "As for me, but s/he..." |
| Emphatic contrast | `ma...mi ma` | Slot 2 + conjunction | Focused elements | `ma mia whera mi ma sha whema` "*I* learn but *s/he* works" |

### Focus and Emphasis

| Function | Particle | Position | Scope | Example |
|----------|----------|----------|-------|---------|
| Word-level focus | `ma` | Slot 2 final | Individual words | `na ma he nowhea` "*the* person" |
| Contrastive focus | `ma...mi` | Slot 2 + contrast | Contrasted elements | `ma mia mi sha` "*I* but s/he" |
| Corrective focus | `me...ma` | Negation + emphasis | Corrections | `me mia, ma sha` "Not I, *s/he*" |

## Functional Specializations

### Academic Discourse

**Topic-based organization**: Academic texts use systematic topic establishment
```
so ha ne moruphea, ro pa ma riphe ta phera.
POL TOP INAN findings, INF SPRL EMPH important PRS be
"Politely, as for the findings, they are *most* important." (I infer)
```

**Contrastive analysis**: Academic arguments use systematic contrast
```
ha ne whethea, mia ta whera mi ha ne lophui, me mia whera.
TOP INAN book, 1SG PRS learn CNTR TOP INAN art, NEG 1SG learn
"As for books, I learn, but as for art, I don't learn."
```

### Narrative Discourse

**Topic sequence management**: Stories manage topic flow across episodes
```
Story Opening:
ha ne moshethea, mia li sharo.
TOP INAN mountain, 1SG PST go
"As for the mountain, I went."

Topic Continuation:
mia li na he nowhea whelu.
1SG PST OBJ HUM person love
"I loved the person." (mountain topic continues)

Topic Shift:
ha ne rupheu, sha li me wanume phera.
TOP INAN weather, 3SG PST NEG good be
"As for the weather, it wasn't good." (new topic)
```

### Conversational Discourse

**Dynamic topic negotiation**: Conversations use flexible topic management
```
Speaker A:
ha ne whethea, mia ta whera.
TOP INAN book, 1SG PRS learn
"As for books, I learn."

Speaker B:
mi mia na ma ne lophui whelu.
CNTR 1SG OBJ EMPH INAN art love
"But I love *art*." (contrast + shift)
```

**Information checking and clarification**:
```
wa ma ne whethea mia ta whera.
Q EMPH INAN book 1SG PRS learn
"Do I learn from *books*?" (clarifying focus)
```

## Information Structure Patterns

### Given-New Information Management

**Given information**: Reduced marking, topic position
```
ha ne whethea (given topic), mia ta ma la whera (new information).
TOP INAN book, 1SG PRS EMPH PROG learn
"As for books (given), I *am* learning (new focus)."
```

**New information**: Emphasis marking, focus position
```
mia ta na ma he nowhea whera.
1SG PRS OBJ EMPH HUM person learn
"I learn from *the person*." (new/focused object)
```

### Discourse Progression Patterns

**Linear progression**: Topic maintained, predicate focus
```
ha ne whethea, mia ta whera. mia ta ma wanume whera.
TOP INAN book, 1SG PRS learn. 1SG PRS EMPH well learn
"As for books, I learn. I learn *well*." (predicate focus)
```

**Branching progression**: Topic shift, new focus
```
ha ne whethea, mia ta whera. ha ma ne lophui, sha ta misha phera.
TOP INAN book, 1SG PRS learn. TOP EMPH INAN art, 3SG PRS beautiful be
"As for books, I learn. As for *art*, it is beautiful." (topic shift + emphasis)
```

## Cross-Category Discourse Effects

### Discourse-Evidentiality Integration

**Topic-sensitive evidential choice**:
```
Established Topic (confident):
ha ne whethea, hi mia ta whera.
TOP INAN book, DIR 1SG PRS learn
"As for books, I learn." (direct - confident about established topic)

New Topic (less certain):
ha ne lophui, ro mia ta misha phera.
TOP INAN art, INF 1SG PRS beautiful be
"As for art, I am beautiful." (inference - less certain about new topic)
```

### Discourse-Temporal Integration

**Narrative temporal sequencing with topic management**:
```
ha ne pheutua, mia li wi whera.
TOP INAN morning, 1SG PST INCEP learn
"As for the morning, I started learning."

mia li la whera.
1SG PST PROG learn
"I was learning." (topic continues, temporal progression)

ha ne whethoa, mia li wu whera.
TOP INAN evening, 1SG PST CESS learn
"As for the evening, I finished learning." (topic shift, temporal progression)
```

### Discourse-Politeness Integration

**Register-sensitive topic and focus marking**:
```
Formal Academic:
so ha ne moruphea, ro pa ma riphe ta phera.
POL TOP INAN findings, INF SPRL EMPH important PRS be
"Politely, as for the findings, they are *most* important."

Casual Conversation:
ne moruphea ma riphe ta phera.
INAN findings EMPH important PRS be
"The findings are *important*." (minimal topic/politeness marking)
```

## Processing and Discourse Cognition

### Information Structure Planning

**Discourse planning requires**:
1. **Topic assessment**: What is the current discourse topic?
2. **Information status**: What is given vs. new information?
3. **Contrast evaluation**: What alternatives are being compared?
4. **Focus selection**: What information needs emphasis?
5. **Particle coordination**: How should discourse particles combine with other systems?

### Discourse Acquisition

**Learning progression**:
**Stage 1**: Master basic topic marking (`ha`) and contrast (`mi`)
**Stage 2**: Learn emphasis patterns (`ma`) and information structure
**Stage 3**: Integrate discourse particles with evidentiality and politeness
**Stage 4**: Develop strategic discourse management across extended texts
