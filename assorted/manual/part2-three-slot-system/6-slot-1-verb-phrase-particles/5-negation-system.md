# Negation System

## The Negation Particle `me`

Phi employs a single, systematic negation particle `me` that operates within Slot 1 to negate verb phrases and their associated meanings. Unlike natural languages with multiple negation strategies, phi's approach provides clear, unambiguous negation with predictable scope interactions.

## Core Function and Position

### Basic Negation Pattern
The particle `me` appears in **final position** within Slot 1, following all tense, aspect, and modal particles:

**Slot 1 Order**: Tense > Aspect > Mood > **Negation (`me`)**

```
mia li la me whera.
1SG PST PROG NEG learn
"I was not learning."

mia su po me whera.
1SG FUT HAB NEG learn
"I will not usually learn."
```

### Scope Properties
`me` has **verb phrase scope**, negating:
- The main verb action
- Associated objects and complements
- The entire predicate meaning

```
mia me na ne whethea whera.
1SG NEG OBJ INAN book learn
"I do not learn from books." (negates entire learning activity)
```

## Basic Negation Patterns

### Simple Negation
```
mia me whera.
1SG NEG learn
"I do not learn."

sha me na ne lophui whona.
3SG NEG OBJ INAN art look
"S/he does not look at art."

ne riwhea me whuru.
INAN wind NEG blow
"The wind does not blow."
```

### Negation with Explicit Tense
```
mia li me whera.        (past negation)
1SG PST NEG learn
"I did not learn."

mia ta me whera.        (present negation)
1SG PRS NEG learn
"I do not learn."

mia su me whera.        (future negation)
1SG FUT NEG learn
"I will not learn."
```

## Negation with Aspect

### Progressive Negation
```
mia la me whera.
1SG PROG NEG learn
"I am not learning."

mia li la me whera.
1SG PST PROG NEG learn
"I was not learning."
```

### Perfect Negation
```
mia ni me whera.
1SG PRF NEG learn
"I have not learned."

mia li ni me whera.
1SG PST PRF NEG learn
"I had not learned."
```

### Habitual Negation
```
mia po me whera.
1SG HAB NEG learn
"I usually do not learn."

mia li po me whera.
1SG PST HAB NEG learn
"I used to not learn."
```

### Perfective Negation
```
mia pu me whera.
1SG PFV NEG learn
"I did not learn (completely)."

mia li pu me whera.
1SG PST PFV NEG learn
"I had not learned (completely)."
```

## Negation with Modality

### Imperative Negation (Prohibition)
```
to me whera.
IMP NEG learn
"Do not learn!" / "Don't learn!"

so to me na ne whethea whera.
POL IMP NEG OBJ INAN book learn
"Please do not learn from books."
```

### Obligative Negation
Two distinct patterns with different meanings:

**Negative obligation** (you should not):
```
mia ru me whera.
1SG OBLG NEG learn
"I should not learn."
```

**Non-obligation** (you don't have to):
```
me ru mia whera.
NEG OBLG 1SG learn
"I don't have to learn."
```

### Desiderative Negation
```
mia we me whera.
1SG DES NEG learn
"I want to not learn." / "I don't want to learn."

mia me we whera.
1SG NEG DES learn
"I do not want to learn."
```

## Scope Distinctions

### Verb Phrase Negation (Standard)
`me` in Slot 1 negates the entire verb phrase:
```
mia me na ne whethea whera.
1SG NEG OBJ INAN book learn
"I do not learn from books." (negates the entire learning activity)
```

### Constituent Negation (Slot 2)
`me` can also appear in Slot 2 to negate specific constituents:
```
mia na me ne whethea whera.
1SG OBJ NEG INAN book learn
"I learn from not-books." (negates only the object)

mia me lo whethea whera.
1SG NEG PL book learn
"I learn from not-books." (negates plurality)
```

## Complex Negation Patterns

### Multiple Particles with Negation
```
hi mia li po me na ne whethea whera.
DIR 1SG PST HAB NEG OBJ INAN book learn
"I used to not learn from books." (I see this directly)

so ru mia la me whera.
POL OBLG 1SG PROG NEG learn
"I should politely not be learning."
```

### Negation in Conditional Contexts
```
tu mia me whera, mia su me riphe phera.
COND 1SG NEG learn, 1SG FUT NEG important be
"If I don't learn, I will not be important."

tu mia li me whera, mia ta ru whera.
COND 1SG PST NEG learn, 1SG PRS OBLG learn
"If I didn't learn, I should learn (now)."
```

### Contrastive Negation
```
mia me whera mi sha ta whera.
1SG NEG learn CNTR 3SG PRS learn
"I do not learn, but s/he learns."

mia li me whera mi mia ta la whera.
1SG PST NEG learn CNTR 1SG PRS PROG learn
"I did not learn, but I am learning (now)."
```

## Double Negation

### Cancellation Effect
Two negation particles cancel each other out:
```
mia me me whera.
1SG NEG NEG learn
"I don't not learn." = "I do learn."
```

### Emphatic Affirmation
Double negation often adds emphasis:
```
mia me me whemo.
1SG NEG NEG think
"I don't not think." = "I definitely think."

ro mia me me riphe phera.
INF 1SG NEG NEG important be
"I infer I'm not unimportant." = "I infer I am important."
```

## Register and Politeness

### Formal Negation
Explicit particle marking with politeness:
```
so hi mia ta me na ne whethea whera.
POL DIR 1SG PRS NEG OBJ INAN book learn
"I politely do not learn from books." (I see this directly)
```

### Informal Negation
Simplified structures:
```
mia me whera.
1SG NEG learn
"I don't learn."

me whera.        (implied subject)
NEG learn
"(Someone) doesn't learn."
```

### Polite Refusal
```
so mia me we whera.
POL 1SG NEG DES learn
"I politely do not want to learn."

so me mia na thueta whera.
POL NEG 1SG OBJ that learn
"I politely don't learn that."
```

## Cross-Linguistic Comparisons

### Universal Patterns
- **Sentence negation**: Found in all human languages
- **Scope ambiguity**: Common across languages
- **Multiple strategies**: Natural languages often have several negation methods

### Phi's Innovations
- **Single particle**: Eliminates complexity
- **Clear positioning**: Reduces scope ambiguity
- **Systematic interactions**: Predictable combinations
- **Slot-based scope**: Explicit scope distinctions

## Advanced Negation Usage

### Discourse Negation
```
wa mia me whera. ho mia me whera.
Q 1SG NEG learn. EXCL 1SG NEG learn.
"Do I not learn? How I don't learn!"
```

### Evidential Negation
```
hi mia me whera.        (direct observation)
DIR 1SG NEG learn
"I am not learning." (I see this directly)

ro mia me whera.        (inference)
INF 1SG NEG learn
"I am not learning." (I infer this)
```

### Temporal Negation Sequences
```
mia li me whera. mia ta me whera. mia su me whera.
1SG PST NEG learn. 1SG PRS NEG learn. 1SG FUT NEG learn.
"I didn't learn. I don't learn. I won't learn."
```

## Common Errors and Solutions

### Position Errors
❌ **Incorrect**: `mia whera me` (negation after verb)
✅ **Correct**: `mia me whera` (negation before verb)

### Scope Confusion
❌ **Unclear**: `mia na me ne whethea whera` (ambiguous scope)
✅ **Clear**: `mia me na ne whethea whera` (clear VP negation)

### Double Negation Misuse
❌ **Unintended**: `mia me ru me whera` (accidental cancellation)
✅ **Intended**: `mia ru me whera` (should not) OR `me ru mia whera` (don't have to)
