# Number and Quantification

## Three-Way Number System

Phi employs a sophisticated three-way number marking system that provides precise quantification options beyond simple singular/plural distinctions. This system allows speakers to encode specific quantity ranges while maintaining flexibility for general plural reference.

## The Complete Number Paradigm

| Particle | Number | Quantity Range | English Gloss | Usage Context |
|----------|--------|----------------|---------------|---------------|
| `wo` | Paucal | 2-5 items | "a few" | Small definite groups |
| `lo` | Plural | Unspecified > 1 | "some/many" | General plural reference |
| `no` | Greater Plural | 6+ items | "many/lots" | Large definite groups |

*Note: Singular (1 item) is unmarked - no particle needed.*

## Individual Number Analysis

### Unmarked Singular

**Function**: Default state requiring no particle for single items.

**Basic singular patterns**:
```
he nowhea ta whera.
HUM person PRS learn
"The person learns." (one person)

mia na ne whethea ta whera.
1SG OBJ INAN book PRS learn
"I learn from a book." (one book)

pi shiphe ta whumi.
ANIM dog PRS run
"The dog runs." (one dog)
```

### 1. `wo` - Paucal Number

**Function**: Marks small, definite groups of 2-5 items.

**Core usage principles**:
- Precise small quantities (2, 3, 4, 5)
- Countable, discrete items
- Often implies "a few" or "several"
- Emphasizes the small, manageable nature

**Basic paucal patterns**:
```
wo he nowhea ta whera.
PAUC HUM person PRS learn
"A few people learn." (2-5 people)

mia na wo ne whethea ta whera.
1SG OBJ PAUC INAN book PRS learn
"I learn from a few books." (2-5 books)

wo pi thiphe ta phawi.
PAUC ANIM bird PRS fly
"A few birds fly." (2-5 birds)
```

**Emphasis on small quantity**:
```
mia wo he philowhea ta whelu.
1SG PAUC HUM friend PRS love
"I love a few friends." (small, close group)

wo ne riwhea ta me whuru.
PAUC INAN wind PRS NEG blow
"A few winds do not blow." (specific few)
```

**With other particles**:
```
si wo he nowhea na ne lophui ta whona.
SUBJ PAUC HUM person OBJ INAN art PRS look
"A few people look at art."

mia li na wo ne whethea whera.
1SG PST OBJ PAUC INAN book learn
"I learned from a few books."
```

### 2. `lo` - General Plural

**Function**: Marks unspecified plural quantities, the default plural marker.

**Core usage principles**:
- General plural reference
- Unspecified quantity greater than one
- Most common plural marker
- Neutral about exact number

**Basic plural patterns**:
```
lo he nowhea ta whera.
PL HUM person PRS learn
"People learn." (general plural)

mia na lo ne whethea ta whera.
1SG OBJ PL INAN book PRS learn
"I learn from books." (unspecified number)

lo pi thiphe ta phawi.
PL ANIM bird PRS fly
"Birds fly." (general statement)
```

**Indefinite quantities**:
```
mia lo he philowhea ta whelu.
1SG PL HUM friend PRS love
"I love friends." (unspecified number)

lo ne riwhea ta whuru.
PL INAN wind PRS blow
"Winds blow." (general phenomenon)
```

**Most frequent plural usage**:
```
si lo he nowhea na ne lophui ta whona.
SUBJ PL HUM person OBJ INAN art PRS look
"People look at art." (standard plural)

mia li na lo ne whethea whera.
1SG PST OBJ PL INAN book learn
"I learned from books." (common pattern)
```

### 3. `no` - Greater Plural

**Function**: Marks large, definite groups of 6 or more items.

**Core usage principles**:
- Large quantities (6+)
- Emphasis on abundance or magnitude
- Countable items in significant numbers
- Often implies "many" or "lots of"

**Basic greater plural patterns**:
```
no he nowhea ta whera.
GRPL HUM person PRS learn
"Many people learn." (6+ people)

mia na no ne whethea ta whera.
1SG OBJ GRPL INAN book PRS learn
"I learn from many books." (6+ books)

no pi thiphe ta phawi.
GRPL ANIM bird PRS fly
"Many birds fly." (large flock)
```

**Emphasis on large quantity**:
```
mia no he philowhea ta whelu.
1SG GRPL HUM friend PRS love
"I love many friends." (large social circle)

no ne riwhea ta whuru.
GRPL INAN wind PRS blow
"Many winds blow." (storm conditions)
```

**Abundance and magnitude**:
```
si no he nowhea na ne lophui ta whona.
SUBJ GRPL HUM person OBJ INAN art PRS look
"Many people look at art." (crowded gallery)

mia li na no ne whethea whera.
1SG PST OBJ GRPL INAN book learn
"I learned from many books." (extensive study)
```

## Number Hierarchy and Usage

### Quantity Specificity Scale
Phi's number system creates a specificity hierarchy:

**Most specific**: `wo` (2-5) > **General**: `lo` (>1) > **Emphasized**: `no` (6+)

```
wo he nowhea ta whera.     (precise: few people)
lo he nowhea ta whera.     (general: people)  
no he nowhea ta whera.     (emphatic: many people)
```

### Context-Dependent Selection

**Known small quantities**:
```
mia na wo ne whethea shia phiato ta whera.
1SG OBJ PAUC INAN book about this PRS learn
"I learn from a few books about this." (speaker knows it's 2-5)
```

**Unknown/general quantities**:
```
mia na lo ne whethea shia phiato ta whera.
1SG OBJ PL INAN book about this PRS learn
"I learn from books about this." (unspecified number)
```

**Emphasized large quantities**:
```
mia na no ne whethea shia phiato ta whera.
1SG OBJ GRPL INAN book about this PRS learn
"I learn from many books about this." (emphasizing abundance)
```

## Number and Animacy Interactions

### Human Entities
```
wo he nowhea ta tolime whema.
PAUC HUM person PRS together work
"A few people work together." (small team)

lo he nowhea ta tolime whema.
PL HUM person PRS together work
"People work together." (general cooperation)

no he nowhea ta tolime whema.
GRPL HUM person PRS together work
"Many people work together." (large collaboration)
```

### Animate Entities
```
wo pi shiphe ta phola.
PAUC ANIM dog PRS walk
"A few dogs walk." (2-5 dogs)

lo pi shiphe ta phola.
PL ANIM dog PRS walk
"Dogs walk." (general plural)

no pi shiphe ta phola.
GRPL ANIM dog PRS walk
"Many dogs walk." (pack or large group)
```

### Inanimate Entities
```
wo ne whethea ta riphe phera.
PAUC INAN book PRS important be
"A few books are important." (specific few)

lo ne whethea ta riphe phera.
PL INAN book PRS important be
"Books are important." (books in general)

no ne whethea ta riphe phera.
GRPL INAN book PRS important be
"Many books are important." (emphasis on quantity)
```

## Number in Different Contexts

### Questions
```
wa wo he nowhea ta whera.
Q PAUC HUM person PRS learn
"Do a few people learn?"

hamite lo ne whethea ta riphe phera.
how PL INAN book PRS important be
"How are books important?"

wa no pi thiphe ta phawi.
Q GRPL ANIM bird PRS fly
"Do many birds fly?"
```

### Conditionals
```
tu wo he nowhea ta whera, sha su riphe phera.
COND PAUC HUM person PRS learn, 3PL FUT important be
"If a few people learn, they will be important."

tu no ne whethea ta riphe phera, mia su lo whera.
COND GRPL INAN book PRS important be, 1SG FUT PL learn
"If many books are important, I will learn them."
```

### Complex Combinations
```
si wo he nowhea na lo ne whethea na no pi thiphe ta whelu.
SUBJ PAUC HUM person OBJ PL INAN book OBJ GRPL ANIM bird PRS love
"A few people love books and many birds." (multiple number distinctions)
```

## Register and Style Variation

### Formal Academic
Precise number marking:
```
hi si wo he phuwhea na no ne moruphea ta thero.
DIR SUBJ PAUC HUM researcher OBJ GRPL INAN findings PRS require
"A few researchers require many findings." (I see this directly)
```

### Informal Conversation
Simplified number marking:
```
lo he nowhea whera.
PL HUM person learn
"People learn." (general statement)

mia wo whethea whera.
1SG PAUC book learn
"I learn from a few books." (casual)
```

### Narrative Discourse
Strategic number marking for effect:
```
wo he nowhea ta sharo. lo pi thiphe ta phawi. no ne whethea ta phera.
PAUC HUM person PRS go. PL ANIM bird PRS fly. GRPL INAN book PRS be.
"A few people go. Birds fly. Many books exist." (varying perspective)
```

## Cross-Linguistic Perspectives

### Number System Typology
- **Dual systems**: Some languages mark exactly two items
- **Paucal systems**: Found in Austronesian and Pacific languages
- **General plural**: Universal across human languages
- **Phi innovation**: Systematic three-way distinction

### Comparative Examples
- **Fijian**: Has paucal marking
- **Arabic**: Dual + plural system
- **English**: Simple singular/plural
- **Japanese**: Number marking optional

## Learning Guidelines

### Acquisition Sequence
1. **Start with `lo`**: General plural for all contexts
2. **Add `wo`**: Small, definite quantities
3. **Master `no`**: Large, emphasized quantities
4. **Practice selection**: Context-appropriate choice

### Common Patterns
- **Default to `lo`**: When quantity unknown
- **Use `wo`**: When you can count items (2-5)
- **Choose `no`**: When emphasizing abundance
- **Omit particles**: For singular reference
