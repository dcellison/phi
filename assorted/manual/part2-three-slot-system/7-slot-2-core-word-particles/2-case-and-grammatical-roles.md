# Case and Grammatical Roles

## Three-Way Role Marking System

Phi employs optional but precise grammatical role markers that clarify the functional relationships between words in sentences. Unlike obligatory case systems in natural languages, phi's approach prioritizes clarity when needed while allowing efficient omission in unambiguous contexts.

## The Complete Role Paradigm

| Particle | Role | English Function | Usage Context | Semantic Scope |
|----------|------|------------------|---------------|----------------|
| `si` | Subject | "who/what does" | Agent, experiencer | Sentence subject |
| `na` | Object | "who/what receives" | Patient, theme | Direct/indirect object |
| `te` | Verb | "the action/state" | Predicate marking | Main verb |

## Individual Role Analysis

### 1. `si` - Subject Marker

**Function**: Explicitly marks the subject (agent/experiencer) of the sentence.

**Core usage principles**:
- Clarifies subject in complex sentences
- Required in formal/academic contexts
- Optional in clear, simple sentences
- Resolves ambiguity in flexible word order

**Basic subject marking**:
```
si mia ta whera.
SUBJ 1SG PRS learn
"I learn." (explicit subject marking)

si he nowhea na ne whethea ta whera.
SUBJ HUM person OBJ INAN book PRS learn
"The person learns from books." (clear roles)

si ne riwhea ta whuru.
SUBJ INAN wind PRS blow
"The wind blows." (inanimate subject)
```

**Complex sentence clarity**:
```
si he nowhea na he nowhea ta whisi.
SUBJ HUM person OBJ HUM person PRS see
"The person sees the person." (disambiguates which is subject)

si mia na ne lophui ta whona mi si sha na ne whethea ta whera.
SUBJ 1SG OBJ INAN art PRS look CNTR SUBJ 3SG OBJ INAN book PRS learn
"I look at art, but s/he learns from books." (clear contrast)
```

**Formal/academic usage**:
```
hi si he phuwhea na ne monowhea ta thero.
DIR SUBJ HUM researcher OBJ INAN method PRS require
"The researcher requires the method." (I see this directly)
```

### 2. `na` - Object Marker

**Function**: Explicitly marks direct and indirect objects (patients/themes).

**Core usage principles**:
- Clarifies object relationships
- Distinguishes direct from indirect objects
- Essential with animate objects
- Supports flexible word order

**Basic object marking**:
```
mia na ne whethea ta whera.
1SG OBJ INAN book PRS learn
"I learn from books." (explicit object)

sha na he nowhea ta whisi.
3SG OBJ HUM person PRS see
"S/he sees the person." (animate object)

mia na ne lophui ta whona.
1SG OBJ INAN art PRS look
"I look at art." (inanimate object)
```

**Multiple objects**:
```
mia na he nowhea na ne whethea ta shelu.
1SG OBJ HUM person OBJ INAN book PRS give
"I give books to the person." (indirect + direct object)

si he nowhea na ne whethea na he phiphea ta whelu.
SUBJ HUM person OBJ INAN book OBJ HUM student PRS give
"The person gives books to the student."
```

**Object fronting** (with clear marking):
```
na ne whethea si mia ta whera.
OBJ INAN book SUBJ 1SG PRS learn
"Books, I learn from." (topicalized object)
```

### 3. `te` - Verb Marker

**Function**: Explicitly marks the main verb or predicate in the sentence.

**Core usage principles**:
- Clarifies predicate in complex structures
- Disambiguates verb from noun forms
- Required in formal linguistic contexts
- Optional in conversational speech

**Basic verb marking**:
```
mia te whera.
1SG VRB learn
"I learn." (explicit verb marking)

si he nowhea te na ne whethea whera.
SUBJ HUM person VRB OBJ INAN book learn
"The person learns from books." (clear predicate)
```

**Complex predicate structures**:
```
si mia ta te la whera.
SUBJ 1SG PRS VRB PROG learn
"I am learning." (progressive predicate)

mia li me te na ne lophui whona.
1SG PST NEG VRB OBJ INAN art look
"I did not look at art." (negated predicate)
```

**Formal discourse**:
```
so hi si he nowhea ta te na ne ruphea shelu.
POL DIR SUBJ HUM person PRS VRB OBJ INAN information give
"The person gives information." (polite, direct evidence, explicit structure)
```

## Case Interaction Patterns

### Subject-Object Relationships
Case markers clarify core grammatical relationships:

```
si he nowhea na he nowhea ta whelu.
SUBJ HUM person OBJ HUM person PRS love
"The person loves the person." (clear who loves whom)

si mia na ne whethea te shio phiato whera.
SUBJ 1SG OBJ INAN book VRB from this learn
"I learn about books from this." (multiple relationships)
```

### Role Reversal and Flexibility
Case marking enables flexible word order:

```
na ne whethea si mia te whera.       (OSV)
OBJ INAN book SUBJ 1SG VRB learn
"Books, I learn from."

te whera si mia na ne whethea.       (VSO)
VRB learn SUBJ 1SG OBJ INAN book
"Learn, I do, from books."
```

### Complex Sentence Coordination
Multiple clauses with clear role marking:

```
si mia te whera mi si sha te whema.
SUBJ 1SG VRB learn CNTR SUBJ 3SG VRB work
"I learn, but s/he works."

tu si mia te whera, si mia su te riphe phera.
COND SUBJ 1SG VRB learn, SUBJ 1SG FUT VRB important be
"If I learn, I will be important."
```

## Register Variation

### Formal Academic
Explicit role marking throughout:
```
so hi si he phuwhea ta te na ne monowhea na he phiphea whelu.
POL DIR SUBJ HUM researcher PRS VRB OBJ INAN method OBJ HUM student give
"The researcher gives the method to the student." (fully explicit)
```

### Informal Conversation
Minimal role marking:
```
mia whera.                     (subject understood)
1SG learn
"I learn."

na ne whethea whera.           (object marked for clarity)
OBJ INAN book learn
"Learn from books."
```

### Written Academic
Systematic role marking for precision:
```
si he lonowhea ta te na ne moruphea whipu nene na he lo phiphea whelu.
SUBJ HUM researchers PRS VRB OBJ INAN findings know and OBJ HUM PL students give
"Researchers know the findings and give them to students."
```

## Animacy and Case Interactions

### Animate Object Requirement
Animate objects typically require explicit marking:

```
sha na he nowhea ta whisi.     (animate object - marking recommended)
3SG OBJ HUM person PRS see
"S/he sees the person."

sha ne riwhea ta whisi.        (inanimate object - marking optional)
3SG INAN wind PRS see
"S/he sees the wind."
```

### Hierarchy Effects
Human > animate > inanimate affects case marking patterns:

```
si he nowhea na pi shiphe ta thelu.
SUBJ HUM person OBJ ANIM dog PRS care
"The person cares for the dog." (high animacy = explicit marking)

mia ne riwhea whisi.
1SG INAN wind see
"I see the wind." (low animacy = minimal marking)
```

## Cross-Linguistic Perspectives

### Nominative-Accusative Pattern
Phi follows nominative-accusative alignment:
- **Nominative** (`si`): subjects of transitive and intransitive verbs
- **Accusative** (`na`): objects of transitive verbs
- **No ergative split**: consistent across all verb types

### Optional Case Marking
Unlike obligatory case languages:
- **Finnish**: All nouns must have case
- **Japanese**: Particles frequently omitted in speech
- **Phi**: Strategic marking for clarity and formality

## Advanced Usage Patterns

### Multiple Case Marking
```
si mia na he nowhea na ne whethea na ne loruphea te whelu.
SUBJ 1SG OBJ HUM person OBJ INAN book OBJ INAN knowledge VRB give
"I give knowledge about books to the person." (multiple objects)
```

### Case in Questions
```
wa si he nowhea na thueta te whisi.
Q SUBJ HUM person OBJ that VRB see
"Does the person see that?"

hamite si mia na ne whethea te whera.
how SUBJ 1SG OBJ INAN book VRB learn
"How do I learn from books?"
```

### Contrastive Case Marking
```
si mia na ne whethea te whera mi me si sha.
SUBJ 1SG OBJ INAN book VRB learn CNTR NEG SUBJ 3SG
"I learn from books, but not s/he." (contrastive subjects)
```
